Key Relationship
The US Dollar & Bitcoin
The DXY Inverse

Bitcoin and the US Dollar Index (DXY) have a historically inverse relationship. When the dollar weakens, capital flows into hard assets.

A weakening dollar cycle — driven by rate cuts and fiscal deficits — is structurally bullish for Bitcoin over 12–18 month timeframes.

"Bitcoin is not anti-dollar. It's the answer to a question about what money should be."
When DXY Falls
Risk assets rally. Bitcoin historically outperforms all asset classes in weak-dollar environments.
When DXY Rises
Short-term headwinds for crypto. But BTC's long-term supply scarcity absorbs these cycles over 4+ year horizons.
Current Read (Q1 2026)
DXY at key resistance. Rate cut expectations = structural dollar weakness ahead. Watch for breakdowns.
Global Liquidity
Global M2 — The tide that lifts all boats

Global M2 is the total money supply across all major economies. It is the single most powerful macro correlate to Bitcoin's price — with a ~12-week lead.

When central banks print, expand credit, or cut rates → M2 rises → excess capital finds its way into scarce assets.

Global M2 expanding as of Q1 2026
The Liquidity Cycle
Tightening → BTC tops. Easing → BTC bottoms. The 2022 bear market was a liquidity withdrawal event. The 2024–2025 cycle reopened as M2 expanded again.
Why This Matters to You
You're not timing the news. You're reading the liquidity cycle. This is the professional investor edge — macro first, asset second.

Market Structure
The 4-Year Bitcoin Cycle
1
Halving (supply shock)

Bitcoin block reward cuts in half. Supply issuance drops. Historically triggers 12–18 month bull markets. Last halving: April 2024.

2
Accumulation phase

Smart money buys quietly. On-chain signals show supply moving to long-term holders. We are/were here in 2023–early 2024.

3
Expansion / euphoria

Price discovery phase. Retail enters. Media coverage spikes. FOMO drives final leg. 2024–2025 cycle.

4
Distribution / bear

Long-term holders sell. On-chain shows profit-taking. Liquidity contracts. 2022 was this phase.

Where Are We Now?
Post-halving 2024. Historically Q4 2024–Q2 2026 is the most profitable window of the cycle. The cycle isn't over.
The Framework Rule
Don't react to the news cycle. Read the on-chain data. The halving clock doesn't care about headlines.
On-Chain Intelligence
What we watch — and why
MVRV Z-Score
Market Value vs Realised Value. Measures how overheated or undervalued Bitcoin is relative to what people paid. Red zone = top risk. Blue zone = accumulate.
SOPR
Spent Output Profit Ratio. When spent coins are at a loss, holders are capitulating — historically the best time to buy. Measures market pain.
Exchange Flows
BTC flowing onto exchanges = selling pressure. BTC flowing off = long-term holding. When supply leaves exchanges, price tends to follow.
Long-Term Holder Supply
Coins held 155+ days. When LTH supply is accumulating, smart money is positioning. The strongest signal of where we are in the cycle.
Funding Rates
Positive = longs paying shorts (overheated). Negative = shorts paying longs (fear). Used to fade extremes and size positions accordingly.

Portfolio
& Risk
How professionals size positions and protect capital
Risk First
Risk Management — The non-negotiables
Position Sizing
Never allocate more than you can hold through a 70–80% drawdown without panic selling. If you'd sell at -50%, you're over-allocated. Size down.
The 1% Rule (Alts)
For speculative altcoin positions: max 1–2% of total portfolio per position. Most won't survive a bear market. Size accordingly.
Drawdowns Are Normal
Bitcoin has drawn down 80%+ four times and recovered to new highs each time. Your job is to stay solvent through the cycle.
The CS Framework: Conviction Tiers
Tier 1 — Bitcoin (60–80%) Core holding. Long-term. Never trade it. Buy the cycle. Tier 2 — Ethereum (10–20%) High conviction. Protocol-level bet. 3–5 year horizon. Tier 3 — Alts (5–20%) Speculative. High risk, high reward. Liquid only.
Construction
Building a portfolio that survives cycles
1
Establish your BTC base

Start with Bitcoin. Get comfortable with the volatility. Build conviction through one full cycle before diversifying heavily.

2
Dollar-cost average (DCA)

Regular buys at fixed intervals remove the emotional burden of timing. DCA on-chain signals, not price alone.

3
Hold liquidity in peaks

When on-chain signals show MVRV is extreme, reduce exposure. Cash is a position in a euphoria market.

Rebalancing Rule
If alts grow to exceed their target allocation % due to price appreciation, trim back to target. Don't let speculation dominate your portfolio accidentally.
The Accumulation Mindset
The goal is to accumulate as much BTC as possible over a 4–10 year window. Every decision should serve that goal. Not short-term gains.
Altcoins
When, why, and how — in context of BTC
When to be cautious
  • Bitcoin dominance rising (alts losing to BTC)
  • Early cycle — BTC hasn't broken ATH yet
  • Market euphoria — everyone is bullish on alts
  • No clear narrative or use case
When alts outperform
  • BTC dominance peaks and starts to fall
  • Post-halving mid-cycle phase
  • Clear sector narratives (AI, DeFi, L2s)
  • BTC has already made 3–4x from cycle bottom
The CS Altcoin Filter
Only buy altcoins that pass all 5:
  1. Real team with track record
  2. Clear product / market fit
  3. Liquid on major exchange
  4. Narrative supported by data
  5. BTC-denominated chart is trending up
"Most altcoins return to zero. A handful return 100x. The filter separates them."
