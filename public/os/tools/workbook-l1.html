Step 01 of 08

Define Your Goals

Before investing anything, get clear on what you want to achieve and why digital assets fit into your financial life.

Question 1 of 5
Why are you interested in digital assets?
Understanding why you're here is the most important starting point. Investors come to digital assets for many different reasons — some for long-term wealth building, some for portfolio diversification, some drawn by the technology itself. There's no single correct reason. What matters is that your strategy is built around your goals, not someone else's.

Step 06 of 08

Plan Your Tax Approach

Tax isn't optional — but staying organised from day one makes it manageable.

⚠️

Tax Disclaimer

This section contains general educational information only. It is not tax advice. Tax laws vary significantly by jurisdiction and change frequently. Always consult a qualified tax professional for advice specific to your situation.

Question 1 of 3
Which country are you in? (we'll show general tax context)
🇦🇺 Australia 🇳🇿 New Zealand 🇺🇸 USA 🇨🇦 Canada 🌍 Other
🇦🇺 Australia — General Context (not tax advice)
In Australia, digital assets are generally treated as capital gains tax (CGT) assets. A CGT event occurs when you sell, swap, or spend digital assets. Holding for more than 12 months may make you eligible for a 50% CGT discount — but this depends on your individual circumstances. The ATO requires detailed record-keeping of all transactions. Consult a registered tax agent for your specific situation.
🇳🇿 New Zealand — General Context (not tax advice)
New Zealand generally does not have a capital gains tax, and digital assets may not be taxed if held as investments. However, if you are deemed to be "trading" — buying with the intention to sell — income tax may apply. IRD guidance on digital assets has evolved over time. Always consult a qualified NZ tax professional.
🇺🇸 USA — General Context (not tax advice)
In the US, digital assets are treated as property by the IRS. Short-term gains (held less than 12 months) are taxed at ordinary income rates. Long-term gains (held more than 12 months) are taxed at capital gains rates (0%, 15%, or 20% depending on income). Every sell, swap, and crypto payment is a taxable event. Consult a qualified US tax professional or CPA.
🇨🇦 Canada — General Context (not tax advice)
In Canada, 50% of capital gains from digital assets are generally included in taxable income. If you're considered to be in the business of trading, 100% may be taxable as business income. The CRA requires records of all transactions. Always consult a qualified Canadian tax professional.
🌍 Other Jurisdictions — General Context (not tax advice)
Tax treatment of digital assets varies widely by country and is evolving rapidly in most jurisdictions. Some countries have capital gains taxes, some treat digital assets as foreign currency, and some have specific digital asset tax frameworks. Always consult a qualified tax professional in your country.

Setting price targets before you buy removes the emotional decision-making at the peak. Some investors use a staged ladder approach (e.g. selling tranches at different price levels). Others use fixed rules like "sell 25% at each doubling." Neither approach is universally correct — the key is that the decision is made with a clear head, not in the heat of the moment.
