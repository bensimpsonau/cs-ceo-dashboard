TOPIC 1

Tokenomics Deep Dive — Why Some Tokens Are Worth Something

The most technical L2 topic. Members learn to read token economics the way a financial analyst reads a company's revenue model. This is the difference between "I like the narrative" and "I understand the value accrual mechanism."

Sub-Topics to Cover:

Fee Generation Models: How Protocols Make Money

Transaction fees (DEXs like Uniswap), borrowing interest (Aave, Compound), liquidation fees (perpetuals/Hyperliquid), MEV extraction. Which fee models are sustainable vs which are subsidised by token emissions. Revenue-to-FDV ratio as a valuation metric. Token Terminal as a data source.

Token Buyback Mechanisms and Value Accrual

How buybacks work: protocol uses fee revenue to buy its own token off the market (reduces supply). Examples: HYPE (Hyperliquid) uses trading fees for buybacks. Burn mechanisms: reducing total supply permanently. Staking rewards: distributing fees to token stakers. Compare: buyback + burn vs fee distribution vs holding with no value accrual.

Utility vs Governance vs Security-Like Tokens

Utility tokens: required to use the protocol (gas fees, access). Governance tokens: voting rights but often no direct value accrual (the "governance premium" problem). Security-like tokens: tokens that generate yield from real protocol revenue (regulatory grey area). How to classify a token and what it means for investment thesis.

Vesting Schedules and Supply Dynamics

VC unlock schedules: when early investors can sell. Team vesting: typical 1-year cliff, 4-year vest. How to find and read token unlock schedules (Token Unlocks, CoinGecko). Historical price impact of large unlocks. Why high-inflation tokens are almost always bad investments (CS 98% filter criterion).

Inflation/Deflation Mechanics Across Protocols

Token emission schedules: how new tokens enter circulation. Net inflation = emissions minus burns/buybacks. Why a "high APY" staking reward is meaningless if offset by equal inflation. Real yield vs nominal yield — the critical distinction for L2 members.

HYPE/Hyperliquid as the Gold Standard Case Study

Real product (perpetual DEX with real volume), real revenue (trading fees), real users (measurable on-chain). Token buyback mechanism with protocol revenue. No VC investors = no unlock pressure. Community-owned distribution. CS portfolio position: HYPE @ $23.90. Walk through the full analysis framework using HYPE as the example.

TOPIC 2

Early-Stage Project Discovery — Finding Projects Before CEX Listing

For L2 members who want exposure to early-stage opportunities. High risk, high reward — but with a framework, not YOLO.

Finding and Evaluating Projects on Decentralised Exchanges

How DEXs work as the first listing venue for new tokens. Using DEX screeners (DexScreener, GeckoTerminal) to find new projects. Red flags: low liquidity, concentrated ownership, anonymous teams, copy-paste contracts. Green flags: real users, growing TVL, active community, identifiable team.

Risk Assessment Framework for Early-Stage

Position sizing: never more than 1-2% of portfolio in pre-CEX projects. Due diligence checklist: audit status, team doxxing, contract verification, liquidity lock. Using on-chain data: wallet concentration, transaction patterns, holder distribution. The rule: "If you can't afford to lose 100% of this position, don't take it."

TOPIC 3

Real-World Asset (RWA) Tokenisation — The Emerging Frontier

The bridge between traditional finance and digital assets. Tokenised gold, real estate, securities — what's real, what's hype, and how it fits in a portfolio.

What RWA Tokenisation Means

Putting real-world assets (gold, silver, real estate, securities, bonds) on blockchain. Fractional ownership, 24/7 trading, global access. How tokenised US Treasuries became a $1B+ market. The difference between a tokenised asset and a traditional ETF.

Current RWA Protocols and Platforms

Ondo Finance (tokenised treasuries), Maple Finance (institutional credit — CS holds syrupUSDC @ 33.4%), Centrifuge (real-world credit), Paxos Gold (tokenised gold). How to evaluate RWA protocols: regulatory compliance, audit trail, real backing vs synthetic exposure.

Risks and Regulatory Considerations

Regulatory uncertainty: are tokenised securities securities? Custodial risk: who actually holds the underlying asset? Redemption risk: can you actually convert your token back to the real asset? Jurisdiction-specific considerations for Australian members.

How RWAs Fit in a Digital Asset Portfolio

RWAs as a yield source (tokenised treasuries earning ~5%). As a stablecoin alternative with yield. As diversification within a digital asset portfolio. Position sizing: part of the stablecoin/yield allocation, not the growth allocation.
