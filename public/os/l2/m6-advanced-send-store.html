TOPIC 1

Advanced Cold Storage — Multi-Signature Security

For portfolios above $100K, a single hardware wallet with a single seed phrase is no longer sufficient. Multi-sig adds redundancy and eliminates single points of failure.

Multi-Sig Wallets: What They Are and When You Need One

Multi-sig = requires multiple keys (e.g., 2-of-3, 3-of-5) to authorise a transaction. Even if one key is compromised, funds are safe. Think of it like a bank vault that needs two keys to open. When to implement: generally $100K+ portfolios, or when estate planning requires access by multiple parties.

Setting Up Multi-Sig for High-Value Portfolios

Bitcoin multi-sig: Unchained Capital, Casa, Sparrow Wallet (DIY). Ethereum multi-sig: Safe (formerly Gnosis Safe). Setup walkthrough: choosing key holders, distributing devices, testing recovery. The 2-of-3 setup: you hold 2 keys (different locations), trusted third party holds 1 key. Testing: always test with a small amount first.

Institutional-Grade Cold Storage Practices

How institutions store digital assets: multiple hardware security modules (HSMs), air-gapped signing, geographic distribution. Simplified for individual high-net-worth investors: multiple hardware wallets, different manufacturers (Ledger + Trezor), different locations. Custodial vs self-custody: when to consider a regulated custodian (generally $1M+).

Passphrase Security (25th Word / Hidden Wallet)

The "25th word": an additional passphrase added to your 24-word seed. Creates a completely separate hidden wallet. Use case: plausible deniability — if someone forces you to open your wallet, the visible wallet shows a small amount while the hidden wallet holds the bulk. Setup and testing walkthrough. Warning: if you forget the passphrase, funds are permanently lost.

"The level of paranoia should be equal to the number of zeros you're trying to protect."

TOPIC 2

Advanced Security & Estate Planning

The question nobody wants to think about: "What happens to my digital assets if something happens to me?" For L2 members with significant portfolios, this isn't optional — it's essential.

Geographical Distribution of Keys

Why: protects against natural disaster, theft, and house fire. How: distribute keys/seed phrases across multiple physical locations (home, safe deposit box, trusted family member, lawyer's vault). The rule: no single location holds enough information to access funds AND no single location's loss makes funds inaccessible.

Secure Inheritance Planning

The problem: if you hold your own keys and nobody else knows how to access them, your digital assets die with you. Solutions: documented step-by-step recovery instructions stored securely. Letter of intent with lawyer referencing digital asset holdings. Trusted family member with partial access (multi-sig key). Professional services: Casa inheritance protocol, Unchained inheritance. What NOT to do: never store seed phrases in a will (public document upon probate).

Custodians vs Full Self-Sovereignty

When custodians make sense: SMSF holdings (compliance requires a regulated custodian), very large portfolios where family can't manage self-custody. When self-sovereignty is better: most individual investors who are willing to learn. The trade-off: convenience and estate simplicity vs counterparty risk. Regulated custodians in Australia: Independent Reserve Custody, Coinbase Custody.

Advanced Seed Phrase Backup Methods

Metal plates: stainless steel backup that survives fire, flood, corrosion. Shamir's Secret Sharing (SSS): split a seed phrase into multiple shares (e.g., 3-of-5), where any 3 shares can reconstruct the full phrase. Supported by Trezor Model T. Vault services: third-party secure storage for backup materials. Testing recovery: periodically test that your backup actually works.
