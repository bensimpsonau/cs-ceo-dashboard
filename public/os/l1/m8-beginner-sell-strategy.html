STEP 08 B2 — PRACTICAL HOW-TOS WEEK 5

Sell Strategy

The most emotionally challenging module — and the most important. Teaches members when and how to sell, reframes loss-cutting as a skill, and builds a systematic exit strategy. Directly addresses the ICP’s #1 fear: no exit plan.

Delivery & Timing

📅 Week 5 ⏱ ~45 mins (1-2 sessions) 🎤 L1 Weekly Coaching Call 📄 Workbook template + case studies

Live delivery: L1 Weekly Coaching Call (1-2 sessions, ~45 mins each). Workbook template + case studies.

Self-paced: Fill-in sell strategy workbook. Refer to real sell plan examples to de-risk emotional decision-making.

PSYCHOLOGY PROBLEM ROUND-TRIPPER

Why Most People Never Sell — The Psychology Problem

Unpacking the biggest emotional block — and the round-tripper risk.

  • Emotional attachment to positions ("it’s not a loss until you sell")
  • Hope bias: "it’ll come back" mentality and why it’s dangerous
  • The round-tripper problem: made money in 2021, lost it all by 2022
  • Why having a sell strategy is the professional investor’s edge over retail

Key Questions Answered

  • “Should I sell?”
  • “What if it goes back up?”
  • “When is the right time?”
👥 Member Evidence

The dominant member profile — the Round-Tripper. Greg went from $180K to $80K and didn’t cut. This is the module that prevents repeat pain.

SELL TYPES

The 3 Types of Sell Strategy

Target-based selling, DCA-out, trailing stops — objective rules for taking profits and cutting losses.

  • Target-based selling: pre-set price triggers (e.g., "If Bitcoin hits $150K, I sell 25%.")
  • DCA-out: systematic selling, removes timing decisions
  • Trailing stops: technical/percentage rules, e.g., exit if an asset drops 25% from peak

“A strategy in your head is not a strategy. It’s a vague intention.”

— Teaching Quote

Key Questions Answered

  • “What’s the best way to take profits?”
  • “Should I sell all at once or in stages?”
CASE STUDY

The Polygon Story — Ben’s Personal Loss-Cutting Lesson

Why loss-cutting is a skill — Simon’s Polygon story: cutting before 70-80% loss; opportunity cost and the sunk cost fallacy.

  • Opportunity cost framework: what is your capital doing while you hold a loser?
  • How to separate emotion from rational decision-making in the red

“My best investment decision wasn’t Solana at 10x. It was getting out of Polygon before I lost 70-80%.”

— Teaching Framework #3: The Best Trade Was a Loss

Key Questions Answered

  • “Should I sell my altcoin at a loss?”
  • “Will it come back?”
👥 Member Evidence

Sunit asked "if this was your portfolio, what would you do?" — this story answers it. Gives permission to take the loss.

SELL PLAN

Building Your Personal Sell Plan

How to write your pre-committed exit plan — making decisions with a clear head, not in the heat of the moment.

  • Decide exit targets BEFORE buying
  • Write it down — commitment device
  • Set alerts and reminders for prices
  • Different rules for Bitcoin (hold asset) vs altcoins (explicit exit rules)

Key Questions Answered

  • “How do I actually write a sell plan?”
  • “What should my targets be?”
RE-ENTRY FRAMEWORK

Re-Entry Framework

How to get back in after selling — remove timing anxiety and avoid emotional re-entry mistakes.

  • DCA back in after major correction
  • Use market cycle positioning: where are we?
  • Signals that risk-to-reward is skewed back in your favour
  • Patience: wait for value, not just a price drop

Key Questions Answered

  • “I sold — when do I buy back?”
  • “How do I know when to re-enter?”
TAX IMPLICATIONS

Tax Implications of Selling — Brief Overview

Every sell event is a CGT event; 12-month rule for discount; factor tax into your sell plan.

  • Every sell event is a CGT event (in Australia, most jurisdictions)
  • The 12-month rule: 50% discount for long-term holders
  • Why your accountant needs to know every sell — link to Tax module

Key Questions Answered

  • “Do I pay tax when I sell?”
  • “How do I factor tax in?”
SELL MISTAKES

Common Selling Mistakes — What to Avoid

The round-tripper, panic selling, selling winners, holding losers — the most common pain points.

  • Selling too early in a bull run
  • Not selling at all — the round-tripper
  • Panic selling during corrections
  • Selling winners and holding losers
👥 Member Evidence

Multiple members have made these mistakes. This module is designed to prevent them — and end the round-trip pain cycle.

✅ Member Outcomes

After completing this module, members can:

✓Understand why most people never sell and how to override the psychology
✓Know the 3 types of sell strategy and which fits their situation
✓Have a written, pre-committed sell plan with specific targets and triggers
✓Understand how to re-enter after selling (not just "wait and see")
✓Can distinguish between Bitcoin sell rules and altcoin sell rules
✓Are aware of tax implications and have briefed their accountant

🧠 Teaching Frameworks Used in This Module

#3 — "The Best Trade Was a Loss"

"My best investment decision wasn’t Solana at 10x. It was getting out of Polygon before I lost 70-80%."

Use in: Sell strategy, portfolio review calls, exit coaching.

Why it works: Reframes loss-cutting as a skill, not a failure. Empowers action when stuck in a losing position.

📋 Instructor Delivery Notes

Session format: L1 Weekly Coaching Call (1-2 sessions, ~45 mins each). Most emotionally challenging, most important. Directly tackles member pain.

Audience: Round-trippers, new investors, those with no sell plan. Make it safe to admit mistakes and to learn from pain.

Tone: Honest, empathetic, tough but caring. This is the "painkiller" module — get real about the consequences of not selling.

Key pivot point: The Polygon story and member calls are the permission slip to cut losses.

Hands-on: Force every member to write down their sell plan and share with the group/accountability partner.

CEX only rule: 🚫 No yield, no staking, no DeFi, no borrowing content in Level 1.

Language: Use "digital assets" not "crypto." Bitcoin stays as "Bitcoin." Community is on Circle.
