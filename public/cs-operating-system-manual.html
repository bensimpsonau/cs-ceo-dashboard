Section 01

Introduction

The Shift Operating System transforms the overwhelming, noisy world of crypto into a structured, repeatable process — giving every member a clear path from first principles to a complete, written investment strategy.

What Is the Shift OS?

The Shift OS is not a signals group. It's not a tips service. It's the infrastructure that Ben Simpson wished existed when he started investing nine years ago — a system that replaces emotional decision-making with evidence-based frameworks.

"Education, not advice." Every module, call, and resource is designed to build independent thinking. The goal is members who can evaluate any opportunity themselves — not members who wait for someone to tell them what to buy.

The Three Investor Types

Every member falls primarily into one of three categories. The Shift OS tailors frameworks, worked examples, and allocation guidance to each:

💰 Wealth Generation

Growing capital significantly. Higher risk tolerance. Longer time horizon. Larger altcoin allocation as satellite positions around a Bitcoin core.

🛡️ Wealth Protection

Protecting existing wealth. Bitcoin-dominant (70–80% of crypto portfolio). Lower amounts per DCA. Focus on long-term thesis over price action.

📈 Income Creation

Generating yield on holdings. Stablecoin and DeFi strategies. Systematic withdrawal schedules. Yield strategies covered in Advanced track.

Bitcoin

Why Bitcoin is fundamentally different from every other cryptocurrency — and how to build conviction before the fear hits.

Learning Objectives

  • Understand Bitcoin's core value proposition: fixed supply, decentralised, no counterparty
  • Distinguish Bitcoin from altcoins as two separate asset classes
  • Build a personal "I will buy at X" conviction framework
  • Understand the emotional cycle and why best buying opportunities feel terrible
  • Define a BTC allocation target and DCA approach

What's Covered

Two separate asset classes — BTC as digital gold/monetary network vs altcoins as venture bets. Fixed supply of 21 million. Network effect and the Lindy effect. Institutional adoption (ETFs, sovereign wealth funds, corporate treasuries). DCA as the default strategy. The 200-week MA as accumulation floor. The "I will buy at X" framework. The emotional cycle of investing. The confidence scale (rate conviction 1–10).

Key Deliverable: Written BTC allocation target (%), buy prices, DCA plan, conviction level (1–10), and what would change your mind.

"Bitcoin is the same asset today as it was at $120K — nothing changed except what someone's willing to pay."

Altcoins

Understanding altcoins as a higher-risk asset class — and how to filter the 98% you should never invest in.

Learning Objectives

  • Understand why altcoins are venture bets, not monetary assets
  • Learn how narrative cycles work and why most fail
  • Apply the CS filter framework to evaluate any altcoin
  • Understand tokenomics and the supply trap
  • Define maximum altcoin allocation and filter criteria

What's Covered

The altcoin landscape — 5–10 winners per cycle, thousands of losers. Historical top-20 analysis (2017, 2021 — most down 90%+ or dead). Narrative cycles (2017: ICOs, 2021: NFTs/memecoins, 2024–25: AI/RWA). Tokenomics: FDV vs market cap, VC unlock schedules, inflation as headwind. Daily active users as the litmus test (Solana 2M+ vs Cardano). The CS filter. Position sizing: no single alt >5–10% of crypto portfolio, total alt exposure <30–40%.

Key Deliverable: Per-altcoin assessment — which you hold, why, filter score, max position size, exit criteria.

Country-by-Country Tax Rules

CountryKey Rules
🇦🇺 AustraliaCGT event on disposal. 50% discount if held 12+ months. SMSF option. ATO treats crypto as property.
🇳🇿 New ZealandIntent-based — if bought to sell, it's income. No formal CGT. IRD guidance increasingly strict.

🇨🇦 Canada50% capital gains inclusion rate. TFSA not eligible for crypto held directly.

Multi-entity structures, DeFi tax complexity, CGT optimisation, and engaging advisors effectively.

Advanced Structures

StructureKey Details
SMSF (AU)Contributions taxed at 15%. CGT at 10% with 12-month discount. Sole purpose test applies. Most powerful AU structure for crypto.
Discretionary TrustIncome splitting, asset protection, CGT discount preservation. Complex setup — needs specialist.

CGT Optimisation: Maximise 12-month holding. Loss harvesting in bear markets. Wash sale rules (US: 30+ days). Plan before tax time.

Borrowing Against Crypto

How crypto-backed lending works — mechanics, platforms, liquidation risk, and who it's appropriate for.

Platform Comparison

CeFi

Ledn

Proof-of-reserves, no rehypothecation. Min ~$1K. Best for centralised convenience with transparency.

CeFi

Unchained

2-of-3 multisig (you hold 2 keys). Min ~$10K–$150K. Best for large positions prioritising security.

DeFi

Coinbase / Morpho

Smart contract governed, lower rates. Best for DeFi-comfortable members.

DeFi

Aave

No KYC, algorithmic rates. Best for advanced DeFi users.

The Conservative Buffer Framework: Borrow at 30–35% LTV maximum, not the 50% ceiling. A 40% BTC drawdown should not threaten collateral.

Who This Is For: Clear use for funds. Realistic repayment plan. Confidence to hold through volatility. Position large enough that losing collateral isn't catastrophic. Not for: funding more speculation, small positions, or financial pressure situations.

"If you don't understand the liquidation mechanics, you shouldn't be borrowing."
"Over-borrowing is the most common mistake. Borrow at 30–35% LTV and treat the buffer as insurance."
