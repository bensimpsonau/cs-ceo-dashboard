01 / Strategic Role

The Newsletter Is a Re-engagement Engine

The newsletter has one strategic job: re-engage leads who have touched the funnel but have not converted. This includes people who commented a keyword, used a lead magnet tool, watched the VSL, or visited the booking page but did not book. The newsletter keeps CS in their inbox every week until they are ready. It is not a content dump. It is a carefully designed re-engagement layer that moves people back toward the VSL and then to a call.

The Safety Net Rule: Every lead who enters the CS ecosystem but does not convert should end up on the newsletter list. Every ManyChat interaction that captures an email, every VSL page visitor (via pixel + email match), every event attendee, every content upgrade download. The newsletter is the last touchpoint standing between a lead and being lost forever. If someone has shown any intent at all, they get the Weekly Shift until they buy or unsubscribe.
02 / 7-Section Structure

House of Brands Newsletter Architecture

Every issue follows the same 7-section structure. Consistent structure builds reading habits. Readers learn where to find what they care about. The structure is designed so that every section independently adds value, but sections 5 and 6 are the conversion layers.

The 7 Sections

Reading time target: 4-5 minutes total. Readers should be able to scan the entire issue in 2 minutes or read it fully in 5. Respect their time. Every word earns its place. If it does not add value or move them closer to a conversion, cut it.
03 / Section Deep Dives

Example Copy for Each Section

These are ready-to-use templates showing the exact voice, tone, and structure for each section. Adapt the specifics each week but keep the format consistent.

1
Ben's Market Take — Example Copy
BTC at $87K. Here is what actually matters this week.

Everyone is focused on price. I am focused on structure.

Three things happened this week that matter more than the chart:

1. The SEC approved the first spot Ethereum ETF options. This is not about price today. It is about institutional infrastructure being built in real-time. The pipes are being laid for the next wave of capital.

2. Australian SMSF digital asset holdings hit $4.2B. Up 180% from 2024. The money is moving. Slowly, quietly, but it is moving. If you are reading this and you still do not have a digital asset allocation, you are now in the minority of serious investors.

3. The RBA held rates. Cash is still losing purchasing power. The question is not "should I buy Bitcoin?" The question is "can I afford not to have any exposure at all?"

None of this is financial advice. All of this is context. The investors who do well in digital assets are the ones who understand the structural shifts, not the ones watching the 1-hour chart.

Voice rules: Direct. Opinionated but grounded. No price predictions. No hype. No "to the moon." Frame everything as structural shifts and infrastructure. The reader should feel smarter after reading this section. Use "digital assets" not "crypto."

2
Member Win / Case Study — Example Copy
From "I have $2M in property and no digital asset strategy" to a fully structured portfolio in 90 days.

James (name changed) joined Platinum in January. Successful business owner. $2.3M net worth. Property heavy. Zero digital asset exposure.

His biggest fear: "I know I should have digital assets but I do not want to make a $200K mistake."

In 90 days inside Platinum, James:
- Built a 10% digital asset allocation across BTC, ETH, and two infrastructure plays
- Set up a separate trust structure for his holdings (saving an estimated $47K in tax over the first cycle)
- Implemented a systematic DCA strategy with clear rebalancing triggers

His words: "I went from paralysed to structured. The framework removed the guesswork. I know exactly what I own, why I own it, and when to act."

That is what Platinum does. Not tips. Not picks. Infrastructure.

Voice rules: Specific numbers. Specific outcomes. Specific timeframes. The reader must see themselves in the story. Choose members who match the ICP (business owners, professionals, $500K-$5M+). Avoid vague outcomes like "changed my life." Use concrete: "$47K tax saving" or "10% allocation."

3
Educational Piece — Example Copy
The 3-Bucket Framework: How Our Members Structure Digital Asset Portfolios

Most investors treat digital assets as one thing. They are not. Inside Platinum, we teach a 3-bucket framework:

Bucket 1: Store of Value (50-60% of allocation)
BTC. That is it. This is your digital gold. Long-term hold. Never trade it. It sits in cold storage and does its job: preserve purchasing power over decades.

Bucket 2: Infrastructure (25-35% of allocation)
ETH and select layer-1/layer-2 protocols. These are the platforms that everything else is built on. Think of them as digital real estate. You want exposure to the infrastructure layer, not the applications built on top.

Bucket 3: Selective Growth (10-15% of allocation)
This is where the research matters. Sector bets with conviction. DeFi protocols, real-world asset tokenisation, AI infrastructure tokens. Only assets that have been through the Platinum research framework. This bucket has the highest potential upside and the highest risk. It requires active management.

The framework is simple. The implementation is not. That is the gap between reading about digital assets and actually building a portfolio that performs.

Voice rules: Teach something genuinely useful. Give the framework. But the implementation is where Platinum lives. This creates a natural knowledge gap without being manipulative. The reader thinks: "I understand the concept. But I need help applying it to my $2M portfolio." Pull content from real Platinum member questions and Ben's actual advice.

4
What is Happening This Week — Example Copy
5 things that matter this week

1. BlackRock's IBIT crossed $80B AUM. The largest digital asset ETF in history. Institutional money is not coming. It is here.

2. Australia's Treasury released new digital asset tax guidance. CGT discount rules clarified for long-term holders. Good news for anyone with a 12-month+ holding period.

3. Ethereum's Pectra upgrade went live. Account abstraction and blob scaling. Technical, but it matters because it makes ETH cheaper and more useful as infrastructure.

4. JP Morgan launched tokenised bond settlement on-chain. When the biggest bank in the world is building on blockchain, the debate about whether digital assets are legitimate is over.

5. BTC mining difficulty hit a new all-time high. More hash power = more security = more institutional confidence. The network has never been stronger.

Voice rules: Curated, not comprehensive. 3-5 items max. Ben's opinion on each (one sentence). The reader should feel like they just got the "what matters" version of the week's news without having to follow 20 Twitter accounts. Avoid breathless hype. Stick to structural, institutional, regulatory moves.

06 / Segmentation

Dynamic Content by Subscriber Segment

The 7-section structure stays the same for everyone. But Sections 5 and 6 can be personalised based on the subscriber's funnel stage. Customer.io conditional blocks handle this.

Segment-Specific Content
SegmentSection 5 (Lead Magnet)Section 6 (VSL CTA)
New subscriber (no LM) Show the week's rotating lead magnet keyword with full hook copy. "If you have been reading for a few weeks and you are ready to go deeper, watch this 15-minute video." Standard VSL CTA.
Used 1 lead magnet Show a DIFFERENT lead magnet from the one they already used. "You used the SCORECARD. Now try the CALCULATOR to see your recommended allocation." Same VSL CTA with personalised bridge: "You already got your [score/strategy/allocation]. Ready to see the full framework?"
Used 2+ lead magnets Show a lead magnet they have not used yet. If they have used all 5, show the STRATEGY tool again (highest engagement, always has new output). Stronger VSL CTA: "You have used [X] of our tools. You clearly take this seriously. Here is the next step."
Visited VSL, no book Still show lead magnet (adds another touchpoint). But make it contextual: "Before you book a call, try this." Reframe: "Still thinking about it? Here is what one of our members said after their first portfolio review call." [Testimonial + VSL link]
Lapsed Platinum Skip the lead magnet. They already know the tools. "Miss being in the room? Here is what the community has been working on this week." [Link to a Platinum preview or renewal page]
Active Platinum Skip the lead magnet. Replace with: "Share this week's SCORECARD with someone you think would benefit. Here is a link you can forward." Skip the VSL CTA. Replace with: "Have feedback on Platinum? Reply to this email. Ben reads every reply."
Implementation note: Start with NO segmentation for the launch. Send the same 7-section issue to everyone for the first 4 weeks. Measure baseline metrics. Then layer in segmentation one segment at a time starting Week 5. Trying to personalise everything from day one is how newsletters never launch. Ship the basics. Optimise later.

08 / Voice, Tone, and Compliance

Sounds Like Ben, Every Time

Do
  • Write like Ben talks. Direct. Clear. No fluff.
  • Use "digital assets" not "crypto"
  • Use "systematic", "framework", "infrastructure", "conviction"
  • Reference real numbers, real members, real outcomes
  • Speak to the investor, not the gambler
  • Frame digital assets as serious wealth infrastructure
  • Keep sentences short. One idea per sentence.
  • Use first person ("I" and "we")
  • Include financial disclaimer in every issue
  • Respect the reader's intelligence and time
Never
  • Price predictions ("BTC will hit $200K")
  • Return promises ("10x your money")
  • Hype language ("moon", "ape in", "100x", "guaranteed")
  • Em dashes (use periods or commas instead)
  • Value stacking ("$50,000 worth of bonuses")
  • Countdown timers or fake urgency
  • Generic greetings ("Hope this finds you well")
  • Clickbait subject lines
  • More than 2 CTAs per issue (1 LM + 1 VSL)
  • Content pulled from keyword research tools
Subject Line Framework

Subject lines follow the same rules as all CS copy: direct, specific, no clickbait. The reader should know what they are getting before they open.

FormatExample
Market context + insight"BTC at $87K. 3 things that matter more than the chart."
Member outcome"From $0 digital assets to a structured portfolio in 90 days."
Framework reveal"The 3-bucket framework we teach inside Platinum."
Question"How protected is your portfolio? (2-min test)"
News-driven"BlackRock hit $80B in BTC. Here is what it means for you."

A/B test subject lines on every issue. Customer.io supports this natively. Test 2 variants on 20% of the list, send the winner to the remaining 80%.

10 / Keyword Rotation

One keyword per week in Section 5. Rotate through all 5. Match the keyword to the educational piece when possible. This keeps the lead magnet engine fed from the email list and introduces each tool to subscribers who may not have seen the IG posts.

If SCORECARD consistently gets 3x the clicks of BLUEPRINT, weight SCORECARD more heavily in the rotation. After 8 weeks of data, adjust the rotation to reflect actual performance.
