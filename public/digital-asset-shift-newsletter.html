Market Pulse

Bitcoin ETFs Cross $150 Billion in Assets Under Management

The combined AUM of spot Bitcoin ETFs has surpassed $150 billion, a milestone reached just 14 months after launch. BlackRock's IBIT leads at $62 billion, with weekly net inflows holding steady at $1.2 billion. This is no longer an experiment. Institutional allocators are treating Bitcoin ETFs as a core portfolio building block.

Bloomberg, CoinShares

Australia Advances Digital Asset Licensing Framework

The Australian Treasury has opened formal consultation on a national digital asset licensing regime, with a target implementation date of Q3 2026. The proposed framework covers exchanges, custody providers, and stablecoin issuers. For Australian investors, this means clearer protections and a more mature market structure.

Australian Treasury, AFR

Morgan Stanley Expands Direct Bitcoin Custody for Wealth Clients

Morgan Stanley, managing $5.5 trillion in client assets, is rolling out direct Bitcoin custody to its wealth management division. Eligible clients can now hold Bitcoin alongside traditional assets within Morgan Stanley accounts. This is the kind of infrastructure shift that removes friction for serious allocators.

Financial Times, CNBC

Strategy Framework

Every quarter, step back and ask yourself these five questions. They are designed to surface blind spots before the market does it for you.

The 5-Question Portfolio Check

  1. What percentage of my total net worth is in digital assets? Is it aligned with my risk tolerance?
  2. Am I holding assets I no longer have conviction in? Would I buy them again today at this price?
  3. Do I have a clear exit strategy for each position, or am I hoping and guessing?
  4. Is my custody setup secure and documented? Could my family access these assets if needed?
  5. Have I taken any profits in the last 90 days, or am I 100% exposed to downside?

If any answer made you uncomfortable, that is your starting point. Protection before growth.
