### OS COMPONENT 9: TAX, STRUCTURE & PARTNERS

## SLIDE 53 — Tax Reality: Three Countries, One Rule
**Headline:** "Every Disposal Is a Taxable Event. No Exceptions."

**The universal truth:** All three countries — Australia, New Zealand, and the US — treat crypto as **property, not currency.** Every sale, trade, swap, DeFi interaction, and spend is likely a taxable event.

| Country | How Crypto Gains Are Taxed | Long-Term Discount? | Key Nuance |
|---------|---------------------------|--------------------|-|
| 🇦🇺 **Australia** | Capital gains added to your taxable income | **Yes — 50% CGT discount** for assets held 12+ months. Only half the gain counts. | ATO has significantly increased crypto focus. Every crypto-to-crypto swap triggers CGT. |
| 🇳🇿 **New Zealand** | Profits taxed as **ordinary income** at marginal rate | **No discount.** No formal CGT exists. | IRD taxes based on **intent** — if you bought hoping it goes up, they treat profits as taxable. Most crypto buyers fall into this. Grey areas remain around DeFi. |
| 🇺🇸 **United States** | Capital gains rules apply (like stocks) | **Yes — long-term capital gains rates** (lower than income tax) for 12+ month hold. Short-term = ordinary income rate. | US citizens taxed on **worldwide income** even living abroad. American expats in AU/NZ have dual reporting obligations. |

**Common taxable events across all three:**
- Selling crypto for fiat (AUD/NZD/USD)
- Trading one crypto for another (BTC → ETH = taxable)
- Using crypto to buy goods or services
- Receiving staking rewards, yield, airdrops, or mining income

- **"Your time horizon affects your tax bill as much as your price target."** In AU and US, holding 12+ months can cut your tax significantly.
- **"Tax is not something to figure out at the last minute. With decent records and the right accountant, it's manageable."**

## SLIDE 60 — Building Your Sell Strategy: Three Trigger Types
**Headline:** "Three Components. One Written Document."

**Before you build triggers — ask three questions for every position:**
1. **What is my target?** What price or return = success? A range, not a single number.
2. **What is my timeframe?** 3 months, 1 year, full cycle? A 6-month trade and a multi-year hold are managed completely differently.
3. **What would change my mind?** Key team leaves, major exploit, loses market share, regulatory action. Define these conditions before they happen.

---

**1. Price-based triggers:**
- Scale-out selling: sell in portions as price rises — 25% at 2×, another 25% at 3×, let remaining run
- Stop-loss levels: pre-set a floor (e.g. 15–25% below entry for higher-risk alts) — limits damage, removes emotion
- Moving average breaks: sustained break below 50-day or 200-day MA = trend has shifted, trimming makes sense

**2. Condition-based triggers:**
- **Fundamental changes:** Team changes significantly, major partnership falls through, competitor clearly takes market share → the reason you bought no longer holds
- **Overvaluation signals:** Market cap requires unrealistic adoption to justify upside → risk/reward has shifted
- **Liquidity conditions tighten:** Reduce altcoin exposure, rotate to BTC or stablecoins — this is what our current portfolio reflects

**3. Time-based triggers:**
- **Cycle-based selling:** Sell gradually over a defined window (months, not one day) during historically strong periods. You don't need to call the exact top.
- **Personal milestones:** House deposit, business funding, specific financial target → hitting your goal is a valid trigger regardless of market direction. **"Protecting gains that matter to you personally is always legitimate."**
