The Shift Engine: Marketing & Content Operating System

This is the single source of truth for how Collective Shift creates, distributes, and measures every piece of marketing content. It unifies brand identity, social strategy, email marketing, content production, and measurement into one operating system - powered by a House of Brands model with four distinct voices: Ben Simpson (founder), Travis Thomas (growth), Sarn Elliott (education), and Matt Willemsen (research). No more solo-creator dependency. One system. Four voices. Every channel.

Executive Summary & Vision

Why the Shift

For five years, Collective Shift's content was synonymous with Ben Simpson. That was a strength (personal trust, founder intimacy) and a ceiling (one person can't scale to 61+ content pieces per week across six platforms). The House of Brands model solves this: Ben stays the primary face, but Travis, Sarn, and Matt each own content lanes that compound the brand's reach, credibility, and surface area without diluting what makes CS unique.

How Individual Brands Serve the Master

  • Discovery: People find Travis on YouTube, Sarn's tutorial, or Matt's research thread. Each touchpoint routes them to the CS ecosystem.
  • Credibility: Multiple trusted voices are more convincing than one. When a prospect sees Ben's conviction, Matt's data, and Travis's framework - they're sold on the team, not a person.
  • Scalability: Ben can't film 37 reels/week forever. Four voices means 60+ pieces without burning out one person.
  • Resilience: If Ben takes a week off, content doesn't stop. The engine keeps running.

Optimise (Improve Quality)

  • ManyChat triggers below 50/week
  • Content scores below 3.0 average
  • High volume but low conversion to calls
  • Any person consistently scoring 2 or below
