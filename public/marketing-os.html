Executive Summary & Vision

Why the Shift

For five years, Collective Shift's content was synonymous with Ben Simpson. The House of Brands model solves this: Ben stays the primary face, but Travis, Sarn, and Matt each own content lanes that compound the brand's reach, credibility, and surface area without diluting what makes CS unique.

How Individual Brands Serve the Master

  • Discovery: People find Travis on YouTube, Sarn's tutorial, or Matt's research thread. Each touchpoint routes them to the CS ecosystem.
  • Credibility: Multiple trusted voices are more convincing than one. When a prospect sees Ben's conviction, Matt's data, and Travis's framework - they're sold on the team, not a person.
  • Scalability: Ben can't film 37 reels/week forever. Four voices means 60+ pieces without burning out one person.
  • Resilience: If Ben takes a week off, content doesn't stop. The engine keeps running.

The Do This / Not That

Do This

  • "Digital assets" - professional positioning
  • "Build generational wealth" - ICP's #1 goal
  • "Bitcoin" (capital B, always)
  • Dashes (-) or commas - clean writing
  • "Members / community" - not followers
  • "Educator / peer investor"
  • Data-backed claims with sources

Not That

  • "Crypto" - signals retail culture
  • Em dashes - look like AI text
  • "Moon / lambo / WAGMI / degen"
  • "Financial advice" / "signals"
  • "Guru" / "expert"
  • Income claims or return guarantees
  • All caps for body text
