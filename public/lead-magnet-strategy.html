07 / Costco Model

Low-Ticket to Platinum Ascension Path

Jeremy Haynes' Low-Ticket to High-Ticket Ascension (Skill #16) applied to CS. The lead magnet gives a taste. The taste creates a gap. The gap creates desire for the full solution. Nobody goes from cold to $14,999. They go from cold to free tool to knowledge gap to VSL to setter call to Platinum. Each step is an easy yes.

The Temperature Ladder
TemperatureTouchpointCost to ProspectWhat It DoesNext Step
Cold TOFU content (Reel, YouTube, ad) Free (attention) Shifts from "digital assets are a scam" to "wait, I should pay attention to this" Follow, watch more, comment keyword
Cool Lead magnet tool (any of 5 keywords) Free (3 minutes + IG DM) Solves one splinter problem. Creates knowledge gap. "My score is 3/10. What do I do?" Watch VSL or engage with setter
Warm VSL (15 min video) Free (15 minutes) Full framework overview. States $14,999 price. Builds conviction. "This is exactly what I need." Book a portfolio review call
Hot Setter call (15-20 min) Free (time) Qualification. Confirms fit, budget awareness, intent. Answers remaining questions. Strategy session with closer
Ready Closer call / strategy session (30-45 min) Free (time) Deep portfolio review. Personalised recommendations. The close. Platinum ($14,999/yr)
MEMBER Platinum membership $14,999/year Full system. Personalised guidance. Community. Tax strategy. Portfolio management education. Black Tier ($33K) upsell at Day 120
The Costco Model: Costco gives you free samples. You taste the sample. You buy the product. The sample is free but it is NOT the product. The lead magnet is the sample. It must be genuinely good (so they trust you) but incomplete (so they want more). If the sample is the entire meal, nobody buys. If the sample is stale, nobody trusts you. Get this balance right and the entire funnel works.

If a variant's close rate is below 20%, investigate: wrong audience? Weak tool? Poor setter context? If cost per Platinum from a variant exceeds $3,500, pause and diagnose before scaling.
