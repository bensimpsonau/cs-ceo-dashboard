Part B: The 5-Layer Protection Model

The actual framework. Five layers, each building on the previous. This is the "how" — from identifying the gap in their current portfolio to setting the rules that keep the allocation working over time. Not speculation. Protection with asymmetric upside.

Layer 1 Identify the Gap
Inflation rate vs real portfolio returns. Show them the math. If CPI is 4% and their portfolio returns 6% pre-tax, the real return after tax may be negative. Most investors have never run this calculation. This layer makes the abstract problem concrete with their own numbers.
Layer 2 The Protection Allocation
Why 5-10% in digital assets changes a portfolio's risk profile without dominating it. The non-correlation thesis: Bitcoin has historically shown low correlation with equities, property, and bonds. A small allocation acts as portfolio insurance — it doesn't need to be the main bet to materially improve the risk-adjusted return.
Layer 3 Choose Your Vehicle
NA: Spot Bitcoin, ETF (BlackRock IBIT / Fidelity FBTC), Self-Directed IRA, Solo 401(k), direct custody.
AU: Spot Bitcoin, SMSF allocation, direct custody.
Each vehicle has different tax treatment, custody requirements, and accessibility. The right choice depends on their existing structure, not on the market.
Layer 4 Structure the Entry
DCA vs lump sum. Why structure matters more than timing. A systematic entry removes emotion from the equation. For a $100K allocation: DCA over 3-6 months reduces timing risk while maintaining upside exposure. The process is the edge, not the price.
Layer 5 Set Your Rules
Rebalancing triggers, custody standards, what you're protecting against (currency debasement, not volatility speculation). Rules decided in advance, not in the moment. When to rebalance back to target weight. When to add. What constitutes an acceptable drawdown. This is what separates investing from gambling.
Part C: The 3 Rules of Intelligent Positioning

Three non-negotiable rules that separate intelligent allocators from retail speculators. These are the guardrails. Print them. Follow them. They prevent every expensive mistake we have seen in 9 years.

Rule 1
Never allocate what you can't afford to hold through a 50% drawdown
If a 50% drop in your digital asset allocation would force you to sell, change your lifestyle, or lose sleep — you have allocated too much. Size for the worst case. If the worst case is tolerable, the upside takes care of itself.
Rule 2
Structure before size — get the vehicle/custody right before sizing up
Do not put $100K into a personal exchange account and then try to restructure into a Self-Directed IRA later. The tax event alone could cost tens of thousands. Get the structure right first. Then scale into it. This saves more money than any trade ever will.
Rule 3
Protect first, grow second — this is insurance with asymmetric upside
Frame this allocation as portfolio insurance, not a growth bet. The asymmetry is the bonus: if it works, the upside is 10x+. If it doesn't, the downside is 5-10% of your portfolio — uncomfortable but not life-changing. That is the definition of a smart hedge.
