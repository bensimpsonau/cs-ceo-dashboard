01 / Strategy Overview

The Full Funnel: Cold to Platinum

Organic and paid are not separate strategies. They feed the same funnel. Organic proves which hooks and magnets convert. Paid amplifies the winners. Both channels deliver leads into the same email nurture and setter pipeline. The only difference is the entry point.

Funnel Architecture
Step 1: Cold Audience
HNW Australians, 35-60, $500K+ investable. Self-directed investors who know digital assets matter but lack professional infrastructure. Reached via Hammer Them content ads (paid) or organic Reels/YouTube (organic).
↓
Step 2: TOF Ad or Organic Reel
Organic: Ben publishes IG Reel with Hand Raiser CTA. "Comment [KEYWORD] and I will DM you the link." Paid: Meta lead gen ad drives to landing page with email capture and lead magnet download.
↓
Step 3: Lead Magnet Delivery
Organic: ManyChat DM delivers link within 60 seconds. Paid: Landing page instant download + email confirmation. Both qualify: tools ask about portfolio size, goals, risk. The lead magnet diagnoses. It does not prescribe.
↓
Step 4: 14-Day Email Nurture
Customer.io sequence: 9 emails over 14 days. Delivers value, builds trust, handles objections. Every email reinforces the gap between what the lead magnet showed and what Platinum solves. Soft CTA on Day 13. Hard CTA on Day 14.
↓
Step 5: Setter Call
15-20 min qualification call. Setter has full context: which lead magnet, what result, email engagement signals. Confirms fit, budget awareness, intent. If qualified, books closer call.
↓
Step 6: Closer → Platinum $14,999
30-45 min strategy session. Deep portfolio review. Personalised recommendations. The close. Target: 25-35% close rate. Revenue per close: $14,999.
Key principle: Lead magnets are qualification tools, not just list builders. Every question the tool asks, every metric it surfaces, and every word in the copy is designed to attract people who can afford $14,999 and repel people who cannot. A lead magnet that generates 500 unqualified leads is worse than one that generates 50 HNW investors who match the ICP. Engineering the offer means embedding qualification into the hook, the copy, and the tool itself.

02 / The Lead Magnet Ideas Bank

12 Lead Magnets, 3 Categories, One Funnel

Twelve different entry points, one destination. Each lead magnet targets a different pain bucket but qualifies for the same outcome: a setter call that leads to Platinum $14,999. Grouped by type: self-assessment tools the ICP loves to benchmark themselves with, education and frameworks that make them feel informed not sold to, and interactive high-intent tools that qualify the hottest leads.

04 / Split Test Ideas

3 Tests Per Lead Magnet, Ready to Run

For each of the 5 lead magnets: one ManyChat DM hook test, one paid ad angle test, and one landing page headline test. All copy is genuinely different, not reworded. Run each test with a minimum of 200 impressions per variant before calling a winner.

STRATEGY  Split Tests
ManyChat Opening DM
Test A — Direct Delivery
"Hey, here is your Digital Asset Strategy Tool. It asks 5 quick questions about your situation then generates your personalised strategy. Takes about 3 minutes."
Hypothesis: wins on clarity. No friction, no tease. The person commented the keyword so they already want it. Just deliver.
Test B — Curiosity Gap
"Hey, this is the same strategy tool our Platinum members use before they build their portfolios. Most people are surprised by what it recommends. Plug in your numbers and see."
Hypothesis: may drive higher completion rate by creating curiosity about what "surprised" means. Risk: feels less personal.
Paid Ad Angle
Test A — Aspiration
"I built a tool that creates your personalised digital asset strategy in 3 minutes. The same framework 3,000+ investors use to structure $500K-$5M+ portfolios."
Hypothesis: wins with warm audiences who already follow Ben. The aspirational "3,000+ investors" line creates social proof and draws in people who want to be in that group.
Test B — Problem Agitation
"You have been thinking about digital assets for months. Maybe years. You have read the articles. Watched the videos. And you still do not have a plan. This tool changes that in 3 minutes."
Hypothesis: wins with cold audiences. Calls out the inaction pattern directly. More emotional. May outperform on CPL but could attract lower-intent leads.
Landing Page Headline
Test A — Outcome-Led
"Get Your Personalised Digital Asset Strategy in 3 Minutes"
Hypothesis: wins. Clear, specific, time-bound. HNW investors are busy. Telling them exactly what they get and how long it takes removes friction.
Test B — Authority-Led
"The Strategy Framework 3,000+ Australian Investors Use to Build Digital Asset Portfolios"
Hypothesis: may win if traffic is cold and needs trust before action. Social proof in the headline does the convincing. Risk: less clear on what you get.
SCORECARD  Split Tests
ManyChat Opening DM
Test A — Score Anchor
"Hey, here is your Wealth Protection Scorecard. 6 questions, about 2 minutes. The average score is 4.2 out of 10. Let me know what you get."
Hypothesis: wins. The "4.2 out of 10" creates immediate curiosity. People want to beat the average. Drives completion rate.
Test B — Fear Framing
"Hey, here is the scorecard. It will show you exactly where your portfolio is exposed. Most people are not prepared for what they find. 6 questions. 2 minutes."
Hypothesis: stronger emotional hook but may reduce completion if people feel anxiety about the result. Test against A to see if the fear framing lifts or suppresses action.
Paid Ad Angle
Test A — Diagnostic
"Most HNW investors think their portfolio is protected from inflation. Most are wrong. Take this 2-minute scorecard and find out where you actually stand."
Hypothesis: wins. Classic pattern interrupt. "Most are wrong" challenges their assumption. The diagnostic framing makes it feel safe (it is a test, not a pitch).
Test B — Statistic Lead
"We scored 3,000+ investor portfolios for inflation protection. The average was 4.2 out of 10. Where does yours sit? 2-minute assessment. Instant result."
Hypothesis: the specific number (4.2/10) may outperform the vaguer "most are wrong" on click-through. Adds credibility. Risk: less emotionally charged.
Landing Page Headline
Test A — Challenge
"How Protected Is Your Portfolio? Take the 2-Minute Scorecard."
Hypothesis: wins. Direct challenge. Implies they might not know the answer. Short. Clean.
Test B — Shock Stat
"The Average Investor Scores 4.2 Out of 10. What Will You Score?"
Hypothesis: the competitive angle ("I bet I score higher") may drive more completions. Risk: some people may be put off by the implication they will score badly.
CALCULATOR  Split Tests
ManyChat Opening DM
Test A — Speed Focus
"Hey, here is the Allocation Calculator. Plug in your numbers and it will give you your recommended digital asset allocation in about 60 seconds."
Hypothesis: wins. "60 seconds" is the strongest selling point. Fastest tool in the set. Speed = low commitment = higher completion.
Test B — Comparison Hook
"Hey, here is the calculator. It will show you your recommended allocation AND how it compares to what institutional-grade portfolios look like. Most people are surprised by the gap."
Hypothesis: the "gap" framing may drive more curiosity and shareability. Risk: slightly longer, slightly more friction. Test whether the comparison angle outperforms pure speed.

05 / Paid TOF Ad Strategy

Jeremy's Framework Applied to CS Lead Magnets

Ad objective: lead generation, not awareness. Organic proves which hooks convert. Paid amplifies the winners. Never put paid behind a lead magnet that has not been tested organically first. The IG comment data tells you what the market wants. The paid budget tells the market to give you more of it.

4 Ad Types to Run
Type 1 Video View Retargeting Ads
ObjectiveVideo Views / ThruPlay. Build warm audiences from cold traffic at scale. This is the "Hammer Them" layer.
AudienceCold: broad interests (personal finance, property investment, ASX, SMSF). Then retarget 25%+ viewers into lead magnet ads.
Budget30% of total TOF ad spend. Target cost per ThruPlay: $0.25-$0.50.
Hook/AngleBen's podcast clips, market commentary, Platinum member testimonials, educational clips on allocation and tax. Content only. No CTA. No pitch.
CTANone. The goal is consumption, not clicks. These viewers become the warm audience for Type 2.
Type 2 Lead Magnet Direct Ads
ObjectiveLead generation. Drive to lead magnet landing page with email capture. "Free: [Lead Magnet Name]".
AudienceWarm: 25%+ video viewers from Type 1, IG/FB engagers (30-day), email subscriber lookalike. Do NOT run to cold.
Budget40% of total TOF ad spend. Target CPL: under $30.

CTA"Get Your Free [Strategy/Scorecard/Allocation/Blueprint/Tax Guide]" — one button, one page, one action.
Type 3 Venus Fly Trap Sequence
ObjectiveWarm cold audiences through a 3-video content sequence before pitching. V1 Educate, V2 Prove, V3 Trust, then DR (book a call).
AudienceCold: broad HNW interests. Sequence narrows: V1 viewers (25%+) see V2, V2 viewers see V3, V3 viewers see DR. 75-90% drop-off per step is normal.
Budget20% of total TOF ad spend. V1 gets the largest share (largest audience, cheapest views).
Hook/AngleV1 (Educate): "Most HNW investors are missing a critical piece of their portfolio infrastructure. Here is what the top 1% are doing with digital assets." V2 (Prove): Member case study with specific results. V3 (Trust): Objection handling: risk, time, "I already own some Bitcoin." DR: "Book a portfolio review call."
CTAV1-V3: none (consumption only). DR: "Book Your Free Portfolio Review" or lead magnet landing page.

Budget10% of total TOF ad spend. Small budget but highest quality. Scale if ROAS holds above 2x.
Hook/AngleSame creative as Type 2 (lead magnet direct ads). The audience quality does the work. No need for different creative.
