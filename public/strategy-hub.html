Three Investor Types

Every member falls into one of three categories. The Shift OS tailors frameworks, worked examples, and allocation guidance to each:

💰 Wealth Generation

Growing capital significantly. Higher risk tolerance. Longer time horizon. Larger altcoin allocation as satellite positions around a Bitcoin core.

🛡️ Wealth Protection

Protecting existing wealth. Bitcoin-dominant (70–80%). Lower amounts per DCA. Focus on long-term thesis over price action.

📈 Income Creation

Generating yield on holdings. Stablecoin and DeFi strategies. Systematic withdrawal schedules. Yield strategies in Advanced track.

Level 1

Level 1 — Beginner Track

The complete foundation: from understanding why digital assets exist to a written investment strategy with buy targets, allocation, and sell plan.

Foundations Session (B1: Modules 1–4)

A one-off, 2-hour live session (40–50 slides) covering the foundational framework every investor needs before allocating a dollar.

B1-M1

Foundations

Why digital assets exist, why they matter, and where we are in the cycle. The macro lens every investor needs.

  • Understand what makes Bitcoin fundamentally different from traditional assets
  • Identify where we are in the market cycle using simple signals
  • Understand why most people lose money (emotional decisions, no framework)
  • Articulate a one-sentence market view

Key Deliverable: Written answers to: (1) Why did you join CS? (2) What's your #1 goal?

B1-M2

Bitcoin

Why Bitcoin is fundamentally different from every other digital asset — and how to build conviction before the fear hits.

  • Understand Bitcoin's core value: fixed supply, decentralised, no counterparty
  • Distinguish Bitcoin from altcoins as two separate asset classes
  • Build a personal "I will buy at X" conviction framework
  • Define a BTC allocation target and DCA approach

Key Deliverable: Written BTC allocation target (%), buy prices, DCA plan, conviction level (1–10).

B1-M3

Altcoins

Understanding altcoins as a higher-risk asset class — and how to filter the 98% you should never invest in.

  • Understand why altcoins are venture bets, not monetary assets
  • Learn how narrative cycles work and why most fail
  • Apply the CS filter framework to evaluate any altcoin
  • Understand tokenomics and the supply trap

Key Deliverable: Per-altcoin assessment — which you hold, why, filter score, max position size, exit criteria.

B1-M4

How We Invest / Principles

The CS investment philosophy — frameworks over predictions, risk-first, education not advice.

  • Understand CS philosophy: frameworks > predictions
  • Identify the four types of investment risk (market, security, liquidity, emotional)
  • Apply the 50% rule to personal position sizing
  • Recognise the emotional cycle and cognitive biases

Key Deliverable: Master action — Goals + BTC plan + altcoin framework + setup plan + sell strategy = investment thesis.

Practical How-To's (B2: Modules 5–8)

B2 modules rotate through the weekly Beginner Coaching Call. 15–25 slides per module. Interactive coaching format.

B2-M5

How to Buy & Sell

The practical mechanics of buying and selling on a centralised exchange — by country, step by step. Exchange selection, market vs limit orders, OTC for larger amounts.

B2-M6

Send, Receive & Store Safely

Moving digital assets safely, understanding addresses and networks, and setting up self-custody with a hardware wallet. "24 words. Handwritten. Two locations. Never digital."

B2-M7

Tax Basics

Country-specific tax basics (AU, NZ, US, CA), transaction tracking with Koinly/CryptoTaxCalculator, and finding a specialist accountant.

B2-M8

Sell Strategy Basics

The most important module. Building a written sell strategy before you need it — by goal type. Scale-out frameworks for each investor type.
