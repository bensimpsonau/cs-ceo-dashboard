03 / Hidden VSSL Framework

Jeremy Haynes' Hidden VSSL: content that sells without triggering the "this guy is selling me" response. For HNW audiences who are allergic to hard sells. Every organic post follows this structure. The audience gets genuine value AND naturally encounters the Platinum offer without feeling pitched.

Jeremy Framework: Hidden VSSL

Hidden VSSL Structure for Every Organic Post

The Formula

  1. Hook (3 sec): Pattern interrupt. Bold claim or provocative question. Text on screen matches spoken word.
  2. Education (60-120 sec): Genuine, useful content. Teach something real. This is NOT a teaser. Give actual value.
  3. Natural Bridge (10 sec): The invisible transition. "This is exactly what we help our Platinum members implement..." or "If you want this built for your specific situation..."
  4. Soft CTA (5 sec): Comment a keyword or DM. Never "buy now" or "sign up." Always a next step that feels like getting more value, not entering a sales funnel.

Content-Demographic Matching (Jeremy's Rule): For HNW Australians, the right formats are: talking head in a professional setting, market analysis with screen shares, case studies with specific (anonymised) results, long-form YouTube, LinkedIn articles. 04 / What Not How Framework

Every piece of content Ben creates must follow this rule: tell them WHAT to do and WHY, but not exactly HOW. The "how" is what Platinum delivers. This creates desire without giving everything away. It's the engine behind the Hidden VSSL and the lead magnet ascension funnel.

Jeremy Framework: What Not How

Structuring Every Ben Video and Post

What to Include (FREE content)

  • The problem and why it matters ("You're losing purchasing power every year")
  • The category of solution ("You need an allocation framework")
  • Proof that the solution works ("Our members have built portfolios that...")
  • General principles ("Institutional investors allocate 2-5% minimum")
  • The cost of inaction ("Every year you wait costs you X in opportunity")
  • Social proof ("500+ investors have used this framework")

What to Hold Back (PLATINUM delivers)

  • The exact personalised allocation for their situation
  • Step-by-step implementation (which coins, which custodians, which structure)
  • Personalised tax strategy and SMSF setup
  • Ongoing portfolio management and rebalancing triggers
  • Access to the research team and live calls
  • The community of HNW peers making the same journey
The Knowledge Gap Principle: Every free piece of content should answer one question and raise two more. The lead magnet (STRATEGY, SCORECARD, CALCULATOR, BLUEPRINT, TAX) diagnoses their situation. Platinum prescribes and implements. If the free content does everything Platinum does, there's no reason to upgrade. The content should make them say: "I see the problem clearly now. I need help solving it."

05 / The 90/10 Rule

Jeremy Haynes' content ratio: 90% value, 10% offer. Applied rigorously. When your audience trusts that 9 out of 10 posts will teach them something useful, they don't skip the 1 post that sells. They lean in.

Jeremy Framework: 90/10 Content Ratio

90% Value / 10% Offer

What "Value" Means (The 90%)

  • Education: Teach something real about digital assets, allocation, tax, or wealth protection. Use the What Not How framework.
  • Market commentary: Ben's take on what's happening. ETF flows, institutional moves, macro shifts. Authority positioning.
  • Social proof (organic): Member stories told as lessons, not testimonials. "Here's what one investor learned when they restructured their portfolio."
  • Behind-the-scenes: Platinum day footage, team research process, what it looks like inside the membership. Let people see the product without pitching it.
  • Thought leadership: Contrarian takes, industry analysis, future predictions. Position Ben as the authority.

What "Offer" Means (The 10%)

  • Direct CTA to book a portfolio review call
  • Lead magnet promotion with ManyChat keyword (STRATEGY, SCORECARD, etc.)
  • Platinum membership open invitation
  • Monthly webinar announcement

08 / Pixel Conditioning

Jeremy Haynes' most important concept for high-ticket: train Meta's algorithm to find your ideal buyer by being transparent about price and qualification. Short-term pain, long-term gold.

Jeremy Framework: Pixel Conditioning

State the Price. Always.

Non-Negotiable: $14,999 must appear in the VSL, on the landing page, and in pre-call communications. If the current funnel hides the price to get more calls, this is actively damaging pixel quality and attracting wrong-fit leads. Fix this FIRST.

How Pixel Conditioning Works

  1. Week 1-2: Fewer calls. Unqualified people see the price, bounce, and tell Meta "I'm not the right person." This is good.
  2. Week 3-4: Call volume stabilises. But the people booking are price-aware and pre-qualified.
  3. Week 5+: Meta's algorithm has learned what a $14,999 buyer looks like. It finds MORE of them. Call quality transforms. Close rates go from 20% to 40%+. Show rates go from 50% to 70%+.

Where $14,999 Must Appear

  • In the VSL (spoken by Ben, on screen)
  • On the landing page (below the VSL)
  • In the ManyChat qualification flow ("Platinum is $14,999/year. Is that within your investment range?")
  • In the confirmation page (reinforces the investment level)
  • In the pre-call email ("Your portfolio review is booked. Platinum membership is $14,999/year.")

Rich-Person Messaging (Jeremy's Rule)

HNW investors EXPECT price transparency. Hiding the price signals you're a scam. Being upfront about $14,999 signals confidence and professionalism. This matches CS brand voice:

  • Use: "digital assets" (not "crypto"). "Infrastructure" (not "portfolio"). "Framework" (not "course"). "Membership" (not "program").
  • Tone: Direct, authoritative, certain. No filler. No hype. No urgency tactics. No value stacks ("this is worth $50K but..."). HNW investors see right through it.
  • Claims: Conservative. "Our members have built institutional-grade portfolios" NOT "make 100x returns."

14 / Two-Track Ad Structure

Jeremy's non-negotiable: content consumption and direct response must run as separate campaigns. Different objectives, different budgets, different optimisation targets. Never mix them.

Campaign Architecture

Track 1: "Ben Simpson Organic"

Type: Hammer Them (content consumption)

Objective: Video Views / ThruPlay

Budget: 30% of total ($21-24K/mo)

Creative: 20+ content clips, rotated weekly

CTA: None. Pure content consumption.

Goal: Build warm retargetable audiences cheaply

Key Metric: Cost per ThruPlay ($0.25-$0.50)

Track 2: "Ben Simpson Education"

Type: Direct Response (call booking)

Objective: Conversions (book a call)

Budget: 70% of total ($49-58K/mo)

Creative: VSL, lead magnet ads, Venus Fly Trap DR

CTA: Book a portfolio review call

Goal: 117 booked calls/month at $600-700/call

Key Metric: Cost per booked call ($600-700)

Why Separate: Mixing content consumption and direct response in the same campaign confuses Meta's algorithm. It doesn't know whether to optimise for views or conversions. The result: worse at both. Separate campaigns = each one optimises for its specific objective. The content campaign builds the audience. The DR campaign converts it.

16 / Meta Ad Compliance and Risk Mitigation

Jeremy Haynes' Meta Ad Restrictions Prep: digital asset education is in Meta's highest-risk category. One account ban without preparation = revenue to zero. Prepare now.

Jeremy Framework: Meta Ad Restrictions Prep

Compliance Checklist

  • Remove from all ad copy: Specific return promises, guaranteed outcomes, income claims, urgency on financial products, "get rich" language
  • Reframe everything as: Educational. "Learn how." "Build infrastructure." "Understand your options."
  • Backup domain ready: If collectiveshift.io gets restricted, have bensimpson.com.au or similar with a duplicate funnel
  • Backup conversion events: If "Lead" events get restricted, have "PageView" or "ViewContent" configured as fallbacks
  • Third-party attribution: Hyros or similar, so if Meta tracking degrades you still have data
  • Customer.io sync: Block active + cancelled Platinum and Pro members from seeing acquisition ads (saves budget + improves pixel quality)
  • Documentation: Keep clean records of all ad content, landing pages, and compliance checks for appeals
