📊 Member Segmentation Framework

Segment all 40 May renewals into three tiers based on engagement signals, tenure, and value. The tier determines who reaches out, what offer they get, and how aggressive the retention effort is.

Tier Definitions

Tier 1: High-Value Likely Renewers

Ben Personal Touch

Size estimate: 12-15 members (~$180-225K)

Signals:

  • Attended 3+ Platinum calls in past 90 days
  • Active in community (posted or reacted in past 30 days)
  • Opened 60%+ of emails in past 90 days
  • Multi-seat account OR referred another member
  • Previously renewed at least once

Strategy: Ben personal outreach. Short video or voice note. Thank them. Show them what is coming in the next 12 months. Make them feel like insiders. Offer multi-year lock-in as a loyalty reward, not a discount.

Owner: Ben (personal outreach) + Reece or DT (logistics and payment)

Tier 2: At-Risk Members

Nurture + Call

Size estimate: 15-18 members (~$225-270K)

Signals:

  • Moderate engagement: attends some calls, opens some emails, but inconsistent
  • No community activity in past 60 days
  • Has not logged in to portal in 30+ days
  • First-year member (no prior renewal history)
  • Mentioned concerns about market conditions or timing

Strategy: Re-engage before asking for renewal. Deliver value first. Market update from Ben, personalised content recommendation, then closer call to understand their situation. Anchor on what they have already learned and built. Surface the cost of leaving (community access, research pipeline, upcoming content).

Owner: Reece or DT (primary) with Ben support (video message or call if needed)

Tier 3: Likely Churn

Multi-Year or Downgrade Path

Size estimate: 8-12 members (~$120-180K)

Signals:

  • No login in 90+ days
  • Zero call attendance in past 90 days
  • Email open rate below 20%
  • No community engagement
  • Expressed financial concerns or dissatisfaction
  • Market downturn has them spooked (BTC down, Fear index at 9)

Strategy: Acknowledge the market. Lead with empathy, not sales. Offer two paths: (1) Multi-year lock at a meaningful discount to reduce annual pain, or (2) Downgrade to Pro ($3,000/yr) with a clear path back to Platinum. Losing them entirely = $0. Pro retention = $3,000 and a future Platinum renewal when conditions improve.

Owner: Reece or DT (outreach) + Ben (personal video for highest-value members in this tier)

Pricing Psychology

Pixel Conditioning Effect

Every member has seen $14,999 repeatedly. In the original sales process, in renewal emails, in community references. That number is anchored. When they see $13,499 or $11,999, the discount registers immediately because the anchor is so well established. This is pixel conditioning applied to renewals.

Anchoring Sequence

Always present the standard rate first ($14,999). Then introduce multi-year as an alternative. The standard rate anchors the perceived value. The multi-year rate feels like a reward by comparison. Never lead with the discount.

Loss Framing

Frame the multi-year as "locking in the current rate." Imply (without guaranteeing) that annual pricing may increase. "At $14,999 this year, the multi-year option lets you lock in today's rate. I cannot guarantee what the annual price will be in 12 months as we add more value to the membership."

Cycle Framing

The 3-year option specifically maps to market cycles. "You will be positioned through the entire next cycle. No renewal anxiety. No re-evaluation during a market downturn. Just continuous access to research, community, and framework."
