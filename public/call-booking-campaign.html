5. Optimization & Best Practices

Subject Line A/B Testing Plan

  • Test one variable at a time. Subject line A vs B, not subject + preview text + send time simultaneously.
  • Sample size: Send variant A to 15% of the segment, variant B to 15%, wait 2 hours, send winner to remaining 70%.
  • What to test: Data-led ("$22.67 trillion") vs emotional ("Every cycle, people say the same thing"). Scarcity-led ("3 sessions left") vs curiosity ("The biggest bank just made its move").
  • Track in Customer.io: Tag each campaign step. UTM: ?utm_source=email&utm_medium=campaign&utm_campaign=4day-sessions-v3&utm_content=email1a

Overbooked? Waitlist Strategy

  • If all 20 spots fill before Day 4, send a "Sold Out" email immediately to the full list.
  • Include a waitlist link: "All 20 sessions are taken. Join the waitlist and you'll be first in line if someone cancels: [WAITLIST_LINK]"
  • This is actually the best outcome. It validates the scarcity and creates demand for the next round.
  • Add 3-5 overflow spots the next week for waitlist contacts.

Running This Quarterly Without Fatigue

  • Rotate the market angle: Q2 = "Money Printing + EOFY + $MSBT." Q3 = "Mid-Year Review + Halving Window." Q4 = "Year-End + Regulation Clarity." Q1 = "New Year + Fresh ETF Data."
  • Rotate the bonus: Q2 = Blueprint + EOFY Tax Guide. Q3 = Blueprint + Exit Strategy Template. Q4 = Blueprint + Market Brief.
  • Never run the same copy twice. Same structure, new data and testimonials each quarter.
  • Rest period: Minimum 6 weeks between campaigns.
  • Exclude recent participants: Anyone who booked a session last round should not receive the next one.
