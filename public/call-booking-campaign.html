Executive Summary

This 4-day campaign takes the proven Four Day Cash Machine framework and redirects every conversion point to a single outcome: booking a free 30-minute strategy call with the Collective Shift team. Instead of discounting a product, we are opening Ben's calendar as the offer itself. The call is the conversion mechanism for Accelerator ($5,000) and Platinum ($15,000). By stacking a free $97 Starter Kit bonus on top and creating real scarcity around limited call slots (25 spots total across Ben, Zen, and James), we give the 15,000-person Customer.io list a time-pressured reason to act now. Based on a conservative 35% open rate, 8% click rate, and 30% close rate on qualified calls, this campaign should generate 25+ strategy call bookings and 7-10 new Accelerator or Platinum sign-ups in 4 days. That is $35,000 to $150,000 in new revenue from a single email sequence with zero ad spend. The real upside: every booked call, even ones that do not close immediately, enters a prospect into the high-touch sales pipeline where follow-up and trust-building continue.

The Offer

Free 30-minute Strategy Call with Ben, Zen, or James. When you book, you get instant access to The Digital Asset Starter Kit ($97 value) as a bonus. Only 25 call slots available this week.

Why This Works (Call-Booking Psychology)

  • Real scarcity: Ben, Zen, and James can physically only take 25 calls in a week. This is not manufactured urgency. There are 25 calendar slots and 15,000 people receiving this email.
  • Value stacking: The call itself is valuable (personalised strategy for their portfolio). The $97 Starter Kit bonus reduces friction and gives immediate value even before the call happens.
  • Warm list advantage: These people already follow CS, opened past emails, attended webinars, or downloaded content. They know Ben. Trust is partially built. The campaign provides the final nudge.
  • Consultative, not hard-sell: The call is positioned as a strategy session, not a sales pitch. The ICP data confirms: "I can't be with the techno bro. And I can't be with the old world solid bankers." They want trusted guidance, not pressure.
  • Time pressure creates action: "This week only" with a specific closing deadline (Day 3 midnight) compresses the decision window.

Segmentation: Who Gets This Campaign

Not all 15,000 contacts should receive every email. Segment for maximum impact:

  • Priority 1 (send everything): Engaged subscribers who opened 3+ emails in the last 90 days, past webinar/masterclass attendees, Blueprint assessment completers who did not book a call, people who visited the pricing page or VSL page
  • Priority 2 (send emails 1-3 only): Subscribers who opened at least 1 email in the last 180 days, Starter Kit purchasers who did not upgrade
  • Exclude: Current Platinum and Pro members (they are already clients), unengaged contacts (no opens in 6+ months), anyone who has unsubscribed from promotional content

Timing Recommendations

  • Best launch day: Tuesday or Wednesday. Avoids Monday inbox overload and Friday wind-down.
  • SMS (Step 1): 8:00 AM AEST / 6:00 PM ET (Monday prior) or 8:00 AM AEST Tuesday
  • Email 1 (Step 2): 4 hours after SMS. 12:00 PM AEST / 10:00 PM ET
  • Email 2 (Step 3): Next morning 9:00 AM AEST / 7:00 PM ET
  • Email 3 (Step 4): Day after, 9:00 AM AEST / 7:00 PM ET
  • Email 4 (Step 5): Day after, 10:00 AM AEST / 8:00 PM ET (urgency timing)
  • Multi-timezone tip: AU list gets AEST-optimised sends. US/UK segment gets a 12-hour offset send of the same email.

Post-Campaign · Day 4

Post-Campaign: Follow-Up Sequences

For People Who Booked a Call

  • Immediately after booking: Automated Calendly confirmation + a personal email from Ben confirming the time and setting expectations for the call.
  • Starter Kit access: Deliver the $97 Starter Kit via Circle invitation within 15 minutes of booking. Include a note: "Start Module 1 before our call so we can skip the basics and get straight to your strategy."
  • 24 hours before call: SMS reminder with call link and a 2-minute prep checklist (know your current portfolio size, your 12-month goal, and your biggest question).
  • 1 hour before call: Final SMS reminder. "Looking forward to chatting in an hour. [CALL_LINK]"

No-Show Follow-Up

  • 30 minutes after missed call: SMS: "Hey [NAME], I think we missed each other. No stress. Grab a new time here: [RESCHEDULE_LINK]"
  • 24 hours later: Email from Ben: "Life happens. If you still want to chat about your digital asset strategy, I've held a spot for you. Rebook here: [RESCHEDULE_LINK]"
  • If no rebook after 3 days: Final email. "I don't want to keep chasing you. If the timing isn't right, no worries at all. But the Starter Kit is yours to keep. And whenever you're ready, we're here."

For People Who Didn't Book

  • Day 5 (one email): "The call spots are gone, but the Starter Kit is still available at collectiveshift.io/starter-kit if you want to start on your own timeline. No pressure."
  • Re-enter normal nurture sequence: These contacts go back into the Customer.io email cadence (weekly content, market updates, event invites). Do not hard-sell again for at least 3 weeks.

3. Bonus & Offer Ideas

Beyond the Starter Kit, here are 5 promotion concepts you can rotate across future campaign rounds.

1. Early Accelerator Access

Perceived value: $500+

What's included: Priority registration for the next Accelerator cohort ($5K, 4-week program). Bookers skip the waitlist and lock their spot before public announcement.

Why it works: Creates exclusivity. Accelerator cohorts cap at a set number. Getting in early feels like insider access. Pairs well with Q1/Q2 campaigns when a new cohort is launching.

2. Portfolio Snapshot Review

Perceived value: $1,000+

What's included: During the strategy call, Ben/Zen/James will do a live walkthrough of the prospect's current portfolio using CS's framework. They walk away with written notes on allocation, risk, and suggested adjustments.

Why it works: Makes the call itself more tangible and valuable. ICP confidence in their own research is 3.7/10. A professional review addresses that directly.

3. Private Market Brief

Perceived value: $297

What's included: A PDF sent after booking with CS's current market thesis: top 3 positions, risk levels, and the macro setup. Normally only available to Platinum members.

Why it works: Gives a taste of the research quality. Demonstrates that CS has conviction and skin in the game. Builds trust before the call happens.

4. Exclusive Masterclass Replay

Perceived value: $197

What's included: Access to the most recent live masterclass recording (usually gated behind registration). Topics rotate: "How to Build a 6-Figure Digital Asset Portfolio" or "The 3 Mistakes HNW Investors Make."

Why it works: Zero marginal cost to deliver. Extends the value stack without adding team workload. Masterclass content pre-qualifies the prospect for the call.

5. Ben's Exit Strategy Template

Perceived value: $497

What's included: A 1-page PDF template of the exact exit strategy framework CS uses with Platinum members. Includes profit-taking triggers, rebalancing rules, and circuit breakers. No personalisation (that's what the call is for), just the framework.

Why it works: Addresses the #1 ICP pain point directly: exit strategy confidence is rated 2.8 out of 10. This is the single most valuable asset you could give away. And it creates demand for the full implementation (which requires membership).

4. Optimization & Best Practices

Subject Line A/B Testing Plan

  • Test one variable at a time. Subject line A vs B, not subject + preview text + send time simultaneously.
  • Sample size: Send variant A to 15% of the segment, variant B to 15%, wait 2 hours, send winner to remaining 70%.
  • What to test: Curiosity vs specificity ("I'm opening my calendar" vs "25 free strategy call spots"). Personal vs benefit-led ("I nearly went bankrupt" vs "How 400 people built their digital asset strategy").
  • Measure: Open rate for subject lines. Click rate for body copy variations. Book rate for CTA placement.
  • Track in Customer.io: Tag each campaign step. Use UTM parameters on the calendar link: ?utm_source=email&utm_medium=campaign&utm_campaign=4day-calls&utm_content=email1a

Reply Handling

  • Ben replies personally to: Anyone who mentions a specific portfolio size ($100K+), anyone who asks a thoughtful question, anyone who says "I've been following you for X years", anyone who sounds like a qualified Platinum prospect.
  • Team handles: Scheduling questions, technical issues with the calendar link, general "tell me more" responses (send to VSL page), unsubscribe requests.
  • Template for team: "Hey [NAME], thanks for the reply. I've forwarded this to Ben. In the meantime, here's the link to book your call: [CALENDAR_LINK]. Talk soon."

Overbooked? Waitlist Strategy

  • If all 25 spots fill before Day 3, send a "Sold Out" email immediately to the full list.
  • Include a waitlist link: "All spots are taken. But if someone cancels or we add a few extra slots, I want to make sure you're first in line. Join the waitlist: [WAITLIST_LINK]"
  • This is actually the best outcome. It validates the scarcity and creates demand for the next round.
  • Add 3-5 overflow spots the next week for waitlist contacts. Send them a "A spot opened up" email.

Running This Quarterly Without Fatigue

  • Rotate the angle: Q1 = "New Year Strategy." Q2 = "EOFY Tax Strategy." Q3 = "Mid-Year Portfolio Check." Q4 = "Set Up for Next Year."
  • Rotate the bonus: Q1 = Starter Kit. Q2 = Exit Strategy Template. Q3 = Market Brief. Q4 = Masterclass Replay.
  • Never run the same copy twice. Refresh all 5 steps each quarter. Same structure, new specifics.
  • Rest period: Minimum 6 weeks between call booking campaigns. Run value-only content emails in between.
  • Exclude recent participants: Anyone who booked a call in the last campaign should not receive the next one. They are either a client or in the nurture pipeline.

Voice Checklist (Sounds Like Ben, Not AI)

  • Starts with "Hey [NAME]" not "Dear" or "Hi there"
  • No em dashes anywhere in the email
  • Short sentences. Paragraph breaks after 2-3 sentences max.
  • Uses "digital assets" not "crypto"
  • References specific data (116% CAGR, 1,100 members, $97 value)
  • Sounds like a mate who happens to be an expert, not a corporation
  • Includes vulnerability or personal context (not just sales pitch)
  • Signs off with just "Ben" (no "Best regards," no "Warm wishes")
  • P.S. line adds one more sharp point
  • No exclamation marks (or max one per email)
  • No ALL CAPS for emphasis
  • No emoji in email body
  • Would you believe Ben typed this on his phone between meetings? If yes, it passes.

Customer.io Campaign Setup

  • Campaign type: Triggered campaign (manual trigger on launch day)
  • Entry criteria: Segment membership (Priority 1 or Priority 2)

  • Delays: Step 1 (immediate) / Step 2 (4 hours) / Step 3 (next day 9AM) / Step 4 (day after 9AM) / Step 5 (day after 10AM)
  • A/B test: Built into each email step. 15%/15% sample, 2-hour evaluation, winner to remaining 70%.
  • Webhook: Calendly booking event fires to Customer.io, setting a user attribute "booked_strategy_call = true" and triggering the post-booking sequence.
  • Tracking: Create a dashboard in Customer.io: opens, clicks, bookings per step, total revenue attributed.
