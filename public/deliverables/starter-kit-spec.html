7-Day Structure

Each day has two sessions: Morning (Learn) and Afternoon (Do). Total time per day: 30-45 minutes.

Day 1: Foundations

Morning: What Are Digital Assets?
Source: CS10023 Lesson 1 | Blueprint Step 01

Content: Story of money (barter > gold > fiat > digital). What digital assets are and why they exist. Types overview (Bitcoin, stablecoins, tokens). Why this matters for wealth preservation.

Outcome: Understand the basics. Be able to explain digital assets to a friend without using jargon.

Afternoon: What They Can Do For Your Wealth
Source: CS10023 Lesson 2 | Blueprint Step 01

Content: Use cases that matter for investors (store of wealth, inflation hedge, portfolio diversification, borderless transactions). Real-world examples. Advantages over traditional systems.

Outcome: Identify 3 use cases that are personally relevant to your financial situation.

Day 2: Bitcoin Deep Dive

Morning: Where Bitcoin Fits
Source: CS10023 Lesson 3 | Blueprint Step 02

Content: Bitcoin history (Satoshi, 2009, born from the 2008 financial crisis). 6 properties (scarce at 21M cap, transparent, decentralised, borderless, freedom-preserving, fungible). 1 BTC = 100M sats. Bitcoin as digital gold and store of value.

Outcome: Understand why Bitcoin is fundamentally different from every other digital asset.

Afternoon: What Bitcoin Can Do For You
Source: CS10023 Lesson 4 | Blueprint Step 02

Content: Bitcoin as inflation hedge (80%+ of US dollars printed since 2020). Store of value thesis. Non-state money. Settlement advantages. Bitcoin as an alternative financial system.

Outcome: Articulate your personal "why" for Bitcoin. Write one paragraph explaining the inflation hedge thesis in your own words.

Day 3: Risk and Security

Morning: Understanding Risks
Source: CS10023 Lessons 5 | Blueprint Step 04

Content: Why risk management comes BEFORE buying. Terra LUNA example (top-10 to $0 in 48 hours). 12,763 digital assets have fallen 95%+. Risk tolerance assessment. The importance of starting with Bitcoin.

Outcome: Develop a realistic understanding of risk. Complete the risk tolerance self-assessment.

Afternoon: Security Essentials + Top 3 Risks
Source: CS10023 Lessons 6-7 | Blueprint Step 06

Content: Phishing deep-dive (6 warning signs). Exchange risk (your assets on an exchange are IOUs). Storage risk (lost passwords, seed phrases). Scam risk (verification, 2FA, bookmarks). 9 security tips that prevent 90% of problems.

Outcome: Set up 2FA on your email. Understand the three biggest risks and one mitigation for each. Complete the security checklist.

Day 4: Planning

Morning: Create Your Investment Plan
Source: CS10023 Lesson 9 | Blueprint Step 04

Content: Why planning matters. HODL vs DCA (dollar-cost averaging) explained. How much to allocate (percentage-based thinking, not dollar amounts). Time horizon. Basic exit triggers.

Outcome: Write your investment plan: how much to invest monthly, which asset to start with (Bitcoin), and your time horizon.

Afternoon: Choose Your Exchange
Source: CS10023 Lesson 10 | Blueprint Step 05

Content: Exchange selection criteria (your country, local currency support, fees, reputation, security track record). Recommended global exchanges: Coinbase (most trusted, beginner-friendly), Kraken (strong security, competitive fees), Binance (lowest fees, most options). How to compare.

Outcome: Choose your exchange. Write down which one and why.

Day 5: Setup

Morning: Set Up Account + KYC
Source: CS10023 Lesson 11 | Blueprint Step 05

Content: Account creation walkthrough. KYC (Know Your Customer) process explained. What identity documents you'll need. Step-by-step verification guide. What to do if verification is slow.

Outcome: Create your exchange account and submit KYC verification.

Afternoon: Fund Your Account
Source: CS10023 Lesson 12 | Blueprint Step 05

Content: Deposit methods (bank transfer, debit card, wire). Fees for each method. How much to start with (your plan from Day 4). Setting up recurring deposits for DCA. Common issues and troubleshooting.

Outcome: Fund your account with your planned starting amount. Account is ready for purchase.

Day 6: Action

Morning: Make Your First Purchase
Source: CS10023 Lesson 13 | Blueprint Step 05

Content: Buying options (simple buy vs advanced/limit orders). Fee comparison. Fractional buying explained (you don't need a whole Bitcoin). Practical walkthrough. What to do after buying.

Outcome: Buy your first Bitcoin. Screenshot the confirmation. You're now a digital asset investor.

Afternoon: Secure Your Storage
Source: CS10023 Lesson 14 | Blueprint Step 06

Content: Exchange custody = IOUs (FTX cautionary tale). Self-custody explained. Hot wallets vs cold wallets. Hardware wallet recommendations (Ledger, Trezor). When to move to hardware wallet (threshold: ~$2,000 USD equivalent). Withdrawal walkthrough.

Outcome: Understand self-custody. If holding >$2,000, have a plan for a hardware wallet. Know how to withdraw from exchange to wallet.

Day 7: Strategy + Next Steps

Morning: Your Portfolio Plan
Source: Blueprint Steps 01-06 synthesis

Content: Review everything you've built this week: knowledge foundation, risk awareness, investment plan, exchange account, first purchase, security setup. Consolidate into a single-page portfolio plan. DCA schedule confirmation. Portfolio tracking basics.

Outcome: Complete your one-page portfolio plan. Set up your DCA schedule. You have a written strategy, not just a purchase.

Afternoon: What's Next: The Full Shift OS
Source: Blueprint Steps 01-08 overview

Content: Introduce the complete 8-step Shift OS framework. Show them what they've accomplished:
- Step 1 (Foundations): DONE
- Step 2 (Bitcoin Conviction): DONE
- Step 5 (Buy & Sell): DONE
- Step 6 (Send & Store): DONE

Show them what's left:
- Step 3 (Altcoins): Should you diversify beyond Bitcoin? The 80/20 rule says only 20% of altcoins beat Bitcoin over a full cycle. Knowing which ones requires a framework.
- Step 4 (Risk Frameworks): Your basic plan is a start. The full investor profile, 3 Investor Types, and 4 Pre-Start Questions take it to the next level.
- Step 7 (Tax): Every swap, sale, and transfer has tax implications. Holding structures, lending against Bitcoin, compliance strategies.
- Step 8 (Sell Strategy): You have basic exit triggers. The full framework covers 3 types of sell strategies, specific triggers, and the discipline system.

CTA: "Book a free 15-minute strategy call and we'll map out your personalised next steps."

Outcome: Understand where you are in the 8-step framework. Know exactly what gaps remain. Clear path forward.
