GAP 2: YouTube Thumbnail Style Guide

2.1 Face Crop Rules

Positioning:
- Subject fills the right 35-45% of the frame (512-576px of 1280px width)
- Face should be cropped from mid-chest or shoulders up
- Eye line sits at 1/3 from the top of frame (approximately 240px from top)
- Head should not touch the top edge. Leave 30-50px clearance.
- Subject should angle slightly toward center of frame (toward text zone)

Facial Expression Categories:

Expression When to Use Content Type
Shock/surprise Unexpected data, market moves, contrarian takes "Bitcoin just did SOMETHING CRAZY"
Curiosity Questions, explorations, "what if" content "Is Ethereum dead?"
Concern/serious Risk warnings, bear market, mistakes to avoid "The crash nobody sees coming"
Excitement/smile Positive results, portfolio wins, good news "Our portfolio hit a new high"
Confidence/authority Educational, how-to, framework content "How I evaluate any altcoin"

Rules:
- Expression must match the emotional hook of the title
- Never use a neutral/flat expression. Thumbnails need emotion to drive clicks.
- Exaggerate by 20-30% compared to natural expression. Camera flattens emotion.
- Eyes should be clearly visible. No sunglasses. Glasses are fine if not reflective.

Lighting:
- Key light from left side (viewer's perspective), creating slight shadow on right side of face
- Fill light at 50-60% of key to avoid harsh shadows
- Clean, uncluttered background (dark preferred, or blurred environment)
- If shooting specifically for thumbnails, use a separate setup with controlled lighting
- Avoid mixed color temperatures (no warm lamp + cool daylight)

Cutout Style:
- Preferred: subject cut out from background with clean edges, placed on dark gradient
- Alternative: subject on dark background with orange glow/gradient behind head
- Orange glow: radial gradient, #FC5C03 at 20-30% opacity, positioned behind head/shoulders

2.2 Text Rules

  • Word count: 3-5 words maximum. Fewer is better. Thumbnails are read in under 2 seconds.
  • Font: Plus Jakarta Sans ExtraBold (weight 800)
  • Color: White (#FFFFFF) with 2-4px dark stroke or drop shadow (#0D0B14)
  • Size: Minimum 90px for readability on mobile. Target 100-120px for primary text.
  • Alignment: Left-aligned, positioned in the left 55-60% of frame
  • Vertical position: Top-center or center-left of frame
  • Never overlap the face. Text and face occupy separate zones with at least 30px gap.
  • Line breaks: Use manual line breaks to create visual impact. Stack 2-3 short lines.

Text Hierarchy (when using secondary text):
- Primary: PJS 800, 100-120px, #FFFFFF with stroke
- Secondary: PJS 700, 48-60px, #FC5C03 or #0BF4B9 (for emphasis words)
- Never more than 2 text levels per thumbnail

Highlight Techniques:
- Orange underline below key word (6px height, #FC5C03)
- Orange highlight box behind key word (8px padding, #FC5C03 fill, text stays white)
- Color-shift one word to orange or teal for emphasis

2.3 Color System for Thumbnails

Color Hex Usage
CS Dark #0D0B14 Primary background, text stroke
CS Dark Alt #1B1925 Gradient target, secondary zones
White #FFFFFF Primary text
Brand Orange #FC5C03 Accent borders, underlines, highlight boxes, glow effects
Brand Teal #0BF4B9 Positive/growth topics (portfolio up, bull market, gains)
Red Negative #E53E3E Warning/risk topics (crash, mistakes, bear market)
Amber Warning #FFB800 Neutral caution (market uncertainty, "be careful" content)

Rules:
- Maximum 3 colors per thumbnail (background + text + 1 accent)
- Background is always dark (#0D0B14 or gradient variant)
- Text is always white with dark stroke
- Accent color matches content emotion: orange (authority/brand), teal (positive), red (danger), amber (caution)
- Never use blue (#2404FC) in thumbnails. It reads as generic/corporate and disappears on YouTube's blue UI.

Background Treatments:
1. Solid dark (#0D0B14)
2. Subtle diagonal gradient (#0D0B14 to #1B1925)
3. Radial orange glow behind face (low opacity)
4. Dark with teal or orange edge lighting (thin accent line on one side)

2.5 A/B Testing Framework

Process:
1. Create 2 thumbnail variants per video before upload
2. Upload the primary (best guess) as the default thumbnail
3. After 24 hours, swap to variant B
4. After 48 total hours, compare CTR for each variant
5. Keep the higher-CTR thumbnail as the permanent version

Variables to Test (one at a time):

Variable Option A Option B
Expression Shock/surprise Confidence/smile
Text 3-word headline Different 3-word headline
Color accent Orange underline Teal highlight
Layout Face + Text Number Focus
Face position Right 40% Right 30% (more text space)

Win Criteria:
- Higher CTR after 48 hours wins
- Minimum 1,000 impressions per variant before declaring a winner
- If CTR difference is less than 0.5%, call it a tie and keep the original

Performance Log:
Maintain a spreadsheet or Notion database:
- Video title
- Variant A description + CTR
- Variant B description + CTR
- Winner + why
- Lesson learned

Review the log monthly to identify patterns: which expressions, colors, and layouts consistently win for each content type.

Sarn Elliott

4.5 Mobile Optimization Rules

GAP 6: Content Approval Workflow

6.1 Tier 1: Self-Publish (No Approval Needed)

Content that can be published immediately by any authorized team member:

  • Social media reposts or shares of existing approved content
  • Standard-format posts using approved templates (with no new copy)
  • Market data and price updates (factual only, no directional opinion)
  • Story/Reel crossposts from approved long-form content (clips from published YouTube, podcast)
  • Scheduling posts that have already been approved in a previous review cycle

Self-Publish Rules:
- Must use an approved template from this brand bible
- Must pass the 12-item Brand Voice Audit (see 6.6)
- Factual content only. Zero editorial opinion on price direction.
- If in doubt, escalate to Tier 2

6.2 Tier 2: Team Lead Review (24-Hour Turnaround)

Content that requires review before publishing. Reviewer is the Media Director or Ben.

  • New blog or article drafts
  • Email newsletter drafts (Matt drafts The Weekly Shift, Ben reviews)
  • New social media carousels or educational content
  • Podcast episode descriptions and show notes
  • New video titles and descriptions (YouTube, LinkedIn)
  • Updated landing page copy
  • Any content using a new template not yet in the brand bible

Tier 2 Process:
1. Creator drafts content and moves Asana card to "Review" column
2. Reviewer receives Asana notification
3. Review within 24 hours: approve, request changes, or escalate to Tier 3
4. If approved: move to "Scheduled" column
5. If changes requested: card returns to "In Progress" with reviewer comments

6.3 Tier 3: Ben Approval Required (Before Publish)

Content that must have Ben's explicit sign-off before it goes live:

  • Any content making claims about performance, returns, or portfolio results
  • Member testimonials (anonymization verification required)
  • Pricing, offers, promotional content, or limited-time campaigns
  • Partnership or collaboration announcements
  • Content mentioning specific coins or tokens by name with directional opinion (e.g., "we think SOL will outperform")
  • Press and media responses, interview transcripts, quote approvals
  • Content from guest contributors published under any CS brand
  • Any content that could be interpreted as financial advice
  • Changes to the website hero section, pricing page, or application flow

Tier 3 Process:
1. Content follows Tier 2 process first (Team Lead review)
2. Team Lead escalates to Ben with a note explaining why Tier 3 applies
3. Ben reviews and approves, requests changes, or rejects
4. Only Ben can move Tier 3 content to "Scheduled"
5. No exceptions. No "Ben would probably approve this." Get the approval.

6.4 Tier 4: Legal Review

Content that requires legal counsel or compliance review:

  • Financial disclaimers on any content making performance claims
  • Terms of service changes
  • Privacy policy updates
  • Sponsorship or partnership agreements (if any, which is unlikely given the no-affiliate model)
  • Any content related to AFSL requirements or ASIC compliance
  • Member data use in marketing (even anonymized, review with legal)

Tier 4 Process:
1. Content goes through Tier 3 (Ben approval) first
2. Ben escalates to legal counsel
3. Legal provides written approval or required changes
4. Changes implemented and re-reviewed before publishing
5. Legal-approved content is flagged in Asana with "Legal Approved" tag and date

6.6 Brand Voice Audit Checklist

Run this 12-item check before publishing any Tier 2+ content. If any item fails, the content goes back for revision.

# Check Pass/Fail
1 No em dashes anywhere in the content
2 "Digital assets" used instead of "crypto" (exception: "cryptocurrency" in formal/legal context)
3 No hype language (moon, rocket, alpha, degen, LFG, WAGMI, lambo, 100x, etc.)
4 No income claims, return guarantees, or "you will make money" language
5 Language appropriate for ICP (Strategic Wealth Builder, 35-55, high-net-worth professional)
6 90/10 rule respected (give away 10% of the insight, make them want the other 90%)
7 Correct brand palette used per sub-brand matrix
8 Logo placement correct per brand guidelines
9 Font stack correct (Plus Jakarta Sans headings, Barlow body, no unapproved fonts)
10 CTA matches the approved CTA list
11 Social proof and testimonials properly anonymized (first name + last initial only)
12 Spelling and grammar clean (run spellcheck, read aloud)

Bonus Checks (recommended but not blocking):
- Content passes the "would a successful 50-year-old business owner feel respected reading this?" test
- No more than one CTA per piece (social) or two per piece (email/blog)
- Images use real CS photography, not stock
- Content references a specific Shift OS module or framework where applicable

6.7 Emergency Publishing Protocol (Market Events)

When a significant market event happens and time-sensitive content is needed:

Who Can Emergency Publish:
- Matt Willemsen (market updates, data-only)
- Ben Simpson (any content type)

Emergency Publishing Rules:
1. Content must follow the "factual only" template: report what happened, share relevant data points, no directional opinions on price
2. Use the Market Update story template (see GAP 1, Instagram Story Template D) or a plain-text social post
3. No promotional CTAs in emergency content. This is trust-building, not selling.
4. Flag the published content in Asana after posting for retroactive review
5. Create the Asana card post-publish with:
- "EMERGENCY PUBLISH" tag
- Content link
- Publisher name
- Reason for emergency (e.g., "BTC dropped 20% in 4 hours")
- Time published
6. Ben reviews all emergency publishes within 48 hours

What Does NOT Qualify as Emergency:
- A coin going up 10%. That is not urgent.
- A competitor publishing something. Not an emergency.
- A trending topic on Twitter/X. Interesting, not urgent.
- New regulation announced. Important, but typically has a 24-48 hour window for proper review.

True Emergency Examples:
- Major market crash (20%+ drawdown in 24 hours)
- Exchange collapse or insolvency event
- Critical security vulnerability affecting member assets
- Regulatory action that directly impacts Australian digital asset investors
