Selected: Option 1 - "The one question that separates investors from gamblers"

Most people who buy Bitcoin don't have an answer to a simple question: "What would make you sell?"

That's not a gotcha. It's a diagnostic. Because if you can't answer it, you're not investing. You're hoping. And hope is not a strategy.

I've worked with over 1,100 professionals building digital asset strategies since 2018. Our portfolio has delivered 116% CAGR over 6 financial years, independently verified by Crypto Tax Australia. But returns don't matter if you don't have a plan. The pattern is always the same. Someone buys Bitcoin. The price goes up. They feel smart. The price drops 30%. They panic. They either sell at a loss or hold out of stubbornness, not conviction.

Both outcomes come from the same root cause: no plan.

The question that changes everything

Before you buy anything, before you open an exchange account, before you even decide how much to allocate, answer this: "Under what specific conditions would I sell some or all of this position?"

Not "when it feels right." Not "when I need the money." Specific conditions. Written down.

At Collective Shift, we call this your Sell Strategy. It's Step 8 of the Shift OS framework. And it's consistently the area where our members score lowest on the Digital Asset Readiness Assessment. Average confidence in exit strategy: 2.8 out of 10.

Why this matters more than picking the right coin

Everyone wants to know WHAT to buy. That's the exciting question. But it's the wrong starting point.

The right starting point is: What are my rules?

Consider two investors. Both buy Bitcoin at $50,000.

Investor A has no sell strategy. Bitcoin goes to $100,000. They think it'll go higher. It drops to $60,000. They wish they'd sold at $100,000. It goes back to $85,000. They still don't sell. They're paralysed by uncertainty.

Investor B has a written sell strategy. At $100,000, they take 20% off the table (predetermined rule). At $60,000, they DCA more (predetermined rule). At $85,000, they're calm because they already locked in some gains and have a plan for the next trigger.

Same asset. Same market. Completely different experience.

Three types of sell strategies

Without going into the full framework (that's what the Wealth Protection Blueprint covers), here are the three approaches:

  1. Target-based: Sell specific amounts at specific price targets. Simple, objective, removes emotion.
  2. Time-based: Rebalance on a schedule (quarterly, annually). Ignores short-term noise. Good for long-term holders.
  3. Signal-based: Use market cycle indicators, on-chain data, or technical signals. Requires more knowledge but offers more precision.

Most of our Platinum members use a combination of all three. The important thing isn't which type you choose. It's that you choose one and write it down before the market forces a decision on you.

Your takeaway this week

Open a note on your phone right now. Write the answer to this question: "If Bitcoin hits [X price], I will sell [Y%] of my position." Fill in the blanks. It takes 30 seconds. That 30 seconds of planning is worth more than 30 hours of watching charts.
