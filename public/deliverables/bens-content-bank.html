Ben's Weekly Content Sprint - Process Doc

Overview

Ben produces 21 reels/week (3/day). This process ensures he films efficiently, posts consistently, and drives ManyChat engagement.

Sunday: Batch Film Day (2-3 hours)

Time block: 10am-1pm (protect this slot, nothing moves it)

Prep (before filming):
- Review the evergreen bank and pick 10-15 reels to film
- Check the ideation notes (running list from member questions, sales calls, community feedback)
- Set up filming environment (same location, same equipment, same outfit)
- No decision-making on the day. Everything pre-decided.

Filming:
- Film 10-15 reels in one session
- Each reel: read the hook > deliver the flow > CTA. 30-60 seconds each.
- Second takes are fine but don't overthink. Authentic > polished.
- Raw footage uploaded to Google Drive immediately

Post-filming:
- Clipper edits and adds text overlays, captions
- Clipper queues 3 reels per day for Mon-Sat
- Ben reviews Monday morning and approves the week's batch

Monday-Saturday: Daily Posting (3 reels/day)

Time Type Notes
8am Reel #1 From evergreen batch (scheduled)
12pm Reel #2 From evergreen batch OR topical if news breaks
4pm Reel #3 From evergreen batch (scheduled)

Topical reels: When Bitcoin, regulatory, or market news breaks:
- Ben films a topical reel same-day (or records voice note for Clipper to produce)
- Topical reel replaces one scheduled reel (usually the 12pm slot)
- Topical reels use the same ManyChat CTA framework

ManyChat keyword on every reel:
- Text overlay or caption includes "Comment [KEYWORD] for [thing]"
- Alternate between BLUEPRINT, BUILD, PROTECT, STARTERKIT, PROOF, YIELD
- Aim for 3-4 keyword CTAs per day across Ben's reels

Repurposing: Best Reels to Twitter + LinkedIn

Twitter threads (2-3x per week):
- Take the week's best-performing reel (by engagement)
- Expand the 30-second script into a 5-7 tweet thread
- Thread ends with: "If this resonated, I made a free guide that covers this in depth. Comment BLUEPRINT or grab it here: [link]"
- Post timing: 10am (matches existing tweet schedule)

LinkedIn posts (2-3x per week):
- Same best-performing reels, adapted for LinkedIn format
- Longer narrative form (200-400 words)
- More professional tone, same directness
- Include personal experience or member story where possible
- CTA: "Comment BLUEPRINT below or take the free assessment: [link]"

Weekly Checklist

Sunday:
- [ ] Film 10-15 reels from evergreen bank
- [ ] Upload raw footage to Drive
- [ ] Send to Clipper for editing + scheduling

Monday:
- [ ] Review and approve the week's reel queue
- [ ] Confirm ManyChat keywords are correct on each post
- [ ] Check previous week's ManyChat trigger volume

Daily (Mon-Sat):
- [ ] 3 reels posted (8am, 12pm, 4pm)
- [ ] Monitor comments for ManyChat keyword triggers
- [ ] Film topical reel if news breaks (optional)
- [ ] Repurpose best reel to Twitter/LinkedIn (2-3x per week)

Saturday:
- [ ] Review week's performance (which reels hit, which keywords triggered most)
- [ ] Add new ideas to the ideation bank from the week's member interactions
- [ ] Prep Sunday filming list

Evergreen Bank Management

  • Maintain a minimum buffer of 30 filmed reels at all times
  • When buffer drops below 15, prioritise Sunday filming
  • Retire reels after 90 days (re-film with fresh energy if the concept still works)
  • Track which hooks and keywords perform best, create more of those
  • Add 5-10 new concepts to the bank monthly from member questions and sales calls

Content Pillar Distribution Check

Every month, audit the last 30 days of posts:
- BUILD should be ~40% of total reels
- PROTECT should be ~25%
- PROOF should be ~25%
- YIELD should be ~10%

If any pillar is over/under by more than 10%, adjust the next month's batch filming accordingly.
