Body

Most people start their digital asset journey by asking "What should I buy?"

That's like asking a builder what colour to paint the walls before they've laid the foundation.

At Collective Shift, we've worked with over 1,100 professionals building digital asset strategies. Our portfolio has delivered 116% CAGR over 6 financial years, independently verified by Crypto Tax Australia. But the returns aren't the starting point.

The first thing we do with every member, before we talk about Bitcoin, before we talk about altcoins, before we even mention an exchange, is help them figure out which of the three investor types they are.

The 3 Investor Types

1. The Accumulator
Goal: Build a position over time. Not worried about short-term price moves. Focused on the next 5-10 years. Uses dollar-cost averaging (DCA) religiously.

Best for: People with consistent income who can allocate monthly. Patient. Not checking prices daily.

2. The Strategist
Goal: Build a position with tactical entries. Buys more aggressively during significant dips. Has written rules for when to increase allocation.

Best for: People who understand market cycles and want to optimise their entry points without day-trading.

3. The Compounder
Goal: Grow the portfolio actively. Allocates across Bitcoin and selected altcoins. Rebalances based on market conditions. Has a detailed sell strategy.

Best for: People willing to put in more time and have a higher risk tolerance. Usually has a larger portfolio.

Here's the important part: there's no "right" type. But there IS a wrong type for you. And if you're using the wrong framework, you're either taking too much risk or leaving money on the table.

Most people who come to us are trying to be Compounders when they should be Accumulators. They're watching charts, chasing altcoins, and making emotional decisions because they skipped the foundational step of defining their investor type.

How to figure out your type:

Answer these four questions honestly:
1. How many hours per week can I dedicate to managing my portfolio? (Be honest, not aspirational.)
2. If my portfolio dropped 40% tomorrow, would I buy more, hold, or sell?
3. Do I have a written plan for when I would sell?
4. Is my goal preservation, growth, or aggressive growth?

If you answered "less than 2 hours / hold / no / preservation" then you're an Accumulator. And that's a great thing. Some of our most successful members are Accumulators who DCA into Bitcoin and don't touch anything else.

  • Protect her wealth from inflation and currency debasement
  • Build a generational asset for her children and grandchildren
  • Have an allocation to the single best-performing asset class of the last decade

For those goals, there's no "too late." There's only "I haven't started yet."

Leslie is 4 months into her Platinum membership now. She has a structured portfolio, a sell strategy, and something she didn't have before: conviction. Not blind faith. Conviction built on understanding.

Subject Line Rules

  • Curiosity or specificity (not both)
  • Always write 2 alternatives for A/B testing
