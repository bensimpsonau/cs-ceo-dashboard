How to Use This Checklist

Regardless of your background in traditional investing, digital assets operate differently. Price action is faster, information is noisier, and most investors make decisions without a repeatable process.

This checklist gives you that process.

It covers 3 research areas used by the Collective Shift team when evaluating any digital asset project:

  1. Fundamental Analysis — the substance behind the project
  2. Technical Analysis — price structure and key levels
  3. Sentiment Analysis — market psychology and community signals

There is no single right way to use it. Work through the full list, or focus on the section most relevant to your current research. The goal is a repeatable process — not a one-time read.

01 — Fundamental Analysis

The foundation of any serious allocation decision. These factors tell you whether a project has substance — or just a story.

Project
Understand what the project actually does. Start with the website and whitepaper. Look past buzzwords like "decentralised" and "scalable" — focus on the specific problem being solved.
Industry
Identify the real-world industry the project is targeting. The size of that market tells you the upper bound of potential value if the project succeeds.
Team
Research the founders' professional and educational background. In digital assets, many founding teams come from the same company or institution. Track record matters — especially for projects still in development.
Whitepaper
Read it. If you finish with more questions than you started with, treat that as a warning sign. A strong whitepaper clearly explains the technology, tokenomics, and problem being solved.
Funding
Has the project raised venture capital? Which funds backed it, and at what stage? Strong institutional backing is a meaningful signal — not a guarantee, but worth noting.
Backing
Look for non-equity grants from government-backed entities or corporate accelerators. Projects that have cleared these selection processes have typically been stress-tested beyond what a whitepaper alone reveals.

01 — Fundamental Analysis (continued)

Blockchain Use Case
Why does this project need to exist on a blockchain? If the answer isn't clear in the whitepaper and supported by at least one detailed blog post, that's a gap worth probing.
Token Function
Understand how the token captures value. Work tokens, utility tokens, and base-layer cryptocurrencies (like Bitcoin) operate very differently. Know which category you're investing in.
Independent Research
Search for third-party analyst coverage on the project. YouTube, research platforms, and on-chain analysis tools often surface perspectives the project's own materials won't.
Project Progress
If the project has existed for more than 12 months, review their roadmap and past announcements. Have they hit milestones on time? How did they communicate delays? Execution history reveals team quality.
Upcoming Dates
Know what's coming: mainnet releases, major upgrades, token unlock events. Price volatility typically increases around these dates — for both traders and long-term investors.

02 — Technical Analysis

More commonly associated with traders, technical analysis still provides useful context for investors — particularly around entry timing and identifying key price levels.

Price Structure
Identify the current trend. Is the asset in a confirmed uptrend, downtrend, or ranging? Allocating against the trend without a clear thesis is one of the most common mistakes.
Support and Resistance
Map out the key levels where price has historically reacted. These levels often act as decision points — both for entries and for setting risk boundaries.
Volume Analysis
Price moves with volume behind them carry more weight than those without. Look for volume confirmation on breakouts or breakdowns before acting.
Moving Averages
The 50-day and 200-day moving averages are widely watched. Price position relative to these levels gives a quick read on medium and long-term trend direction.
Relative Strength
How is this asset performing relative to Bitcoin and to the broader market? Assets that outperform in bull markets and hold up in corrections often have stronger fundamental demand.
Chart Timeframes
Check multiple timeframes before making a decision. A setup that looks strong on the daily chart may look very different on the weekly. Alignment across timeframes increases conviction.

03 — Sentiment Analysis

Digital assets lack traditional valuation methodologies. That means sentiment carries more weight in this asset class than most investors expect. These factors are often the difference between a good project and a good investment.

Social Media Presence
Review activity on X (Twitter), Reddit, and Telegram. Look for genuine community engagement rather than volume. A project with 50,000 active, thoughtful followers is more meaningful than one with 500,000 bots.
Developer Activity
Check GitHub commit history. Consistent, substantive commits over time signal an active team. Inactivity — or a sudden spike before a token launch — tells a different story.
News and Media Coverage
Track how the project appears in industry publications. Positive coverage from credible sources builds awareness. Negative coverage — especially around security or regulatory issues — warrants serious scrutiny.
Community Sentiment
Read the actual conversations, not just the headlines. Community forums, Discord servers, and Telegram groups often surface concerns and developments before they appear in official channels.
Fear and Greed Index
The Crypto Fear & Greed Index provides a broad read on market psychology. Extreme fear has historically presented better entry conditions. Extreme greed warrants caution.
Influencer and Analyst Sentiment
Be cautious with this one. Track what credible analysts are saying — not to follow their calls, but to understand what narratives are driving market attention.
