Brand Bible v3.0 - April 2026

The Definitive Brand System for Collective Shift

Collective Shift is the world's most trusted digital asset education company. Built on six years of audited performance (116% CAGR verified by Crypto Tax Australia), zero affiliate deals, and a research-first model that serves over 1,100 members across Australia, the US, UK, and Europe. This Brand Bible is the single source of truth for every piece of content, every pixel, every word that carries the Collective Shift name. It defines who we are, how we sound, how we look, and how we show up across every channel and touchpoint. Founded by Ben Simpson - a high school dropout turned TEDx speaker who nearly went bankrupt twice before building an eight-figure education company - Collective Shift exists because the digital asset industry needed an independent voice. One that puts skin in the game, invests alongside its members, and measures success by how long people stay, not how many people sign up. Every team member, every contractor, every piece of content must honour this standard. If it doesn't pass the test of "would a successful 50-year-old business owner feel respected reading this?" - it doesn't ship.

Section 01

Brand Foundation

The bedrock of everything we build. Our mission, origin, values, and positioning define why Collective Shift exists and who it serves.

Mission

The world's most trusted digital asset education company. We help serious investors build generational wealth through systematic, research-driven frameworks. Free from hype, affiliates, and conflicting interests.

Vision

A world where every serious investor has access to independent, institutional-quality digital asset research - regardless of their starting point. Where the default path to digital asset education is one built on transparency, audited performance, and frameworks that endure across market cycles.

Purpose

To replace noise with clarity. The digital asset industry is flooded with influencers shilling tokens for affiliate commissions, Telegram groups pumping and dumping, and gurus selling hype. Collective Shift exists to be the antidote: independent research, real skin in the game, and education that makes investors smarter - not more dependent.

Core Values

1

Independence Above Everything

Zero affiliates. Zero token promotions. Our only revenue comes from members who stay. When we recommend something, the only reason is because our research supports it.

2

Skin in the Game

We invest our own capital alongside members. Public portfolio. If we're wrong, we lose money too. That alignment changes everything.

3

Education Over Advice

Frameworks over signals. Conviction over dependency. We teach you how to think about digital assets - not what to think.

4

Radical Transparency

Audited returns. Honest when we're wrong. We publish our track record because we believe trust is built through verification, not claims.

5

Long-Term Thinking

Generational wealth over decades, not days. Only 25 trades in 3 years. We play the long game because that's where real wealth is built.

6

Respect for Intelligence

Our audience are successful professionals. We speak to them as peers, not prospects. Every piece of content should make them smarter.

Origin Story - Ben Simpson

Ben Simpson started a sportswear business at 16 in Tasmania. His parents rented. If he wanted anything, he had to work for it. At 17, while sending money to China for his business, a friend introduced him to Bitcoin as a way to bypass bank fees and conversion losses. That moment changed everything.

He went on a six-month deep dive trying to understand digital assets. The information was awful. He couldn't tell Bitcoin from Ethereum. It was the ICO era - a wild west with no trusted guides. He saw Vitalik Buterin speak in Sydney when ETH was $80 and bought in. Everyone thought he was insane.

At 18, he told his parents he was going all in on Bitcoin. His mum, a school teacher, made him a deal: if he couldn't make a salary in one year, he'd go back to school. He never went back.

"I was literally living above a 7-Eleven in Bali. A bed with a toilet. My bookkeeper told me we had weeks of cash left before we went belly up."

- Ben Simpson, Founder

But the journey wasn't linear. Ben made hundreds of thousands on paper in his first cycle - then round-tripped the entire thing. Down 80-90%. No exit strategy. No accountability. No mentor. He then followed a YouTuber's theory that BTC would crash to $100, sold everything, and lost more. That was the turning point: the whole game was backwards. Influencers weren't there for your best interest. They were there for affiliate links.

He teamed up with Matt Willemsen (now Director of Research), and everything changed. But even then, he nearly went bankrupt a second time when a business he was involved in collapsed. He spent months too scared to leave the house. He moved to a room above a 7-Eleven in Bali because he couldn't afford anywhere else.

The comeback came when Ben and Matt got early positioning on metaverse investments before Facebook renamed to Meta. 30-40x returns. Hundreds of thousands back in the bank. And this time, he had a system. An exit strategy. Independent research. Fundamentals over technicals.

He started Collective Shift with a $49/month Facebook group. Turned down hundreds of thousands of dollars from exchanges and advertisers. Built the research framework from scratch. Today: over 1,100 members worldwide. 402 Platinum clients. 116% CAGR audited over six financial years. TEDx speaker. And the goal he set on day one? Buying his parents a house. Done.

Section 02

Brand Personality & Voice

How we sound matters as much as what we say. Every word shapes perception. We're educators, not salespeople. Peers, not gurus.

Brand Archetype: The Sage / Mentor

Collective Shift embodies the Sage archetype - the trusted guide who illuminates the path with knowledge, not the guru who demands followers. We combine the Sage's wisdom and research rigour with the Mentor's personal investment and skin-in-the-game authenticity. We don't lecture from a pedestal. We walk the path alongside our members, investing our own capital, sharing our own mistakes, and building frameworks that work because we use them ourselves.

Voice Principles

Conviction-Driven

"I believe Bitcoin is the best savings technology ever created." We don't hedge with vague language. When we have conviction, we state it clearly. When we don't, we say that too.

Peer Investor

"I invest my own capital alongside every member." We're not on a stage looking down. We're in the arena with our members, managing the same risks, facing the same decisions.

Direct and Honest

"Most of the market is noise. We filter out 98% of it." No fluff. No padding. Say what needs to be said. If we're wrong, say that too.

Educator, Not Guru

"I'll teach you how to think, not what to think." We build frameworks. We don't create dependency. If a member can't make their own decisions after working with us, we've failed.

Empathetic

"I've made every mistake. So you don't have to." We understand the fear, the isolation, the overwhelm. Because we've lived it.

Do This / Not That

Do This

  • "Digital assets" - Professional positioning
  • "Protection, preservation, growth" - ICP fears loss more than they chase gains
  • "Build generational wealth" - Long-term horizon matches ICP goals
  • "Long-term wealth building" - Signals institutional thinking
  • "Education / frameworks" - Legal and brand protection
  • "Investment frameworks / research" - We teach systems, not trades
  • Dashes (-) or full stops - Clean, human writing
  • "Educator / peer investor" - We teach alongside, not above
  • "Members / community" - We build membership, not followers
  • "Bitcoin" (capital B, always)

Not That

  • "Crypto" - Signals retail culture
  • "Returns / gains" - Triggers compliance risk and hype perception
  • "Get rich quick" - Attracts the wrong audience entirely
  • "Moon / lambo / WAGMI" - Instant credibility destroyer
  • "Financial advice" - Legal risk. We educate, we don't advise.
  • "Signals / tips" - Makes us sound like a Telegram group
  • Em dashes (--) - They look like AI-generated text. Never use them.
  • "Guru / expert" - Creates a power dynamic we reject
  • "Followers" - We don't want followers, we want members
  • "Crypto" in any headline or CTA

Forbidden Phrases

Never use these in any Collective Shift content:

crypto (except "cryptocurrency" in legal context) - moon - lambo - WAGMI - LFG - degen - alpha (in trading context) - 100x - guaranteed returns - passive income - get rich quick - financial advice - signals - tips - guru - expert - followers - em dashes

Writing Rules

  • No em dashes. Ever. They look like AI-generated content. Use a dash (-), a comma, or rewrite the sentence.
  • "Digital assets" not "crypto." Exception: "cryptocurrency" in formal or legal contexts. "Bitcoin" is always "Bitcoin" (capital B).
  • No corporate speak. If you wouldn't say it in a conversation with a smart friend, don't write it.
  • Educator tone. Every piece of content should teach something. Not sell something.
  • Left-align body text. Centre only titles and single-line labels.
  • Never ALL CAPS for body text. Uppercase only for labels, tags, and short headings.
  • Two fonts maximum. Plus Jakarta Sans for headings, Barlow for body. No exceptions.
  • No income claims or return guarantees. We share audited historical performance. We never promise future results.
  • Respect the 90/10 rule. Free content teaches the WHAT and the WHY (builds trust). Platinum delivers the HOW (delivers transformation).

The 90/10 Rule

Give Away (The 10%)

  • Why digital assets matter
  • The Bitcoin thesis
  • General allocation principles
  • Exit strategy importance
  • Common mistakes
  • Self-custody fundamentals

Reserve for Platinum (The 90%)

  • Specific allocation models
  • The Shift List
  • Exact entry/exit triggers
  • Portfolio alerts
  • 1:1 coaching
  • WhatsApp community
  • Live events
  • Personalised portfolio reviews

The Ben Test

Before publishing anything, run it through these four questions:

  • Would a successful 50-year-old business owner feel respected reading this?
  • Does this sound like Bloomberg or like a Telegram group?
  • Am I teaching a framework, or am I selling hype?
  • Would I be comfortable if a $5M net worth investor saw this?

Design Principles

Clarity Over Cleverness

Every design element should communicate, not decorate. If it doesn't serve the message, remove it.

Consistent Restraint

60-30-10 colour rule. Two fonts. Generous whitespace. Premium means knowing what to leave out.

Data as Art

Charts, stats, and metrics should be beautiful. Our data is our competitive advantage. Make it visual.

Institutional Trust

Every pixel should feel like it belongs in a wealth management firm, not a Telegram group.

Dark Mode / Light Mode

Default is dark. CS brand is built on dark backgrounds. Dark mode is the primary design context for all social media, video, presentations, and the website.

Light mode is used only for email templates (white background for inbox compatibility) and print materials. When designing for light mode:

  • Use cs-logo-dark.png on white/off-white backgrounds
  • Body text: #555555 on white. Headings: #1B1925 on white.
  • Orange accent stays the same (#FC5C03)
  • CTA blue stays the same (#2404FC)
  • Test in email dark mode to ensure contrast and logo visibility

Engagement & Crisis Guidelines

Video Brand Standards

Intro (3-5 Seconds Max)

  • Cold Open (default for YouTube): Start with the hook (first 5-15 seconds = pure content). Insert branded intro at 30-60 seconds. 3 seconds max.
  • Standard Intro: CS icon mark fades in > brief brand sound (1-2s) > presenter lower third. For LinkedIn, events, clips.
  • Never exceed 5 seconds on a branded intro. Every second costs retention.
  • No animation beyond simple fade/scale. Keep it premium, not flashy.

Outro (10-15 Seconds)

  1. Bridge CTA on camera: "If you found this useful, here's what to do next..." Direct to quiz, Platinum, subscribe, or next video.
  2. End screen zone: Two video suggestion boxes + subscribe button
  3. Brand close: CS logo (white) + collectiveshift.io on dark background. Fade to black.

Lower Thirds

  • 2px top accent line (#FC5C03)
  • Semi-transparent dark bar (rgba(13,11,20,0.85), 64px height)
  • Name: PJS 700, 20px, white. Title: Barlow 400, 14px, #B0B0B0
  • Position: bottom-left. Fade in at 0.5s, hold 5s, fade out.
  • Guests: add company/title on third line. Hold 7 seconds.

Captions / Subtitles

  • Font: Barlow Medium (500), 48-56px (long-form) or 56-64px (short-form)
  • White text with dark shadow. Centre-bottom for long-form, centre for short-form.
  • Keyword highlighting: 1-2 key words per block in orange (#FC5C03)
  • Max 2 lines, 42 characters per line

On-Screen Graphics

  • Stat callouts: Number in PJS 800, 72-96px, #FC5C03. Dark card container (#1E1C28). Number counts up from 0 over 1s.
  • Charts: Use brand chart colour sequence. Dark background. Source attribution always.
  • Quote cards: 4px left bar in #FC5C03. Dark card. Barlow 500 Italic, 24px.
  • Chapter markers: Full-screen dark, section label in PJS 700, #FC5C03 uppercase. 2-3 seconds.

A/B Testing Framework

  1. Create 2 thumbnail variants per video before upload
  2. Upload the primary (best guess) as the default
  3. Swap to variant B after 24 hours
  4. Compare CTR after 48 total hours (minimum 1,000 impressions per variant)
  5. Keep the higher-CTR thumbnail. If difference is less than 0.5%, keep original.

Maintain a log: video title, variant descriptions, CTRs, winner, lesson learned. Review monthly to identify patterns.

Content Approval Workflow

Tier 1: Self-Publish (No Approval Needed)

  • Reposts of existing approved content
  • Standard template posts with no new copy
  • Factual market data updates (no directional opinion)
  • Clips from published YouTube/podcast

Must use an approved template and pass the 12-item Brand Voice Audit. If in doubt, escalate.

Tier 2: Team Lead Review (24-Hour Turnaround)

  • New blog/article drafts, email newsletter drafts, carousels, video titles/descriptions
  • New social media content with original copy
  • Updated landing page copy
  • Any content using a new template not yet in the brand bible

Process: Creator moves Asana card to "Review" and tags reviewer. Reviewer approves, requests changes, or escalates within 24 hours.

Tier 3: Ben Approval Required (Before Publish)

  • Performance claims, return stats, member testimonials
  • Pricing, promotions, limited-time campaigns
  • Specific coins with directional opinion
  • Press/media responses, interview transcripts
  • Content from guest contributors
  • Anything that could be interpreted as financial advice
  • Changes to website hero, pricing page, or application flow

No exceptions. No "Ben would probably approve this." Get the approval.

Tier 4: Legal Review

  • Financial disclaimers on performance claims
  • Terms of service or privacy policy changes
  • AFSL or ASIC compliance content
  • Member data use in marketing (even anonymised)

Emergency Publishing Protocol

Who can emergency publish: Matt Willemsen (data-only) or Ben Simpson (any content).

  • Factual only. No directional opinions on price.
  • No promotional CTAs. Trust-building, not selling.
  • Flag in Asana within 48 hours with "EMERGENCY PUBLISH" tag.

What qualifies as emergency: Major market crash (20%+ drawdown), exchange collapse, critical security vulnerability, regulatory action directly impacting Australian digital asset investors.

A coin going up 10%, a competitor publishing something, or a trending topic on Twitter/X are NOT emergencies.

Brand Voice Audit Checklist

Run this 12-item check before publishing any Tier 2+ content. If any item fails, the content goes back for revision.

  • No em dashes anywhere in the content
  • "Digital assets" used instead of "crypto" (exception: "cryptocurrency" in formal/legal context)
  • No hype language (moon, rocket, alpha, degen, LFG, WAGMI, lambo, 100x)
  • No income claims, return guarantees, or "you will make money" language
  • Language appropriate for ICP (Strategic Wealth Builder, 35-55, HNW professional)
  • 90/10 rule respected (give away the what/why, reserve the how for Platinum)
  • Correct brand palette used per sub-brand matrix
  • Logo placement correct per brand guidelines
  • Font stack correct (Plus Jakarta Sans headings, Barlow body, no unapproved fonts)
  • CTA matches the approved CTA list
  • Social proof and testimonials properly anonymised (first name + last initial only)
  • Spelling and grammar clean (run spellcheck, read aloud)

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Design Mistakes

  • Using stock photography or AI-generated faces
  • Adding blockchain iconography (floating coins, candlestick charts)
  • Breaking the 60-30-10 colour rule
  • Using more than 2 fonts
  • Neon, glitch, or "futuristic" visual effects
  • Logo on busy backgrounds without clear space
  • ALL CAPS body text

Copy Mistakes

  • Using em dashes (the #1 tell of AI writing)
  • Saying "crypto" instead of "digital assets"
  • Hype language (moon, lambo, alpha, WAGMI)
  • Income claims or return promises
  • Calling Ben a "guru" or "expert"
  • Using "followers" instead of "members"
  • Hard-selling in educational content
  • Inventing new CTA labels without approval

Legal Notes

  • ABN: All published content must include Collective Shift Pty Ltd ABN in the footer or legal section
  • Disclaimers: Required on any content with performance claims. Past performance is not indicative of future results.
  • Testimonials: Anonymise (first name + last initial only) unless explicit written permission. Usage requires Tier 3 approval.
  • No financial advice. We provide education and frameworks. All investment decisions are the individual's responsibility.
  • No income claims. We share audited historical performance. We never promise future returns.
  • Member data: Even anonymised data in marketing requires legal review (Tier 4).
  • "Bitcoin" always capitalised. "Cryptocurrency" acceptable in legal context only.
