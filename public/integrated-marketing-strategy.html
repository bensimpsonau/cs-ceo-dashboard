03 / Pixel Conditioning Strategy

State $14,999 Everywhere. This Is the #1 Fix.

Jeremy Haynes' Pixel Conditioning framework (Skill #27) may be the single most important concept for CS. When you state the price, unqualified people bounce. That bounce is a SIGNAL to Meta: "Do not find more people like me." When qualified HNW investors see the price and book, that is a signal: "Find more people like me." Over 30-60 days, the pixel retrains on affluent buyers.

The #1 question to audit right now: Is $14,999 stated clearly in the VSL? On the landing page? In pre-call communications? If the answer to any of these is "no," fix it this week. This is the highest-leverage single change CS can make. Accept the 2-4 week conditioning window of fewer calls. The calls that come through after conditioning are pre-sold, price-aware, and ready to buy.
How Pixel Conditioning Works
PhaseWhat HappensDurationAction
Week 1-2Call volume drops 20-40%. Unqualified leads stop booking. This is NORMAL and EXPECTED.14 daysDo not panic. Do not remove the price. Do not reduce ad spend. Monitor call quality, not quantity.

Where $14,999 Must Appear
  • VSL: "Platinum membership is $14,999 per year"
  • Landing page: Clear price on page, not hidden
  • Pre-call email: "This programme is $14,999/year"
  • Confirmation page: Price reinforcement
  • Lead magnet bridge: "Our Platinum programme ($14,999/year)"

What Happens Without Pixel Conditioning
  • Meta finds people who click but cannot afford $15K
  • Show rates stay at 40-50% (tire-kickers)
  • Close rates stay at 15-20% (price shock on call)
  • Cost per Platinum stays high ($3-5K instead of $2.1K)
  • Salespeople burn out fighting unqualified leads
  • Pixel reinforces the wrong audience with every dollar spent
Why HNW investors EXPECT price transparency: Your ICP is 35-60, $1M+ net worth, $200K+ income. These people buy $15K services regularly (financial advisors, accountants, business coaches). Hiding the price signals you are a scam. Being upfront about $14,999 signals confidence, professionalism, and respect for their intelligence. It is "rich-person messaging" (Jeremy Haynes, Competitive Advantages Skill #5). The price IS the qualification filter.

05 / Organic Content Strategy

Hidden VSSL, What Not How, Big Topic, 90/10

Jeremy Haynes' organic content framework has four pillars. Every piece of organic content Ben creates must follow these principles. The content IS the marketing. There is no separate "content strategy" and "marketing strategy." They are the same thing.

1. Hidden VSSL Framework (Skill #14)

Content that sells without triggering the "this person is selling me" response. Perfect for HNW investors who are allergic to hard sells.

Structure: Educational content that naturally leads to "and if you want us to build this for you, that is what Platinum is."

Example: "The 3 Biggest Tax Mistakes HNW Digital Asset Investors Make." Content is genuine education on tax optimisation. Natural bridge: "Our Platinum members get personalised tax strategy as part of their membership. If you want this done for your specific situation..."
3 Hidden VSSLs to create now:
  • Tax mistakes HNW digital asset investors make
  • Portfolio allocation errors (concentrated vs diversified)
  • "What the wealthy actually do with digital assets"
2. What, Not How (90/10 System)

Tell them WHAT to do. Not HOW to do it in granular detail. The "how" is what they pay $14,999 for.

Give 10% of what you know for free. That 10% is still more valuable than what most people share. The remaining 90% creates a gap that makes them want to pay for Platinum.

Example: "You need a digital asset allocation framework with these 5 components." (What.) Platinum teaches them how to build each component for their specific situation, with personalised guidance. (How.)
3. Big Topic Strategy

Every video, every post, every email ties back to one overarching theme. For CS, that theme is: "Build the infrastructure for your digital asset wealth."

Do not scatter across random topics. Do not chase trending altcoins. Do not react to daily price moves unless they connect to the infrastructure thesis. The Big Topic is the filter for all content decisions.

Big Topic for CS: "Professional digital asset infrastructure for high-net-worth investors." Every piece of content must serve this. If it does not, do not publish it.
4. Big Claims Attract Bigger Buyers

Conservative claims for conservative money. But make them SPECIFIC and BOLD within the education framing.

Not: "Make 100x returns." Instead: "Our members built institutional-grade digital asset portfolios." Not: "Get rich with Bitcoin." Instead: "The top 1% of Australian investors have already positioned. Here is the framework they used."

Ben's natural voice: Direct, authoritative, certain. No filler. Data-backed. This is already correct. Double down on it.

A-Player Hiring

Setters and closers who understand HNW psychology. Not crypto bros. Sophisticated financial professionals who happen to understand digital assets. The compounding effect: Offer ownership creates unique positioning. Pixel conditioning ensures the right people see it. Rich-person messaging speaks their language.
