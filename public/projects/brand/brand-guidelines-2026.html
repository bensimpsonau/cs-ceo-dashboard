Section 01

Brand Identity

Who we are, what we stand for, and who we serve.

Mission

Collective Shift empowers Bitcoin and digital asset investors with professional-grade, systematic intelligence — the research, tools, and community that institutional investors have always had, now accessible to the individual.

Brand Positioning

Collective Shift is a long-term, research-driven membership for serious investors. Bitcoin-native. No affiliates. No advertising. No token promotions. Trust-based, member-funded. Institutional grade, individual access.

Section 07

Photography & Imagery

Visual direction that reflects premium wealth management, not crypto culture.

Direction

Our visual style is professional, warm, and elevated. Think private wealth advisory — not crypto tech startup. Every image should feel like it belongs in a boardroom, not a crypto meetup.

✅ Use
  • Professional, warm lighting
  • Clean, uncluttered compositions
  • Wealth management aesthetic — boardrooms, consultations
  • Real team photos (Google Drive photo library)
  • Charts and data visualisations (Bloomberg / McKinsey style)
  • Confident, understated elegance
  • White / light backgrounds for content imagery
❌ Avoid
  • Crypto bro / hype aesthetics
  • Neon lights, dark futuristic scenes
  • Generic stock photos (handshakes, suits, laptops)
  • Gaming / tech startup vibes
  • Loud, flashy graphics
  • Overly dark compositions for general content
  • Any imagery that "codes" as crypto conference booth
Icon Style

Use filled vector icons (Material Design set). Solid fill matching #02000D on light or #FFFFFF on dark. Circular icon containers. No outlined/stroked icons.

Section 08

Voice & Tone

How Collective Shift sounds — across every channel and touchpoint.

Writing Principles

Ben is a peer investor — not a guru. He's in the trades alongside members. The voice is direct, conviction-driven, warm yet authoritative. Data-backed, never hype-driven.

Tone by Channel

ChannelTone
EmailPersonal, direct, slightly conversational — like a letter from a trusted peer
Social (X / Instagram)Punchy, conviction-heavy, opens with bold statement or data point
Sales pagesProblem-aware → aspiration → solution. Never cheesy or desperate.
Slides / PresentationsClean, declarative statements. Data > fluff. No bullet-point overload.
Community (WhatsApp)Warm, human, high-signal — no noise
Scripts / WebinarsEducator mode: explain the why, not just the what

Brand Language

✅ Use These Phrases
  • "Cut through the noise"
  • "Systematic intelligence"
  • "Generational wealth"
  • "Financial sovereignty"
  • "The Silent Thief" (inflation)
  • "Professional-grade" / "Institutional-grade"
  • "No affiliates. No advertising."
  • "By Application Only" (Platinum)
  • "Done-for-you research"
  • "We invest our own capital alongside members"
❌ Never Use
  • "To the moon" / "moon season"
  • "WAGMI" / "NGMI" / crypto bro slang
  • "Get rich quick" (even implicitly)
  • "Guaranteed returns"
  • "We recommend" (legal risk)
  • "Pump" or "Altcoin casino"
  • "Unlock your potential" / generic motivation
  • "Sign up now!" / "Buy today!"
  • "Game-changer" / "World-class"

Voice Examples

✅ On-Brand
Email subject: "Why 74% of investors have no exit strategy"
Social headline: "The institutional window is closing. Here's what that means for your allocation."
CTA: "Apply for Platinum" / "Reserve your seat"
Social proof: "350 high-net-worth members, $100K–$35M+ portfolios"
Risk framing: "Volatility is the price of asymmetric returns. Here's how to manage it."
❌ Off-Brand
Email subject: "🚀 Don't miss this HUGE opportunity!!!"
Social headline: "Bitcoin is going to $1M. Are you ready??"
CTA: "Sign up now!" / "Buy today!"
Social proof: "Thousands of happy customers!"
Risk framing: "This is a sure thing. Don't miss out."

Section 09

Presentation Standards

Ben's standing design orders for all decks, slides, and visual materials.

Design Direction

White backgrounds for content. Dark for hero moments only. The audience associates light themes with finance, wealth management, and trust — where they feel at home. Dark themes code as tech, gaming, and crypto — where they're uncomfortable.

Slide Background Rules

Slide TypeBackgroundNotes
Content slides#FFFFFF or #F9F9F9Light mode — default for most slides
Hero / Title#02000DDark — for dramatic opening moments only
Section intro#1B1925Dark dividers between content blocks
Highlight / CTA#FC5C03Blaze Orange — very sparingly
Data slides#FFFFFFLight preferred — charts should be the focus

Chart Styling

✅ Do
  • Charts should be BIGGER — full-width or near-full-width
  • Professional financial chart aesthetic (Bloomberg, McKinsey)
  • Use charts as focal points, not supporting elements
  • Light backgrounds for data readability
  • One metric per data callout
❌ Don't
  • Don't squeeze charts into corners
  • Don't use multiple competing charts on one slide
  • Don't use dark backgrounds for data slides
  • Don't use small text below 14px
  • Don't overload slides with bullet points
