1. Voice & Content DNA Analysis

Ben's Authentic Voice Profile

Built from analysis of X/Twitter posts, WhatsApp messages (1,699 messages), 18 YouTube videos, and Ben's origin story vlog. This is how Ben actually communicates, not how someone thinks he should.

Core Voice Characteristics

Short. Punchy. No fluff. 46% of Ben's messages are under 30 characters. He sends thoughts as separate messages, not walls of text. Fragments are fine. Complete sentences are optional. The rhythm matters more than the grammar.

"18 months as a nomad. Solo. 8 countries. One bag and a laptop." 82K views, 145 likes, 38 bookmarks. Ben's highest-performing post.

Contrarian framing with data to back it. Ben doesn't say "Bitcoin is good." He says "The less Bitcoin you hold, the MORE risk you carry." He flips the expected narrative. But unlike most contrarians, he brings data. Every opinion has a receipt.

"Conservative (lowest risk): 100% Bitcoin. The less Bitcoin you hold, the more risk you carry." 10 likes. Low reach but perfect voice. This IS Ben.

Personal vulnerability combined with conviction. Ben shares the losses, the near-bankruptcy, the 7-Eleven apartment in Bali. Then he connects it to the system he built. The vulnerability creates trust. The conviction creates authority. Together they create the parasocial relationship that converts followers to Platinum members.

"Years of early mornings, late nights, saying no to a lot of things so I could eventually say yes to stuff like this. No agenda. No meetings. Just exploring somewhere new with my favourite people. This is what it was all for." Personal story from Vietnam family trip. This format = growth engine.

Australian but globally accessible. "Mate" is the default address (83x in exec messages). "Yep" not "yeah." "Keen" for interested. "Nah" for no. But the language never excludes the US or UK audience.

Family and freedom as the "why." Every piece of personal content connects back to family, freedom, or the journey from nothing. "Buying my parents a house" is the north star. This is what separates Ben from every other digital asset educator. They talk about money. Ben talks about what money is for.

The "50x Personal Story" Playbook

  1. Lead with personal, land on framework. Start with a personal angle ("I nearly went bankrupt twice"). Transition to the lesson ("That's why I built the exit strategy framework"). End with the CTA.
  2. The 3:1 ratio. For every 3 personal/story reels, post 1 pure educational reel. Personal gets reach. Educational gets saves.
  3. Anchor every story to an ICP pain point. Nomad life = freedom. Near-bankruptcy = no exit strategy. Buying parents a house = generational wealth.
  4. Recycle the stories. Ben has 5-7 core stories. Tell them from different angles. New followers haven't heard them.

Voice Do's and Don'ts

Sounds Like Ben

  • "Short punchy sentences. Fragments are fine."
  • "The less Bitcoin you hold, the more risk you carry." (Contrarian flip)
  • "Yep." / "100%." / "Nah." (Single-word conviction)
  • "This is what it was all for." (Personal purpose statement)
  • "That's not a [X] problem. That's a [Y] problem." (Reframe)
  • "Digital assets" (never "crypto" in public content)
  • Real numbers. Specific data. Named members (with permission).
  • "I reckon" / "mate" / "keen" (natural Australian)

Does NOT Sound Like Ben

  • Em dashes. Ever. They look like AI.
  • "Crypto" (always "digital assets")
  • Hype language: "moon," "100x," "bull run incoming"
  • Formal: "I hope this finds you well"
  • Corporate: "synergies," "leverage," "circle back"
  • Hedging: "perhaps," "it might be worth considering"
  • Paragraphs. Ben writes bursts, not essays.
  • Income claims or guaranteed returns

FormulaStructureExample

Labelling"If you're doing [action], you're not [what they think]. You're [reframe].""If you're trading without a tax strategy, you're not investing. You're donating."

Content Recycling Rules

  • 30-day rule: Any reel can be re-posted after 30 days with a new hook or angle. Most followers won't see the original.
  • Cross-platform same day is fine: Post the same reel on IG, TikTok, and Shorts on the same day. Different audiences.
  • Evergreen means evergreen: Content in this library never expires. Rotate through the full bank every 3-4 months.
  • Top performers get reshot: If a reel hits 50K+ views, reshoot it with a tighter hook and post it again in 2-3 weeks.
  • Client result posts are always fresh: New screenshots, new quotes, new anonymised stories every week.

Weekly Review Process

Friday Review (30 min, Media Director)

  1. Pull the numbers: Views, saves, ManyChat triggers, DMs for the week.
  2. Identify top 3 performers: What was the hook? What pillar? What format? What time?
  3. Identify bottom 3: Why did they underperform? Wrong hook? Wrong time? Wrong format?
  4. Score each piece: Reach + Engagement + Conversion + Brand Alignment.
  5. Decision: Top performers get reshot/repurposed. Bottom performers get retired or reworked.
  6. Next week: Confirm theme, confirm content IDs, confirm filming brief.

Monthly Review Process

Monthly Review (60 min, Ben + Media Director)

  1. Content performance trends: Which pillars performed best? Which formats? Which hooks?
  2. Funnel metrics: ManyChat triggers > Blueprint completions > Calls booked > Platinum conversions.
  3. Audience growth: Follower growth by platform. Email list growth. Unsubscribe rate.
  4. Pillar rebalancing: Adjust pillar distribution if one is overperforming or underperforming.
  5. New concepts: Add 5-10 new content ideas from member questions, sales calls, and trending topics.
  6. Calendar update: Adjust next month's theme weeks based on data.

Do This / Not That

Do This

  • Film 10-15 reels on Sunday in one session
  • Use the first take (overthinking kills authenticity)
  • End every reel with a natural keyword CTA
  • Post client results 3x/week (screenshots, raw language)
  • Repurpose YouTube into 3 reels per video
  • Send plain text emails from Ben's inbox
  • Use one bio link: collectiveshift.co/cryptoblueprint
  • Lead with personal stories, land on frameworks
  • Track ManyChat triggers and DMs weekly
  • Review top 3 and bottom 3 performers every Friday

Not That

  • Film one reel at a time throughout the week
  • Polish content until it sounds scripted
  • Skip the CTA because "it feels salesy"
  • Use polished testimonial graphics (raw = real)
  • Let YouTube videos die without extracting reels
  • Send designed HTML emails (plain text from Ben converts better)
  • Use Linktree or multi-link tools
  • Lead with news commentary (gets <1K views)
  • Publish without tracking
  • Skip the Friday review
