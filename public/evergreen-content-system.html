5. Content Creation Playbook

Ben's Hook Formula

Reverse-engineered from the top 15 performing posts across X and Instagram. Three hook structures that consistently outperform:

Hook Type 1: The Contrarian Flip

Take an accepted truth and invert it. Works because it creates cognitive dissonance.

[Accepted truth]. [Dramatic pause or full stop]. [Contrarian reframe]. EXAMPLE: "The less Bitcoin you hold, the MORE risk you carry." EXAMPLE: "The biggest risk in digital assets isn't volatility. It's doing nothing." EXAMPLE: "Owning 30 digital assets isn't diversification. It's confusion with a spreadsheet."

Hook Type 2: The Specific Number

Open with a specific, surprising data point. Works because specificity signals authority.

[Specific number]. [Context that makes it shocking]. [Implication]. EXAMPLE: "116% CAGR over 6 financial years. Audited. 25 trades total." EXAMPLE: "Bitcoin has been declared dead 477 times. It's outperformed every other asset." EXAMPLE: "80% of US dollars were printed after 2020. Let that sink in."

Hook Type 3: The Personal Confession

Open with vulnerability. Works because it creates parasocial intimacy at scale.

[Personal admission]. [Emotional context]. [Transition to lesson]. EXAMPLE: "I made hundreds of thousands on paper. Then I lost nearly all of it." EXAMPLE: "18 months as a nomad. Solo. 8 countries. One bag and a laptop." EXAMPLE: "I grew up in a rental house in Tasmania."

"Do This / Not That" for Ben's Voice

Do This

  • "Short punchy sentences."
  • "I reckon most people get this wrong."
  • "Here's what actually happened."
  • "That's not a market problem. That's a planning problem."
  • "Yep." / "100%." / "Nah."
  • Start with a story. End with a lesson.
  • Use real member names (with permission).
  • Admit mistakes openly. Show the losses.
  • Write at a 7th grade reading level.
  • Use "and" to start sentences.

Not That

  • "In this comprehensive guide, we'll explore..."
  • "It is important to consider the implications..."
  • "The synergies between these approaches..."
  • Em dashes. Anywhere. For any reason.
  • "Crypto" (always "digital assets")
  • "To the moon" / "LFG" / "WAGMI"
  • "Dear valued reader"
  • Passive voice throughout
  • Paragraphs longer than 3 sentences
  • "Perhaps you might want to consider..."
