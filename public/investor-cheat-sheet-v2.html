HOW TO SELL IT

Framing, language, and the B2B expansion story

Framing Shifts That Close

1. Lead With the Gap, Not the Product

Don't open with "we built a platform." Open with the gap.

"The information layer for digital assets doesn't exist yet. We're building it."

This positions you as inevitable, not optional.

2. Make the ROAS the Headline

Investors hear "growth" all day. They rarely hear predictable, repeatable growth.

"We spend $1 and get $3.18 back. Every month. For 8 months. That's not a spike. That's a machine."

3. Anchor on the US Expansion

"We built a $5.2M business in a country of 26 million. The US is 330 million, with higher willingness to pay. We're already seeing it in early campaigns."

This makes the $2M feel small relative to the opportunity.

4. Use the Bloomberg Comp Hard

Bloomberg is $12B/year. Everyone in the room knows what it is.

"Bloomberg charges $25K/year per terminal for traditional finance. We charge $15K/year for digital assets. The difference? Our market is growing 30% a year."

5. Create Urgency Around Timing

"Digital assets just crossed 580M holders. ETFs are live. The next 2-3 years will define who owns this category. We're 3 years ahead."

6. The AI Moat

"Every piece of research we've ever produced trains our model. A competitor needs 5 years of proprietary data to replicate what we have today."

7. Handle "Why So Small a Raise?"

"We don't need $20M. We need $2M to scale what's already working. That discipline is why we got to $5.2M with minimal outside capital."
Do NOT say: "crypto" (say "digital assets") | Don't oversell AI as futuristic (it's live today) | Don't apologise for being Australian (frame it: "we built this in a small market on purpose") | Don't lead with the team slide (lead with the market gap and numbers)
