The 2-Track System & 8-Step Strategy Process

How We Got Here

The 2-track system wasn't designed in a boardroom — it was reverse-engineered from member reality. Over the past three months we have:

Phase 3 · B3 & A3 — The Core Deliverable

The 8-Step Strategy Process

Pre-recorded course, self-paced, integrated into weekly coaching calls. Members complete one step per call and build their full written strategy. Culminates in a 1:1 analyst session. This is the product's core intellectual value proposition.

STEP 01
Define Your Goals
Wealth generation, protection, or income?
STEP 02
Assess Your Risk Profile
Both directions — upside AND downside
STEP 03
Build Your Investment Thesis
Your conviction — not ours to follow blindly
STEP 04
Your Portfolio Allocation
BTC core / digital asset satellites / cash reserve
STEP 05
Create Your Buy Strategy
DCA mechanics, automation, entry framework
STEP 06
Plan Your Tax Approach
CGT events, record-keeping, entity structure
STEP 07
Secure Your Assets
Self-custody pathway, hardware wallet, estate
STEP 08
Write Your Sell Strategy
Pre-committed, written — not a vague intention
"A strategy in your head is not a strategy. It's a vague intention." — Shift OS Core Philosophy

Education Call Analysis

Source: 4 education call log files (edu_0–edu_3), Jan–Mar 2026. Period context: Bitcoin corrected $120K+ → ~$75–80K, altcoin bloodbath. This downturn dominates every single call.

Top 10 Member Questions — Ranked by Frequency

  • #1
    "Should I buy now or wait?" / Market timing
    Asked in virtually every single call. "Do I start buying a thousand dollars of Bitcoin each week, or do I wait another month?" — Nigel
  • #2
    "What should I do with my altcoins?" — sell vs hold in a downturn
    80%+ of calls. "Do I basically sell out a portion of XRP, take a $2,000 hit on Solana, and come out net?" — Sunit
  • #3
    "How much should I allocate to Bitcoin vs altcoins?"
    Nearly every portfolio call. "My mentality is 50% higher risk and 50% Bitcoin" — Ryan (Simon recalibrates)
  • #4
    "How do I learn self-custody / cold wallets?"
    ~50% of calls, especially new members. Harry: "SwiftX is blocking withdrawals of my BTC to my cold wallet"
  • #5
    "What about stablecoin yields / DeFi / passive income?"
    ~40% of calls. Nigel: "I didn't even know the terminology... the USDC where they put money in and get a return"
  • #6
    "Is XRP a good investment?"
    ~60% of calls. Almost every member holds XRP and is questioning it. The #1 gateway question.
  • #7
    "What about Hyperliquid / HYPE?"
    Growing topic. Simon consistently flags HYPE as standout — real product-market fit, real revenue, bucking the trend.
  • #8
    "How do I deal with banks blocking transfers?"
    ~30% of calls. Simon: "I've had my accounts frozen by Commonwealth Bank." Universal outside Dubai.
  • #9
    "Should I sell Bitcoin? Does CS ever sell Bitcoin?"
    Harry: "Do you actually ever sell your Bitcoin?" Answer: "Our general strategy is not selling Bitcoin. We very rarely sell Bitcoin."
  • #10
    "Can I earn yield on Bitcoin?"
    Harry: "I wanna explore some earning potential through owning it." Simon: "You cannot earn a yield on Bitcoin. It's not a yielding product."

Critical Knowledge Gaps

Altcoins ≠ Stocks		 80%+ of members treat altcoins like equities. No understanding of equity ownership absence, vetting process, or mortality rates. Step 3 (Altcoins) — the "Tech Startups, Not Stocks" reframe is the highest-leverage teaching moment in the product
HIGH
Self-custody fundamentals		 Nigel, Harry, Dina: terrified of seed phrases, don't understand hot vs cold wallets, 100% exchange-exposed. Harry: exchange blocking BTC withdrawals. Step 7 (Secure Your Assets) — the self-custody pathway from L1 is the #1 unmet educational need

DeFi & yield basics		 Members hear "6% on USDC" but have zero understanding of mechanics, counterparty risk, or how to access L2 content only — gate must hold. This misunderstanding is dangerous if members try before they're ready

DCA execution gap		 Members understand DCA intellectually but don't execute. Emotional barriers prevent action despite understanding the logic. Step 5 (Buy Strategy) — automation setup is the fix. Remove the decision entirely.
MED
Bank friction / on-ramping		 Every new member hits this wall. Banks freeze accounts, block transfers. Members don't know how to handle it. Pre-recorded how-to is sufficient. This is table stakes onboarding content.
MED
When to act vs "be patient"		 John/Johannes explicitly said the "patience" advice from Circle "wasn't sufficient." Members hear "wait" but don't know what the trigger to act looks like. Step 8 (Sell Strategy) + written buy triggers in the workbook. Patience needs objective conditions attached.
LOW
Market cycles		 New members have zero reference for bear/bull transitions. Stacey, Nigel, Greg needed historical context to avoid panic. Step 1 (Foundations) covers this — low urgency for product, high urgency for content
What Members Love ✅
  • Weekly forums/roundtable — Simon + Matt dynamic praised repeatedly. "I like your bullshit approach."
  • 1-on-1 calls — Perceived as massive value. Members feel heard and get personalised frameworks.
  • Analyst honesty — Direct about losses, risks, and uncertainties. Explicitly cited as differentiator.
  • Dashboard — Members use and update it before calls. Functional and referenced.
  • Educational philosophy — Long-term, patient, Bitcoin-first. Resonates strongly with target demographic.
  • Simon's 2026 Outlook post — Referenced in multiple calls. Members want a written, reasoned thesis with price targets.
What's Missing / Could Improve 🔧
  • Beginner onboarding gap — Nigel: "I'm a dead set novice." The gap between joining and competence is large and unstructured.
  • Self-custody pathway — Interest but overwhelmed. No clear step-by-step guided path exists today.
  • Community intimidation — Nigel: "I feel like an idiot just being new and posting." New members afraid to ask questions in Circle.
  • Platform content discovery — Simon: "A lot of members don't jump onto the platform." Content exists but isn't being found.
  • Re-entry framework — John asked directly. No structured framework — it's ad hoc advice per call today.
  • Pre-recorded how-tos — Dee confirmed: members want self-paced video modules for practical setup steps.

5 Content Pillars

Bitcoin & Digital Asset Strategy
The systematic, framework-driven approach to investing. Not speculation — strategy. Covers mechanics of building and managing a digital asset portfolio with discipline. HNW investors don't want tips — they want a system.

Why existing wealth needs digital asset exposure — from a preservation-first lens. Inflation hedging, real estate risks, traditional portfolio vulnerabilities, generational wealth. Primary fear of the ICP: losing what they've built.

Ben's personal portfolio updates, honest stories about mistakes, no-affiliate commitment, member results, myth-busting. No polish, no agenda — just truth. When Ben shows his actual portfolio and owns his mistakes, it creates the parasocial trust that converts viewers to Platinum.

The Digital Asset Education Gap
Why most "crypto" content is garbage. How sophisticated investors are being underserved. What a real framework looks like vs tips, signals, and shilling. ICP has already tried the other stuff — this pillar validates their frustration and positions Ben as the antidote.
