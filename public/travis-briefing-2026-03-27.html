2.4 The 8-Step Strategy Process (Phase 3)

This is the culminating exercise for both Level 1 and Level 2. Members complete a written strategy document across 8 steps — one per coaching call. "A strategy in your head is not a strategy. It's a vague intention."

# Step What the Member Defines L1 Scope L2 Scope
01 Define Your Goals Time horizon, capital targets, life goals tied to portfolio Retirement, property, income targets Multi-entity goals, generational wealth
02 Assess Your Risk Profile Wealth Generation, Protection, or Income Creation Basic risk tolerance & capital sizing Drawdown budgeting, portfolio-level risk
03 Build Your Investment Thesis Why you hold each asset — written, first-principles Bitcoin conviction + selective digital assets Full thesis including DeFi, RWA, altcoin categories
04 Your Portfolio Allocation BTC / altcoin / stablecoin split with rationale CEX-only. Portfolio Allocation Calculator tool. Advanced split including DeFi, yield, multi-entity
05 Create Your Buy Strategy DCA amounts, frequency, automation setup Exchange DCA + lump sum decision framework Advanced entries including DCA-back-in after selling
06 Plan Your Tax Approach Record keeping, software, accountant briefing CGT basics, Koinly/CTC setup, 12-month rule Entity structures, tax-loss harvesting, DeFi events
07 Secure Your Assets Security tier matched to portfolio size Hardware wallet + seed phrase backup Multi-sig, passphrase, estate planning
08 Write Your Sell Strategy Written, pre-committed exit rules — targets, triggers, types Target-based / DCA-out / trailing stops Multi-tier ladders, cycle-aware exits, borrowing alternative

Exit strategy is the ICP's #1 pain point. The Round-Tripper is the dominant prospect type. Step 8 is the most emotionally resonant module — and the most powerful marketing hook. Content built around Step 8 (sell strategy) converts the highest-intent prospects.

Appendix · Reference

Simon's 10 Teaching Frameworks

Battle-tested explanations from 30+ member education calls. Every analyst, content creator, and sales team member should use these consistently. They work because they're real, specific, and memorable.

📌 Usage Instruction for Travis

These frameworks are the most effective content assets CS has. They are reusable across all channels: YouTube scripts, community posts, sales calls, CSM outreach, email sequences. Templatise them. Build a library. Use them everywhere.

  • 1
    Altcoins = Tech Startups, Not Stocks
    "Altcoins should really be called tech startups. The only similarity with Bitcoin is they both use blockchain technology. That's where it ends."
    USE IN: Step 3 Altcoins, all content, onboarding calls · WHY: Immediately shifts the mental model. Members stop applying stock market logic to altcoins.
  • 2
    The DCA Certainty
    "Dollar cost averaging — you catch some up there, you catch some down there, you take the emotion out of it. I've never regretted DCA."
    USE IN: Step 2 Bitcoin, Step 5 Buy & Sell · WHY: Removes timing anxiety. Members who hear this start executing instead of waiting.
  • 3
    The Best Trade Was a Loss
    "My best investment decision wasn't Solana at 10x. It was getting out of Polygon before I lost 70–80%."
    USE IN: Step 8 Sell Strategy · WHY: Counterintuitive, memorable. Reframes selling at a loss as a skill, not a failure. Directly addresses the Round-Tripper archetype.
  • 4
    The Price You Deserve
    "Everyone gets Bitcoin at the price they deserve. That's the cost of entry."
    USE IN: Step 2 Bitcoin, onboarding, bear market messaging · WHY: Removes regret about not buying earlier. Empowers action now.
  • 5
    Paranoia = Zeros
    "The level of paranoia should be equal to the number of zeros you're trying to protect."
    USE IN: Step 6 Send & Store · WHY: Scales security advice proportionally. A $500 portfolio doesn't need the same setup as a $500K portfolio.
  • 6
    The 5-Year Gun-to-Head Test
    "If someone put a gun to my head — where's Bitcoin in 5–10 years? About $500,000. That's 6x from here. Still way better than the bank."
    USE IN: Step 2 Bitcoin, conviction building, bear market coaching · WHY: Forces long-term thinking. Makes the current price feel like an opportunity, not a risk.
  • 7
    One Cycle Is Not Enough
    "No one makes it in a single cycle. Stick it out."
    USE IN: Step 1 Foundations, market cycles content, retention messaging during drawdowns · WHY: Sets expectations. Reduces churn during drawdowns.
  • 8
    High Risk = Both Directions
    "People say 'high risk' but they only hear the upside. They hear '3x up' and ignore the '80% down' part."
    USE IN: Step 4 How We Invest, risk-to-reward pillar, portfolio allocation conversations · WHY: Forces members to internalise downside before allocating.
  • 9
    TA as Tool, Not Strategy
    "95% of people who try to be a day trader lose money. Use TA as a tool, not a strategy."
    USE IN: Step 4 How We Invest, any call where TA interest appears · WHY: Channels curiosity productively. Prevents members from falling into day trading.
  • 10
    Bitcoin: Hold. Altcoins: Trade.
    "I've never met a single person who's ever said: buying and holding Bitcoin for long term was a bad idea. Everyone who's been in the space long enough says: buying and holding an altcoin for long term was a bad idea."
    USE IN: Step 2 Bitcoin, Step 3 Altcoins, all content · WHY: The clearest articulation of the CS philosophy. Immediately differentiates Bitcoin from everything else.
