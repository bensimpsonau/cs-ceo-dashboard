Pre-Production Checklist

Everything Ben needs to know before hitting record. Wardrobe, location, key talking points to memorise, slides to prepare, and things to avoid saying on camera.

👔 What to Wear
• Dark navy or charcoal crew-neck sweater or collared shirt — no logos, no patterns
• Premium but approachable. Think "smart-casual fund manager," not "crypto bro"
• No watch that catches light / reflects on camera. No jewellery that makes noise
• Avoid white (blooms on camera) and black (disappears into dark background)
📍 Where to Record
• Clean, professional background — home office, bookshelf, or minimal studio
• Slight depth of field (background softly blurred) to keep focus on Ben
• Warm, soft lighting — key light at 45 degrees, no harsh shadows
• Quiet space — no echo, no background noise, no air conditioning hum
• If using the studio: dark background matches slide deck aesthetic (#0D1117)
• Eye-level camera. Not looking up, not looking down. Straight into lens
• Slight lean forward — conveys engagement and authority
• Frame: chest up, slight headroom, centred or slightly off-centre (rule of thirds)
• Record in 4K if available — gives editing flexibility for crop/zoom

Compliance Golden Rules:

1. We are an education company. Every sentence must be framed as educational.
2. Never give personalised financial advice on camera. Use "members commonly find" and "here's what we teach."
3. All return claims must include "audited" and "past performance" context.
4. Institutional data citations must be attributed ("BlackRock's research suggests...").
5. The CTA is for a conversation, not a purchase. "Free strategy session" — educational framing only.
🎤 Teleprompter vs Off-Script Guidance
✅ USE Teleprompter For
• Section 1 (Hook) — first 30 seconds must be tight. No rambling.
• The 3 Forces section headers and data points — precision matters.
• The CTA / offer section — exact language around "I'm not selling anything" and the call description.
❌ DO NOT Use Teleprompter For
• Member stories (Section 6) — tell these conversationally from memory.

General rule: Teleprompter for data/precision. Off-script for stories/teaching/emotion. If it sounds read, re-record it.
