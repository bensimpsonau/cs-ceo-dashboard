1. Australian Business Structure Workaround

Key finding

Division 35 non-commercial loss rules apply to individuals only. Not to companies. Confirmed via ATO TR 2001/14 and TR 2003/3. A Pty Ltd running Bitcoin mining can generate real tax losses, offset other company income, and carry losses forward under Subdivision 165-A (COT or SBT).

Depreciation paths for a Pty Ltd

Method Who qualifies Year 1 deduction Notes
$20K Instant Asset Write-Off SBE, turnover <$10M, simplified depreciation 100% if <$20K per asset Extended for 2025-26. Reverts to $1K after 30 June 2026.
Small Business Pool Turnover <$10M 15% Year 1, 30% thereafter Pool <$20K at EOY = full write-off
Standard Div 40 Everyone else ~25% prime cost or ~37.5% DV Over 4 years (computing hardware effective life)

The Australian play

An Antminer S21 Pro at ~AU$3,700 sits well under $20K per asset. A company SBE can instant-write-off 10-20 of them in Year 1, provided each miner is installed and hashing before 30 June 2026. This is the AU equivalent of Moss's 100% bonus depreciation play. Smaller tax shield than the US version, but real and legitimate.

GST, interest, and BTC character

  • GST credits claimable on mining equipment, electricity, hosting, internet if company is GST-registered.
  • Mining to non-resident pools = GST-free supply. Keep credits. Most major pools are non-resident.
  • Interest deductible where borrowed funds are used to produce assessable income. Nexus is clear if BTC-backed loan funds miner purchase.
  • Collateral BTC = capital asset. Mined BTC = trading stock. Companies do NOT get the 50% CGT discount. Private binding ruling recommended on BTC-backed loan being a non-CGT event.

3. AFSL / TPB Compliance Framework

The three lanes

Lane What it is Licence needed
General education Explaining rules, strategy concepts, worked examples with fictional numbers None
Personal tax advice Applying tax law to a specific member's facts TPB registration
Personal financial advice Recommending specific products based on a client's situation AFSL

TPB s90-5 education carve-out

Per TPB(I) 39/2023, a service is only a tax agent service if a client can reasonably rely on it for their compliance. TPB explicitly exempts general tax advice in newsletters, websites, public seminars, and educational content not tailored to a specific tax situation. CS stays inside this carve-out.

CAN say vs CANNOT say

✓ CAN say

  • "Some members use a Pty Ltd structure to run mining activity"
  • "The $20K instant asset write-off applies for 2025-26"
  • "Block Earner offers BTC-backed loans from 9.50% p.a."
  • "Here is a worked example with a hypothetical profile"
  • "Consult a registered tax agent"
  • Host general Q&A with general answers

✗ CANNOT say

  • "You should set up a Pty Ltd"
  • "You can write off these 15 miners against your $400K income"
  • "You should borrow $100K against your 2 BTC"
  • "This will save you $40K in tax this year"
  • "Based on your income, here is what you should do"
  • Host 1:1 strategy calls looking at specific member facts
