Jeremy Haynes Framework Applied

% The 90/10 System

Give away 10% of your absolute best knowledge for free. Hold back 90% for Platinum. The critical rule: your free 10% must be more valuable than what competitors charge for.

10% Free
90% Paid (Platinum)
10% Free (WHAT and WHY)
  • Frameworks and mental models
  • Data, macro context, historical patterns
  • What signals to watch
  • Types of sell strategies (not specifics)
  • Why bear markets matter
  • Emotional cycle education
90% Paid (HOW)
  • Specific entry and exit levels
  • Portfolio alerts and signals
  • Exact allocation models
  • 1:1 coaching and implementation
  • Weekly live calls (8 analyst sessions)
  • Live audited portfolio access
The conversion engine: Viewers think "If this is what he gives away for free, what's inside Platinum?" The gap between WHAT (free) and HOW (paid) creates irresistible pull toward membership.

V V-A-K Modalities (Visual, Auditory, Kinesthetic)

Every video must engage all three learning modalities to maximise retention and emotional impact.

Visual

Screen share with real data sites (tabs format). Charts, on-screen text and graphics. NOT slide decks. ICP sees slides as a sales pitch.

Auditory

Varied tonality, conviction, and clear articulation. Shift between teaching mode and passion. Sound authoritative but never performative.

Kinesthetic

Emotional stories, relatable frustration, personal experiences. The 3am phone checking, watching a portfolio crash, the tension of waiting. Make them feel it.

0 Zero Research Methodology

  • Pull topics from real experience, client conversations, community questions
  • NOT from keyword research tools
  • If a Platinum member asks a question, that becomes the next video
  • Real market pain > theoretical search queries

↺ Binge Loop Strategy

  • End every video with end screen pointing to related content
  • Keep viewers on YouTube (algorithm rewards session contribution)
  • Create content clusters viewers want to binge
  • The algorithm penalises videos that end user sessions

1 Content First, CTA Last

  • 70% education-first content (no hard sell)
  • 30% soft pitch to Platinum
  • Soft mention at 50-60% mark
  • Full Bridge Script in final 60 seconds
  • Make them money first, then ask for the sale

★ Authenticity Over Polish

  • Tabs + talking head > slide decks
  • Film when inspired, not on schedule
  • iPad-level production is fine
  • Copy-paste personal brands fail
  • Viewers crave raw expertise and conviction

The 2026 YouTube algorithm heavily favours "Viewer Satisfaction" above all else. Here is how every priority aligns with the CS strategy.

#1
Viewer Satisfaction

Deliver exactly what the title promises. End with a clear, satisfying conclusion. The algorithm measures whether viewers feel their time was well spent.

Click-Through Rate

Use bold claims backed by experience. A/B test thumbnails. Update underperforming thumbnails after 72 hours. Dollar amounts and "The Honest Answer" drive clicks.

Average View Duration

Prioritise long-form content (15-25 min). Long-form builds the deep trust required for high-ticket ($15K) conversions. V-A-K modalities sustain attention.

Session Contribution

Use end screens to direct viewers to related videos, creating a "binge loop." The algorithm rewards content that keeps viewers ON YouTube, not content that ends sessions.

Language Rules

Consistent language is a brand signal. Every video, title, description, and caption must follow these rules. No exceptions.

RuleDetails
Always: "Digital assets" Never "crypto" or "cryptocurrency" in titles, thumbnails, scripts, or descriptions. Exception: when quoting someone else or referencing "the crypto industry" as a pejorative.
Bitcoin stays "Bitcoin" Bitcoin is a credible, standalone brand. Always capitalised. Never abbreviated to "BTC" in spoken content.
Protection, preservation, growth The three-word messaging framework. Use these words in titles, hooks, and CTAs.
Never: gains, returns, moon Too transactional or speculative. These words attract the wrong audience and position CS like every other channel.
Never: "get in early" / FOMO FOMO plays attract impulsive investors, not strategic wealth builders. The ICP's fear is making a catastrophic mistake, not missing out.
No em dashes Use regular dashes (-) or full stops. Em dashes look like AI-generated content.
Why this matters: Language is positioning. When we say "digital assets" instead of "crypto," we are signalling that this is a serious wealth-building conversation, not a speculative one. The ICP subconsciously registers this distinction. It is part of why Dee chose Ben over competitors.
