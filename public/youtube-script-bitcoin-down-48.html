Section 1: 48% Corrections Are Normal

Let's start with the number everyone is focused on. 48%. That feels enormous. And for anyone who bought near the highs, it is painful. I am not going to pretend otherwise.

But here is what the history actually shows.

In 2013, Bitcoin dropped over 50% mid-cycle. Not at the end of the bull market. In the middle of it. It then went on to make new highs.

In 2017, we saw two separate corrections of roughly 40% each. Both times, people were convinced the bull run was over. Both times, it was not.

In March 2020, the COVID crash took Bitcoin down 63% in a matter of days. 63%. That was worse than what we are seeing now. And what happened next? One of the strongest recoveries in its history.

In 2021, from April to July, we saw a 53% drawdown. That lasted months. It was brutal. And again, Bitcoin recovered to set new all-time highs later that year.

Section 2: What the On-Chain Data Shows

This is where it gets interesting. Because the on-chain data is telling a more nuanced story than the price chart alone.

Let me walk you through four key signals we are watching.

Signal one: Long-term holders are accumulating.

Wallets that have held Bitcoin for more than a year are not selling. In fact, they are adding to their positions through this decline. This is a pattern we have seen at every major bottom in Bitcoin's history. The people with the longest time horizons and the most conviction are buying while everyone else is panicking.

On Screen
Glassnode or similar chart showing long-term holder supply increasing during the drawdown.

Signal two: Exchange outflows are picking up.

Bitcoin is moving off exchanges and into cold storage. When investors move coins to cold storage, it typically means they are planning to hold, not sell. This reduces the available supply on exchanges. Less supply on exchanges with the same or growing demand means upward pressure on price. We are seeing that pattern develop now.

Signal three: Stablecoin supply is growing.

This one is underappreciated. The total supply of stablecoins is increasing. That represents dry powder sitting on the sidelines. Capital that is in the ecosystem, ready to deploy, but waiting. When that capital starts moving into Bitcoin and other digital assets, it creates buying pressure. Right now, it is sitting and waiting. That is not bearish. That is preparation.

On Screen
Chart showing stablecoin total supply growth (USDT + USDC) over the last 6 months.

Signal four: NUPL has not hit capitulation.

NUPL stands for Net Unrealized Profit/Loss. It is a metric that tracks whether the average holder is sitting on profits or losses. When NUPL drops into what we call the "blue zone," that historically signals full capitulation. Maximum pain. The last sellers have sold.

We are not there yet. We are getting closer, but we have not hit that extreme. Short-term holders are carrying significant unrealized losses, which means there is still potential selling pressure on bounces. People who bought in the last few months are underwater and some of them will sell on any relief rally.

The on-chain picture looks like an early accumulation phase rather than a confirmed bottom. Those are not the same thing. And if you understand that distinction, you are already thinking more clearly than 90% of the market.

If the traditional safe haven - US government bonds - is no longer providing reliable protection, that breaks the entire foundation of how portfolios have been built for decades. The Nasdaq is in correction territory, down more than 10% from its 2026 highs. Bonds are falling in parallel. That is not supposed to happen. Bonds are supposed to go up when stocks go down. That relationship has broken.

And here is where it gets really interesting for Bitcoin.

Gold, the traditional inflation hedge and safe haven, is down roughly 20% since the Iran escalation began in early March. Gold. Down 20%. In an inflationary environment with geopolitical risk. That is a notable failure of the traditional safe-haven playbook.

Bitcoin, during that same period, has held relatively steady in the $65,000 to $70,000 range. It has actually held better than gold over the last month.

If bonds no longer provide reliable protection and equities are getting repriced lower, investors need something else. Bitcoin is emerging as that something else for a growing number of allocators.
