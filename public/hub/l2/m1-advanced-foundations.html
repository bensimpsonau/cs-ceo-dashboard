Level 2 A1 — Advanced Foundations Session Step 01

Advanced Foundations

Deep macro-digital asset correlations, institutional flow analysis, advanced ecosystem metrics, and cross-cycle pattern recognition. The advanced mental model for understanding where markets are — and where they're going.

⚠️ Prerequisites — Must Complete Before This Module

  • ✅ All Level 1 modules (Steps 1–8) completed
  • ✅ B3 8-Step Strategy Process completed with 1:1 analyst sign-off
  • ✅ L1 Gate passed — member has a written strategy and understands basic market cycles

📋 Delivery Notes

Format: A1 — One-off live session (advanced content for Steps 1–4). This module covers the Foundations portion (~30 mins of the session).

Slides: 45–55 slides total for the full A1 session. Foundations section: ~12–15 slides.

Audience: Members who've completed all of Level 1. They already understand basic market cycles, blockchain ecosystems, and the investment thesis. This session goes deeper.

Key difference from L1: L1 asks "Why do digital assets exist?" L2 asks "What macro forces drive them and how do we measure ecosystem health objectively?"

TOPIC 1

Advanced Market Structure — Beyond the Basics

L1 taught members that markets move in cycles. L2 teaches them why — the macro forces, institutional flows, and on-chain data that drive price action. Members move from "where are we in the cycle?" to "what data tells me where we are?"

Sub-Topics to Cover:

Macro Correlations: Interest Rates, DXY, Global Liquidity Cycles

How interest rate decisions by the Fed/RBA directly impact risk asset pricing. The DXY (US Dollar Index) inverse correlation with Bitcoin. Global M2 money supply as a leading indicator. When liquidity expands, digital assets rise — teach members to read the macro before reading the chart.

How Institutional Flows Affect Digital Asset Markets

Bitcoin ETF inflows/outflows as a new demand signal. How corporate treasury allocations (MicroStrategy, etc.) create structural demand. The difference between retail sentiment and institutional positioning. Why institutional entry changes the cycle dynamics members learned in L1.

On-Chain Analytics: What the Data Tells Us

NUPL (Net Unrealised Profit/Loss) — measures aggregate holder profitability. MVRV (Market Value to Realised Value) — identifies overvaluation/undervaluation. STH (Short-Term Holder) cost basis — where new buyers are underwater. 200-week moving average — the long-term floor. Teach members to read these, not just know the names.

Cross-Cycle Pattern Recognition and Long-Term Structural Analysis

Overlay the 2014, 2018, 2022, and current cycle. What patterns repeat? What's structurally different this time (ETFs, institutional adoption, macro backdrop)? Teach members to think in multi-cycle terms — reinforces "No one makes it in a single cycle."

📝 Key Questions Answered:

  • "Why does Bitcoin move with stocks?"
  • "What are institutions doing?"
  • "How do I read on-chain data to know where we are in the cycle?"
  • "Is this cycle different from previous ones?"
  • "What macro factors should I be watching?"

TOPIC 2

Advanced Ecosystem Analysis — Multi-Chain Deep Dive

L1 taught members that different blockchains are different ecosystems (Bitcoin ≠ Ethereum ≠ Solana). L2 teaches them how to compare and evaluate ecosystems using quantitative metrics — not headlines or Twitter hype.

Sub-Topics to Cover:

Layer 1 vs Layer 2 Architecture in Depth

What L1s and L2s actually do at a technical level. The scalability trilemma (decentralisation, security, scalability — pick two). How L2s like Arbitrum and Optimism extend Ethereum. Why Solana chose a different architectural approach. This isn't about picking a "winner" — it's about understanding the trade-offs each chain makes.

Ecosystem Health Metrics: Developer Activity, TVL, Active Users

TVL (Total Value Locked) — how much capital is deployed in an ecosystem's DeFi protocols. Developer activity — measured by GitHub commits, active developers, new repos. Active daily/monthly users — real usage vs inflated numbers. Revenue generation — which chains actually make money from fees. How to access this data: DefiLlama, Token Terminal, Electric Capital reports.

Cross-Chain Bridges and Interoperability

Why bridges exist and the risks they carry. Major bridge hacks and what they teach us. The state of cross-chain interoperability. How members should think about multi-chain exposure in their portfolio.

How to Compare Ecosystems Using Quantitative Metrics

Build a comparison framework: TVL/market cap ratio, developer growth rate, fee revenue, user retention. Applied example: compare Ethereum, Solana, and a newer chain (e.g., Sui or Aptos) using the same metrics. Teach members the framework so they can apply it to any new chain that emerges.

📝 Key Questions Answered:

  • "Which ecosystem is winning?"
  • "How do I compare chains objectively?"
  • "What metrics actually matter for blockchain health?"
  • "Is TVL a reliable metric?"
  • "Where do I find this data?"

👥 Member Evidence

Members in L1 consistently didn't understand Bitcoin chain vs Solana vs Ethereum as separate ecosystems. By L2, they have the basic mental model — now they need the quantitative tools to evaluate which ecosystems deserve capital allocation. This feeds directly into the Portfolio Allocation work in Module 4.
