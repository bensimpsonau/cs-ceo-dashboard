TOPIC 1

Advanced Risk Frameworks — Portfolio-Level Risk Management

L1 taught risk as a concept ("high risk = both directions"). L2 applies it at the portfolio level — how positions interact, how to budget for drawdowns, when to rebalance, and how to shift allocation based on cycle positioning.

Correlation Analysis Between Positions

Why holding ETH + SOL + AVAX isn't "diversification" — they're highly correlated. How to measure correlation between digital asset positions. The difference between diversification within digital assets and diversification outside digital assets. Why Bitcoin has the lowest correlation to altcoins during bear markets (the decorrelation benefit).

Drawdown Budgeting: How Much Can You Actually Stomach?

Calculate your maximum portfolio drawdown scenario. If Bitcoin drops 50% and altcoins drop 80%, what happens to your portfolio? The psychological vs financial capacity for drawdowns — they're different. How to structure a portfolio that stays within your pain threshold. Exercise: members calculate their worst-case scenario using their actual allocation.

Rebalancing Strategies: When and How

Time-based rebalancing: quarterly review and adjustment. Threshold-based rebalancing: when any position drifts >10% from target allocation. Tax implications of rebalancing (triggers CGT events). How CS rebalances the portfolio in practice — what triggered recent moves.

Position Sizing for Concentrated Bets

When to take a larger position (conviction is high, risk-to-reward skewed). The "sleep at night" test: if this position went to zero tonight, would you be okay? Kelly Criterion simplified: sizing based on edge and probability. How CS sizes positions: Bitcoin (large), ETH/SOL (medium), new entries (small).

Cycle-Aware Allocation: When to Be Aggressive vs Defensive

How allocation should shift based on where we are in the cycle. Early cycle: higher altcoin allocation, more aggressive sizing. Late cycle: rotate to Bitcoin, increase stablecoin allocation, tighten stops. The CS framework: using on-chain metrics (from Module 1) to inform allocation shifts. Worked example: how the CS portfolio shifted through the last cycle.

TOPIC 2

Multi-Entity Structures — Advanced Portfolio Architecture

For members with significant portfolios who need to think about how their holdings are structured — not just what they hold, but where they hold it. This directly connects to the Advanced Tax module (M7).

Self-Managed Super Funds (SMSFs): Holding Digital Assets

How SMSFs can legally hold digital assets in Australia. The compliance requirements: investment strategy document, separate wallets, audit trail. Approved exchanges and custody solutions for SMSFs. Tax advantages: 15% tax rate in accumulation phase, 0% in pension phase. Key risks: compliance burden, regulatory changes, limited access before preservation age. This is education, not advice — always refer to specialist SMSF accountant.

Family Trusts and Company Structures

When a trust or company structure makes sense for digital asset holdings. Tax rate comparison: personal (up to 47%) vs company (25-30%) vs trust (distributed to beneficiaries). Asset protection benefits of trust structures. Setup costs and ongoing compliance requirements. When to consider: generally $100K+ portfolios with ongoing trading activity.

Jurisdiction-Specific Considerations

🇦🇺 Australia: CGT, SMSF, trust structures. 🇺🇸 USA: IRS reporting, wash sale rules (now applicable to digital assets from 2025). 🇳🇿 New Zealand: different tax treatment for digital assets. 🇬🇧/🇪🇺: Capital gains and income tax considerations. Members with international exposure need specialist advice in each jurisdiction.

When and Why to Use Different Structures

Decision framework: Portfolio size + trading frequency + income level = optimal structure. Under $50K personal: just use personal. $50K-$200K: consider trust if high income earner. $200K+: multi-entity structure likely beneficial. Active traders: company structure for deductibility. Long-term holders: SMSF or trust for tax efficiency.
