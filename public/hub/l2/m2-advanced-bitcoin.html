TOPIC 1

Bitcoin Mining Economics — Understanding Production Costs

This topic bridges the gap between "Bitcoin is scarce" (L1) and "Here's the economic engine that secures the network and creates a price floor." Members learn to use mining economics as an analytical tool for timing and conviction.

Sub-Topics to Cover:

Hash Rate and Difficulty Adjustments Explained

How hash rate measures network security. The difficulty adjustment mechanism (every ~2 weeks). Why hash rate at all-time highs = unprecedented security. What happens when hash rate drops (miner capitulation signal). How to read hash rate charts and what they mean for market positioning.

Miner Economics: Production Cost vs Market Price

Current estimated production cost per Bitcoin (~$40K–$50K range depending on efficiency). What happens when market price drops below production cost — miner stress, capitulation, and forced selling. Why production cost acts as a soft price floor. Energy costs, hardware depreciation, and operational margins. The relationship between miner profitability and network health.

Halving Cycle Impact on Supply and Price Dynamics

The halving mechanism: block reward cut in half every ~210,000 blocks (~4 years). Historical price action before and after halvings (2012, 2016, 2020, 2024). How the halving doubles production cost overnight. Supply shock dynamics: new supply cut by 50% while demand stays constant or grows. Why the halving is the most important recurring event in Bitcoin.

Miner Capitulation and Market Bottoms

The Puell Multiple: ratio of daily mining revenue to its 365-day moving average. Hash Ribbons indicator: when 30-day hash rate crosses below 60-day hash rate. Historical correlation between miner capitulation and market bottoms. How to use these signals alongside other on-chain metrics (from Module 1).

TOPIC 2

Advanced Bitcoin Strategies — Beyond Buy and Hold

L1 established "Bitcoin: Hold." L2 adds the technical depth for experienced holders managing larger positions. UTXO management, Lightning Network, and Bitcoin L2 developments that serious holders should understand.

Sub-Topics to Cover:

Bitcoin-Specific Accumulation Strategies for Larger Portfolios

How accumulation strategy changes at $100K+ vs $10K portfolios. Consolidating UTXOs during low-fee periods. Exchange arbitrage for large buys. OTC desks for very large purchases ($500K+) — less slippage, better execution. Timing DCA with fee market conditions.

UTXO Management for Experienced Holders

What UTXOs are: Unspent Transaction Outputs — the "coins" in your wallet. Why UTXO management matters: too many small UTXOs = high fees when spending. Consolidation strategies: combining UTXOs during low-fee periods. Privacy implications of UTXO management. Coin control features in wallets like Sparrow.

Lightning Network Basics and Use Cases

What Lightning is: a payment channel network on top of Bitcoin. How it enables instant, near-free Bitcoin transactions. Real use cases: Strike, Wallet of Satoshi, merchant payments. Why Lightning matters for Bitcoin's long-term utility. Current limitations and what's being built.

Bitcoin Layer 2 Developments Worth Tracking

Stacks (smart contracts on Bitcoin), Liquid Network (sidechain for traders/institutions), emerging L2 projects. The philosophical debate: should Bitcoin have smart contracts? Why to track these as an investor — potential new utility = potential new demand. Not a recommendation — an awareness exercise.
