🧠 Key Messaging Frameworks

Strategic rules for what to share free vs. reserve for members.

The 90/10 Rule Applied to Social

Give away 90% of the what and why. Reserve 10% — the how, the when, and the personalised.

Give Away Free (90%)Reserve for Platinum (10%)
  • Why digital assets matter (macro thesis)
  • Bitcoin as a treasury reserve asset
  • The institutional shift (BlackRock, Fidelity, etc.)
  • Why inflation erodes purchasing power
  • General portfolio allocation principles
  • The importance of an exit strategy
  • Frameworks for evaluating digital assets
  • Common mistakes investors make
  • Self-custody fundamentals
  • Specific allocation models & percentages
  • The Shift List (vetted assets)
  • Exact entry/exit triggers & timing
  • Portfolio alerts & trade announcements
  • 1-on-1 coaching sessions
  • Live weekly market briefing calls
  • WhatsApp community access
  • In-person masterminds (Miami & Sydney)
  • Personalised portfolio reviews
The "Open Kitchen" Principle

Show your work. Share your thinking process publicly. Let people see HOW you analyse — but the complete, real-time, personalised output is for members. The free content should make people think: "If the free stuff is this good, what's behind the paywall?"

Content Funnel Logic

YouTube (long-form) → Builds trust over weeks/months → Viewer thinks "this person understands my situation"
Instagram / X → Maintains top-of-mind → Reinforces frameworks daily
Event / Masterclass → Converts trust into action → "I need professional infrastructure"
Team Call → Qualifies fit → "Is this person right for Platinum?"
Platinum → Delivers the 10% that transforms outcomes

📝 ICP Language Guide

Mandatory language rules for all external communications. Our ICP is 40–59 HNW professionals ($500K–$2M+ NW).

Word Swaps — Always Follow These

❌ Never Say✅ Always SayWhy
CryptoDigital assetsPositions us as institutional, not speculative
Crypto investingDigital asset investing / allocationOur ICP allocates, they don't "invest in crypto"
Crypto portfolioDigital asset portfolio / allocationLanguage of wealth managers, not traders
Returns / gainsProtection, preservation, growthOur ICP fears loss more than they chase gains
Moon / lambo / WAGMI(Never reference)Immediately signals retail/speculative culture
To the moonLong-term wealth buildingOur members think in decades, not days
FOMO / FUDOpportunity window / market uncertaintyProfessional language for professional investors
Crypto bro(Don't reference at all)Our brand is the antidote to this — don't even name it
Signals / tipsFrameworks / systematic intelligenceWe teach thinking, not dependency
Get rich quickGenerational wealth / long-term strategy5–10+ year horizon, not overnight
Exception: Bitcoin

"Bitcoin" is always "Bitcoin" (proper noun, capital B). It's the only specific asset we name publicly. "Crypto" is acceptable ONLY when quoting a third party or in technical/legal contexts (e.g., "cryptocurrency tax obligations").

The "Ben Test" — Before Publishing, Ask:

1. Would a successful 50-year-old business owner feel respected reading this?
2. Does this sound like Bloomberg or like a Telegram group?
3. Am I teaching a framework, or am I selling hype?
4. Would I be comfortable if a potential investor member saw this?
5. Does this build trust over weeks, or try to convert in seconds?

Sentence Starters That Sound Like Ben
  • "Here's what most people get wrong about..."
  • "I've been investing in this space for nearly a decade, and..."
  • "The data is clear..."
  • "This is the framework I use to..."
  • "I created Collective Shift because..."
  • "The institutional shift is happening right now..."
  • "Your wealth deserves a strategy, not a guess."

Carousel Design Standards
  • Slide 1 (Cover): Bold headline, orange accent, white background. Must work as a standalone thumbnail.
  • Slides 2–9: One idea per slide. Large text, minimal design. CS brand colours.
  • Final Slide: CTA — "Save this 🔖" / "Follow for more" / "Link in bio"

Reel Thumbnail Standards
  • Custom thumbnail with text overlay (not auto-generated)
  • Ben visible (face = higher CTR)

  • Dark overlay (#1A1A2E at 60%) with white text
Story Highlights Covers
