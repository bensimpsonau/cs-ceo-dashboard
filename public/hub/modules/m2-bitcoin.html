STEP 02 FOUNDATIONS SESSION (B1)

Bitcoin

Build real conviction. Not blind faith — educated conviction.

Teaches members why Bitcoin is fundamentally different from every other digital asset, how to build a position with confidence, and why Bitcoin is a hold-forever asset. Every question about timing, DCA, and conviction is answered here.

₿ BITCOIN ONLY 4 TOPICS LEVEL 1 LEVEL 2
🟢 LEVEL 1 CONTENT

The Bitcoin Thesis

Why Bitcoin is fundamentally different from every other digital asset — and from every previous form of money.

  • → Scarcity: the 21 million cap and what it means
  • → Decentralisation: why no one controls Bitcoin
  • → Network effects: Metcalfe's Law applied to Bitcoin
  • → Store of value vs medium of exchange — Bitcoin as digital gold
  • → Why long-term holders consistently outperform

"Everyone gets Bitcoin at the price they deserve. That's the cost of entry."

ANSWERS: "Is it too late to buy Bitcoin?" · "Will it keep going up?"

Bitcoin Conviction Building

Zoomed-out perspective that makes the current price look like opportunity, not risk.

  • → 5-year and 10-year price outlook (zoomed-out perspective)
  • → Production cost analysis and what it means for price floors
  • → Why long-term holders consistently outperform active traders
  • → The 1 Bitcoin goal — owning a whole coin as a milestone

"If someone put a gun to my head — where's Bitcoin in 5-10 years? About $500,000. That's 6x from here. Still way better than the bank."

ANSWERS: "Where will Bitcoin be in 5 years?" · "Should I sell my Bitcoin?"

DCA Entry Strategy & CS Portfolio Entry Prices

The most asked question in every single call — answered completely. Stop waiting. Start executing.

  • → Dollar cost averaging explained: why DCA removes emotion
  • → Practical DCA amounts by portfolio size and risk profile
  • → Lump sum vs DCA: when each approach makes sense
  • → Frequency considerations: weekly vs fortnightly vs monthly
  • → CS Portfolio entry prices: what the CS portfolio actually holds and at what cost basis — real worked examples to build conviction

"Dollar cost averaging — you catch some up there, you catch some down there, you take the emotion out of it. I've never regretted DCA."

ANSWERS: "Should I buy now or wait?" · "How much per week?" · "Do I start now or wait for a dip?"

EVIDENCE: Asked in virtually every single call. Nigel: "Do I start buying a thousand dollars of Bitcoin each week, or do I wait another month?"

Bitcoin: Hold. Altcoins: Trade.

The single most important philosophy in the CS curriculum — clearly explained and backed by data.

  • → Why Bitcoin is a hold-forever asset (data on long-term holders)
  • → Why this rule does NOT apply to altcoins
  • → CS's actual Bitcoin sell history (very rare, very different to altcoins)

"I've never met a single person who's ever said: buying and holding Bitcoin for long term was a bad idea."

ANSWERS: "Do you ever sell Bitcoin?" · "Should I take profits on Bitcoin?"

  • → The relationship between miner capitulation and market bottoms

ANSWERS: "What's the cost to mine a Bitcoin?" · "What happens after the halving?"

Advanced Bitcoin Strategies

ANSWERS: "Is there anything more I can do with my Bitcoin?" · "What about Lightning?"
