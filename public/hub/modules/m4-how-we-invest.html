STEP 04 FOUNDATIONS SESSION (B1) INVESTMENT PRINCIPLES

How We Invest

The CS investment philosophy. Risk-to-reward, portfolio allocation, and avoiding the common traps.

Establishes the Collective Shift investment philosophy and Investment Principles. Teaches members how to think about risk properly, build their own investment thesis, and avoid the most common behavioural mistakes. This is where mindset meets mechanics.

4 TOPICS L1 2 TOPICS L2 PORTFOLIO CALCULATOR
🟢 LEVEL 1 CONTENT

Risk-to-Reward Framework

The framework that changes everything. Risk is a two-sided concept — members must internalise both directions before allocating.

  • → Understanding risk means BOTH upside AND downside
  • → How to evaluate upside vs downside before entering a position
  • → Why "high risk" doesn't just mean "high reward"
  • → Position sizing based on risk profile
  • → When risk-to-reward is skewed in your favour (the signal to act)

"People say 'high risk' but they only hear the upside. They hear '3x up' and ignore the '80% down' part."

ANSWERS: "How risky is this?" · "What's the worst that can happen?" · "How much could I lose?"

Investment Principles

The CS philosophy — what we believe, why we believe it, and how it shapes every investment decision.

  • → The core CS philosophy: self-sovereignty, your own strategy, education over advice
  • → Why we don't give signals — we give frameworks
  • → Building your own investment thesis (not following someone else's)
  • → Patience as a strategy: what "wait" means with clear triggers
  • → The CS portfolio as a reference point, not a prescription

ANSWERS: "What should I buy?" · "When do I act vs wait?" · "Why won't you just tell me what to do?"

Portfolio Allocation by Investor Type

Three types of investor, three different allocation frameworks. Nigel (wealth protection) and Ryan (wealth generation) need completely different portfolios.

  • → Three investor types: Wealth Generation, Wealth Protection, Income Creation
  • → Suggested allocation frameworks for each type
  • → How allocation shifts across market cycles (defensive vs aggressive phases)
  • → Using the Portfolio Allocation Calculator tool

ANSWERS: "How should I split my portfolio?" · "Am I too heavy in altcoins?"

EVIDENCE: Nigel ($200K, wealth protection) vs Ryan ($300K, wealth generation) need completely different allocations

TA as a Tool, Not a Strategy

Managing the natural impulse to trade. Technical analysis has a place — but it's not what you think.

  • → Why 95% of day traders lose money (the data)
  • → How to use TA for entry/exit timing — not as a standalone strategy
  • → Reading basic charts: support/resistance, trend lines
  • → When TA is useful vs when it's noise

"95% of people who try to be a day trader lose money. Use TA as a tool, not a strategy."

ANSWERS: "Should I learn technical analysis?" · "When do I buy the dip?"

EVIDENCE: Harry specifically asked about TradingView and charting

  • → Rebalancing strategies: when and how to rebalance
  • → Position sizing for concentrated bets

ANSWERS: "How do I manage risk across my whole portfolio?" · "When should I go aggressive?"

Multi-Entity Structures
