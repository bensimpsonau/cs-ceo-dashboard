2.1

Your Money Is Losing Value — "The Melting Ice Cube"

Here's the simple version: the money sitting in your bank account is worth less every year. That's called inflation. It's slow enough that you don't notice it day to day — but over 10 or 20 years, it adds up enormously.

Talking Points:

  • • What is inflation? Simply put — prices go up, so the same amount of money buys less over time. Your coffee costs more. Your groceries cost more. Your rent costs more.
  • • Why is it happening? Governments around the world printed an enormous amount of new money, especially since 2020. When there's more money chasing the same stuff, prices rise.
  • • Over 80% of all US dollars in existence were created since 2020. That's a staggering number.
  • • "If your savings are sitting in cash earning 3-4% interest, but inflation is running at 5-6%… you're going backwards."
  • • This isn't about politics. It's just maths. And it's the reason millions of people around the world are looking for alternatives.
  • • "This is why digital assets matter. It's not about getting rich quick. It's about protecting what you've already earned."

SECTION 4 Foundations — Understanding the Basics

~20 minutes · What digital assets are, how market cycles work, and why there are different blockchains

4.1

What Are Digital Assets? — Starting from the Very Beginning

Let's start with the basics. Digital assets (sometimes called "crypto") are a new type of money and technology that lives on the internet. Bitcoin is the most well-known, but there are thousands of others. Here's what you need to know.

Talking Points:

  • • Digital assets are things like Bitcoin and Ethereum — digital money that lives on a network called a blockchain
  • • What's a blockchain? Think of it like a shared spreadsheet that everyone can see, but nobody can cheat on. Every transaction is recorded permanently.
  • • Bitcoin is the original and most important. It's like digital gold — scarce, secure, and not controlled by any government.
  • • Why "digital assets" not "crypto"? — "Crypto" carries a lot of baggage. We use "digital assets" because that's what they are — real assets with real value.
  • • You don't need to understand all the technology to get started. You just need to understand why it matters and how to get involved safely.
  • • "We're going to break this down step by step. No jargon. No assumptions."

"You don't need to understand how the engine works to drive the car. But you do need to understand the road rules. That's what we're teaching you."

4.2

Market Cycles — "The Market Goes Up and Down. That's Normal."

One of the most important things to understand about digital assets: the market moves in cycles. It goes up (sometimes a lot), and it comes down (sometimes a lot). This has happened every time — and every time, it recovered and went higher.

The Four Phases (Simplified):

🌱
1. Accumulation

After a crash. Prices are low. Sentiment is negative. This is actually the best time to buy — but it doesn't feel like it.

📈
2. Growth

Prices start rising. More people notice. Confidence grows. The news starts talking about Bitcoin again.

🎉
3. Euphoria

Everyone's excited. Prices hit new highs. Your taxi driver mentions Bitcoin. This is when most beginners buy — and it's usually near the top.

📉
4. Correction

Prices drop — sometimes 50-80%. People panic and sell. But this is where the cycle resets and the next opportunity begins.

Talking Points:

  • • This pattern has repeated in 2014, 2018, 2022 — and every single time, Bitcoin recovered and made new highs
  • • "Drops of 40-50% are completely normal in this market. If you understand that going in, you won't panic when it happens."
  • • The people who do best are those who buy during the quiet, boring times — not during the hype
  • • Bitcoin halving events (roughly every 4 years) have historically kicked off new growth cycles
  • • "This is a multi-cycle journey. No one builds real wealth in a single cycle."

"No one makes it in a single cycle. Stick it out. The people who understand this are the ones who build real wealth."

— Ben Simpson

4.3

Different Blockchains — "They're Not All the Same Thing"

Here's something that confuses almost everyone at the start: Bitcoin, Ethereum, and Solana are not the same thing. They're completely different systems — like different countries with different currencies. Understanding this is essential before you invest.

The Simple Breakdown:

🟠
Bitcoin

The original. Think of it as digital gold. Its main purpose is to store value safely. Fixed supply of 21 million. The most secure and decentralised blockchain.

🔷
Ethereum

Think of it as a global computer. It lets people build apps and services on top of it. Most digital asset projects are built on Ethereum.

🟣
Solana (and Others)

Newer blockchains built to be faster and cheaper. Like tech startups competing with each other. Some will succeed, some won't — and that's important to understand.

Talking Points:

  • • Key mental model: Think of each blockchain as a separate ecosystem — like Apple vs Android. Different technology, different strengths, different communities.
  • • Bitcoin is in a category of its own. It's the store of value. Everything else is different.
  • • "When someone says 'I bought crypto' — that's like saying 'I bought shares.' Which one? It matters."
  • • You don't need to understand all of them right now. Start with Bitcoin. We'll cover the others in Step 3 (Altcoins).

📝 Key Questions Answered:

  • "What's the difference between Bitcoin and Ethereum?"
  • "Why are there so many different ones?"
  • "Which one should I buy first?"
  • "Are they all as safe as Bitcoin?"

👥 Member Evidence
