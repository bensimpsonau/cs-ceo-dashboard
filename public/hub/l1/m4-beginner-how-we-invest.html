STEP 04 B1 — FOUNDATIONS SESSION WEEK 1

How We Invest

Establishes the CS investment philosophy and investment principles. Teaches members how to think about risk properly, build their own investment thesis, and avoid the most common behavioural mistakes. Investment Principles are the foundation of this module.

Delivery & Timing

📅 Week 1 ⏱ ~30 mins (of 2-hour session) 🎤 Live 2-hour Foundations Session 📹 Recorded session + PDF 🛠 Portfolio Allocation Calculator

Live delivery: Final section of the 2-hour B1 Foundations Session (~30 mins).

Self-paced: Recorded session + PDF + interactive Portfolio Allocation Calculator tool.

RISK FRAMEWORK HIGH IMPACT

Risk-to-Reward Framework — The Framework That Changes Everything

The single most important framework for investment decision-making — understanding that risk means BOTH directions.

  • Understanding risk means BOTH directions — upside AND downside
  • How to evaluate upside vs downside before entering a position
  • Why "high risk" doesn't just mean "high reward"
  • Position sizing based on risk profile
  • When risk-to-reward is skewed in your favour — when to act

"People say 'high risk' but they only hear the upside. They hear '3x up' and ignore the '80% down' part."

— Teaching Framework #8: High Risk = Both Directions

Key Questions Answered

  • "How risky is this?"
  • "What's the worst that can happen?"
  • "How much could I lose?"
👥 Member Evidence

Members consistently underestimate downside — Simon addresses this in every portfolio call. The risk-to-reward framework is the corrective lens that prevents catastrophic allocation mistakes.

CORE PHILOSOPHY

Investment Principles — How CS Thinks About Investing

The intellectual foundation of the entire OS — self-sovereignty, frameworks over signals, and building your own thesis.

  • The core CS philosophy: self-sovereignty, your own strategy, education over advice
  • Why we don't give signals — we give frameworks
  • Building your own investment thesis (not following someone else's)
  • Patience as a strategy: what "wait" actually means with clear triggers
  • The CS portfolio as a reference point, not a prescription — full transparency with entry prices (BTC $86,564, ETH $2,977, SOL $125.79, HYPE $23.90, SYRUP $0.325, syrupUSDC 33.4%)

Key Questions Answered

  • "What should I buy?"
  • "When do I act vs wait?"
INVESTOR TYPES

Portfolio Allocation by Investor Type — Tailored to Your Goals

Three distinct investor types with tailored allocation frameworks — because one size does NOT fit all.

  • Three investor types: 💰 Wealth Generation, 🛡️ Wealth Protection, 📈 Income Creation
  • Suggested allocation frameworks for each type
  • How allocation shifts across market cycles — defensive vs aggressive phases
  • The Portfolio Allocation Calculator tool — interactive, self-serve

Key Questions Answered

  • "How should I split my portfolio?"
  • "Am I too heavy in altcoins?"
👥 Member Evidence

Nigel ($200K, wealth protection) vs Ryan ($300K, wealth generation) need completely different allocations. This is why investor type segmentation matters — the same advice doesn't apply to both.

SIGNAL FILTERING

Signals vs Noise — What to Listen To, What to Ignore

Building the skill to separate real signals from the overwhelming noise of the digital asset space.

  • On-chain metrics worth tracking: NUPL, MVRV, STH cost basis, 200-week moving average
  • What is noise: Twitter, YouTube influencers, "experts" calling tops and bottoms
  • How CS filters signal from noise in its research process
  • Building your personal conviction scale (1-10) — what evidence would change your mind?

Key Questions Answered

  • "How do I know what to trust?"
  • "Where should I get my information?"
TECHNICAL ANALYSIS

TA as a Tool, Not a Strategy — Managing Trading Impulses

Channelling TA curiosity productively — use it for timing, never as a standalone strategy.

  • Why 95% of day traders lose money — the data
  • How to use TA for entry/exit timing (not as a standalone strategy)
  • Reading basic charts: support/resistance, trend lines
  • When TA is useful vs when it's noise

"95% of people who try to be a day trader lose money. Use TA as a tool, not a strategy."

— Teaching Framework #9: TA as Tool, Not Strategy

Key Questions Answered

  • "Should I learn technical analysis?"
  • "When do I buy the dip?"
👥 Member Evidence

Harry asked about TradingView and charting specifically. Members are curious about TA — the key is channelling that curiosity into a complementary tool rather than letting it become a day-trading habit.

Use in: Risk-to-reward pillar, portfolio allocation conversations.

Session format: Final section of the 2-hour B1 Foundations Session. Approximately 30 minutes. This wraps up the Foundations day.

Audience: Members who now understand foundations, Bitcoin, and altcoins. This section ties it all together with an investment framework.

Tone: Practical and grounding. After 90 minutes of theory, this is where members learn HOW to act on everything they've heard.

Key pivot point: The investor type segmentation is where members self-identify. Make sure they leave knowing which type they are — it drives everything from allocation to sell strategy.

Investment Principles note: Investment Principles content lives INSIDE this module — it is NOT a separate step. This is the intellectual foundation of Step 4.

CS Portfolio transparency: Share entry prices openly. This builds trust and sets the "reference point, not a prescription" tone.

CEX only rule: 🚫 No yield, no staking, no DeFi, no borrowing content in Level 1.

Language: Use "digital assets" not "crypto." Bitcoin stays as "Bitcoin." Community is on Circle.
