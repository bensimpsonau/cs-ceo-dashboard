⚠ CFO Note — Travis Bergs

ROAS is revenue ÷ ad spend. It is not profit margin. At 3× ROAS on $82K spend, paid revenue = $246K. After deducting ad spend ($82K), OPEX ($308K), and Alchemy costs, net P&L is near zero. The 5× ROAS figure referenced in earlier versions is aspirational and is not used in this document.

Retainer Risk

Alchemy's actual retainer ask is $10–12K USD/month (~$16,000–$19,100 AUD). This is a fixed cost regardless of whether ads perform. At the top end of the retainer range ($12K USD), Option C (retainer + commission) is more expensive than the current 25% gross margin deal at 2× ROAS. The retainer must be negotiated down.

Key Takeaway

At 2× ROAS, every option is loss-making. This is expected in months 1–3 while the funnel (VSL, Hammer Them, confirmation page) is being built. Renewal revenue ($165K) keeps CS alive during this phase.

At 3× ROAS, only Option B (pure retainer) and Option C (negotiated to $5K USD) approach breakeven. The current deal and Options A, D, and C at Alchemy's ask all remain negative at 3× ROAS on $82K spend.

The renewal revenue is the buffer. Without $165K/month in renewals, every option is deep in the red at both ROAS levels.
Section 04

Negotiating Position

Recommended Position: Option C — But Only at $5K USD Retainer

Option C with the retainer negotiated from $10–12K USD down to $5K USD is the only structure that: (a) aligns Alchemy's incentive to perform, (b) approaches breakeven at 3× ROAS, and (c) is cheaper than the current 25% deal at all ROAS levels. The $10–12K USD retainer Alchemy is asking makes Option C worse than the current deal at 2× ROAS.
