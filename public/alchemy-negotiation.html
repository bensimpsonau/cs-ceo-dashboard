Current ROAS is 1.84x. At 25% of net profit, CS loses money on every paid member acquired. But the problem is worse than paid alone: the 25% fee applies to ALL sales — organic included. On organic, the 25% fee costs CS ~$13K/month more than necessary with zero ROAS justification.

At ~$500K blended revenue, organic (~$175K) contributes ~$135K net profit basis with $0 ad spend. The current 25% fee applies equally to organic and paid — making the renegotiation even more important.

05

Negotiation Talking Points

Ben's framing: This is a transparent conversation, not a fight. Show the numbers. Explain the problem. Ask them to help solve it. People who help design the deal are more committed to making it work.
1
"The current structure means we lose money on every paid member at 1.84x ROAS."
Show the unit economics table. At $20K per member, 1.84x ROAS, 25% fee - CS makes -$1,952 per member. That's not a rounding error. We need 2.5x ROAS just to be at +1% margin. We're not there yet, and this structure makes it impossible to invest in getting there.
2
"The 90-day term is ending. Now is the natural moment to review the deal together."
Not a crisis. Not a threat. The contract was always designed to be reviewed at 90 days. We've got data now that we didn't have in January. Let's use it to set the structure for the next phase.
3
"We want Kishan and Sanjay to be long-term partners, not contractors. Equity does that."
Josh is out. Kishan and Sanjay are stepping up. We want to reward that with real skin in the game. Option 4 offers 0.5-1% equity vesting over 2 years. If CS raises at $10M, that's $50-100K. At an exit, significantly more. That's the trade - below-market retainer in exchange for real upside.
4
"Option 2 means you can still earn 25% - you just have to earn it with performance."
The tiered model isn't a cut. It's a performance structure. Below 2x ROAS, we're both losing money - 10% reflects shared risk. At 5x+ ROAS, you get the full 25%. If they believe they can improve ROAS, this structure rewards them for doing it.
5
"Here's what we're proposing. What would you change?"
After presenting the data and options, open it up. "Here are four structures that work for us at current ROAS. What does a deal look like from your side?" Let them propose. You'll learn what they value most - guaranteed income (retainer), upside (tiered), or ownership (equity).
6
"If we can't find something that works, we pause paid and go organic-only."
This is the BATNA - not a threat, just reality. Organic is already delivering quality leads at near-zero cost. If paid acquisition is structurally unprofitable under any deal we can reach, CS will redirect to organic. No agency needed. They need to understand the "no deal" scenario exists and is real.

BATNA - Best Alternative if They Decline

This is not a bluff. CS has a viable path without paid acquisition. The business ran before paid ads. It can again - and the organic channel is already performing.
Plan B: Pause Paid, Go Organic-First
  • Pause all paid acquisition. Stop the bleed. At 1.84x ROAS and 25% fee, every dollar spent on ads loses money.
  • Go organic-first. Ben's YouTube and content is generating quality leads at near-zero cost. No agency needed for this channel.
  • Hire a media buyer direct when ready. $5-8K/month salary vs 25% of net profit. When ROAS data improves, restart paid with a direct hire.
  • Protect renewals. 65% margin, largest contribution to overhead. The business survives on renewals in the short term while organic builds.

Bottom Line

The current 25% deal loses money at 1.84x ROAS. CS needs to reach ~$700K/month in US sales just to break even - that's not happening today. The fee structure needs to change before the 90-day term rolls to month-to-month on April 30.

Walk in with Option 4. Hybrid retainer ($8K) + tiered performance fee (10%/15%) + equity (0.5-1%). This is the long-term play. If they push back on equity, move to Option 2 (Tiered by ROAS) - same upside potential for them, clean and performance-aligned. Floor offer is Option 1 (flat 15%).

If no deal is possible, CS pauses paid and goes organic-only. The business survives. Alchemy earns zero. A deal that works for both parties is better than no deal.
