Negotiation Talking Points

Travis's recommendation: Be transparent. Show the numbers. Let them propose. Honesty builds trust and gets to a deal faster than posturing.
1
"The current structure means we literally cannot afford to run paid ads."
Show the math. At 1.84x ROAS and 25% take, CS loses money on every paid sale. February: A$280K spend, A$250K return. We took a six-figure personal loan to keep the lights on. This is not a negotiating tactic. This is survival arithmetic.
2
"We want to expand your scope to ALL channels. That is a bigger pie."
Currently they only touch US VSL. We are proposing they manage organic + paid, AU + US. Sanjay replaces Josh on all marketing. Kishan manages all sales. A lower percentage of a much larger revenue pool = more total dollars for them.
3
"We are raising A$3M. Once funded, ad spend goes up significantly."
The raise gives CS runway to invest heavily in paid acquisition. If they are in this deal at a sustainable rate when that happens, their total payout skyrockets. Short-term rate reduction = long-term volume increase. They want to be in this deal when the capital arrives.
4
"Equity alignment means you win when we win at exit."
1-2% equity vesting over 12 months with performance milestones. This makes them partners, not just contractors. If CS exits at $20M+, that equity is worth $200-400K+. That is better than any commission cheque.
5
"The alternative is we pause paid entirely and go organic-only."
This is the BATNA. Not a threat. Just reality. If the economics do not work, CS will redirect all spend to organic content which is already generating high-quality leads at near-zero cost. No agency needed for organic. They need to understand that the current deal is unsustainable and "no deal" is a real option.
6
"Here are the numbers. What would you propose?"
Travis's advice: be transparent and let them participate in solving the problem. Show them the margin analysis. Show them the breakeven math. Ask them what structure they think is fair. People who help design the deal are more committed to making it work.

BATNA - Best Alternative if They Decline

This is not a bluff. If Alchemy declines a restructure, CS has a viable alternative. The business survived before paid ads. It can again.
Plan B: Pause Paid, Go Organic-Only
  • Pause all paid acquisition immediately. Eliminate the A$280K/month ad spend liability.
  • Go organic-only. Ben's YouTube + content is already generating high-quality leads at near-zero cost.
  • Hire a media buyer. A$5,000 - A$8,000/month salary instead of 25% of profit. When ready to restart paid.
  • Focus on renewals. 65% margin, 44% overhead contribution. The business survives on renewals alone in the short term.
Evidence That Organic Works Without an Agency

Dee's Customer Journey

YouTube search. Found Ben. Watched content. Evaluated competitors (Invest Diva, Decentralized Masters). Chose Collective Shift. Signed up for Platinum at A$20,000. Zero agency involvement. Zero ad spend. Pure organic conversion driven by Ben's authenticity.

Wealth Shift Podcast

One episode. Posted 3 weeks late. 20,000 views. Direct sign-ups. No promotion budget. No agency distribution. Just good content finding its audience organically. This is proof of concept that the brand has organic pull.

Bottom Line

The current 25% deal nearly killed the business in February. At 1.84x ROAS, CS loses money on every paid sale after the agency cut. Ben took a personal loan to keep the company alive.

The proposed restructure (Option 4) is not a downgrade for Alchemy. It is a bigger scope (all channels vs US-only), equity upside (1-2% of a company raising A$3M), and the ability to earn 25% again if they deliver 5x+ ROAS. Their total payout at scale will be higher than today.

If they decline, CS pauses paid and goes organic-only. The business survives. Alchemy earns zero. A deal that works for both parties is better than no deal at all.
