Section 02

Pixel Conditioning and Price Anchoring

This is the single highest-leverage change in the entire sales system. When you state the price before the call, everything changes.

What Pixel Conditioning Means

State $14,999 USD ($20,000 AUD) explicitly in:

  • The VSL (Ben says the number on camera)
  • The landing page (visible on the page)
  • Pre-call email and SMS communications
  • The confirmation page after booking

What happens: Unqualified people see $14,999 USD and bounce. They tell Meta's algorithm "I am not the right person." Qualified HNW investors see $14,999 USD and book. They tell Meta "find more people like me."

The Conditioning Window

Weeks 1-4 after implementing price transparency: Fewer calls. This is normal. Push through.

Weeks 4-6: Call quality transforms. Prospects arrive pre-sold, price-aware, and ready to buy.

Weeks 6+: Salespeople become "cashiers" processing orders rather than convincing sceptics. Close rates go from 20% to 40%+. Show rates from 50% to 70%+.

Why This Works for HNW Investors

HNW investors (35-60yo, $500K-$2M+ net worth) expect price transparency. Hiding the price signals you are a scam. Being upfront about $14,999 USD ($20,000 AUD) signals confidence and professionalism. It matches rich-person messaging: understated authority, no hype, direct.

Jeremy Haynes framework: "Rich-person messaging" means you speak TO them. Conservative claims, understated authority, no hype. "Digital assets" not "crypto." This is already the CS brand voice. Pixel conditioning reinforces it.

How Price Anchoring Changes the Closer Call

When a prospect has already seen $14,999 USD ($20,000 AUD) before they get on the call:

  • They have already self-qualified on price (they can afford it)
  • The "sticker shock" objection is eliminated
  • The closer can focus on fit, not justification
  • The conversation shifts from "is it worth it?" to "is this right for me?"
  • Close rate increases because only price-qualified leads get through

Critical Rule

Never use value stacks. Never. "This is worth $50K but you get it for $15K" is the fastest way to lose credibility with HNW buyers. They see right through it. Just state the price. State what they get. Let them decide.

Closer Script: Platinum at $14,999 USD ($20,000 AUD)

The closer's job is to close. By the time a prospect reaches the closer, they have: (1) consumed Hammer Them content, (2) engaged with a lead magnet or VSL, (3) been qualified by the setter, (4) seen the $14,999 USD price, and (5) received the confirmation page content. They are warm, price-aware, and qualified.

Closer Call Framework (30-45 minutes)

This follows the Jeremy Haynes affluent-buyer VSL structure adapted for a live call. HNW investors are time-poor and decisive. Be direct, authoritative, certain. No filler.

Phase 1: Rapport and Agenda (3 minutes)

Closer
"[Name], thanks for making the time. I have reviewed the notes from your conversation with [Setter Name]. Before we dive in, let me tell you exactly how this call works.
I am going to ask you a few questions about your current situation. Then I will walk you through what we do at Collective Shift and specifically how Platinum works. If it is a fit, I will explain exactly how to get started. If it is not, I will tell you. Fair?"
Sets the frame: this is a two-way diagnostic, not a pitch. HNW buyers respect directness.

Phase 2: Discovery (8-10 minutes)

Closer
"Tell me about your current portfolio. What does the big picture look like right now?"
Let them talk. Listen for: property-heavy, super-dependent, concentrated ASX, existing digital assets without framework.
"And when it comes to digital assets specifically, what is your current position?"
Listen for: bought Bitcoin/crypto before, lost money, have some but no strategy, never bought but want to.
"What prompted you to take the call today? What is the main thing you are trying to solve?"
This reveals their primary buying motive. Match to one of the three: (1) Framework/strategy, (2) Tax optimisation, (3) Exit planning/risk management.
"And if you had that solved, if you had a complete digital asset framework that you trusted, what would that change for you?"
This is the "future state" question. Get them to articulate the value in their own words.

Phase 3: Bridge (5 minutes)

Closer
"Based on what you have told me, here is what I am seeing. [Reflect their situation back to them]. The gap is [specific gap: no framework / no exit plan / tax exposure / concentrated in property with no digital asset allocation]."
"This is exactly what Platinum is built for. Let me walk you through what it looks like."
The bridge connects their pain to the solution. Be specific to THEIR situation.

Phase 4: The Offer (5-7 minutes)

Closer
"Platinum is our core membership. $14,999 USD per year ($20,000 AUD). Here is what you get:
Shift OS: Our complete digital asset operating system. Position sizing, asset selection, entry rules, exit planning, and review cadence. This is the framework you have been missing.
Weekly Platinum Calls: Live with Ben and the research team. Market analysis, portfolio reviews, Q&A. Every week.
Research Library: Every piece of analysis we produce. Asset reports, market updates, on-chain analysis, tax strategy guides.
Community: 300+ serious investors sharing data, insights, and holding each other accountable. No noise. No memecoins. Just people like you doing this properly.
Exit Planning Framework: The exact system for knowing when to take profits and when to hold. This is what most people get wrong, and it is what most of our members say was worth the membership alone."
State the deliverables. Do not use value stacks. Do not say "this is worth $50K." Just state what they get and let the quality speak.

Phase 5: Close (5-7 minutes)

Closer
"Based on everything you have told me, and what you are trying to achieve, Platinum is designed for exactly where you are. Does this feel like the right fit?"
Pause. Let them respond. If yes, proceed to logistics. If hesitation, address the specific concern.
"Great. The investment is $14,999 USD ($20,000 AUD) for the year. We accept card or bank transfer. Which works better for you?"
Assumptive close. Give a choice between payment methods, not between buying and not buying.
"Once you are in, you will get an onboarding email within the hour. Welcome video from Ben, your Platinum portal access, and your first quick win: the portfolio review framework. You will have everything you need to get started immediately."

Objection Handling Framework

With pixel conditioning (price stated before the call), most price objections are pre-handled. The objections that remain are about timing, certainty, and fit. Here is the framework for each.

Objection Response Framework Key Principle
"I need to think about it" "I completely understand. Can I ask what specifically you want to think through? Is it the investment, whether this is the right time, or whether it is the right fit for your situation?"

Isolate the real objection. "Think about it" is never the real objection. It is always one of three: price, timing, or trust.

Once isolated: "That makes sense. Let me address that directly..." 		Never let "think about it" stand unchallenged. Isolate the real concern. Then address it directly. If they still want to think, give them a specific timeframe: "When would you like me to follow up? Tomorrow at 2pm work?"
"Too expensive" "I hear you. $14,999 USD ($20,000 AUD) is a serious investment. Can I put it in context?"

"If your portfolio is [$X], and our members typically improve their allocation strategy by even a small margin, the return on this membership pays for itself many times over. And that is before the tax optimisation, which alone saves most members thousands every year."

"The question is not whether $14,999 USD is a lot of money. It is. The question is what it costs you to not have this framework for another 12 months." 		With pixel conditioning, this objection is rare (they already knew the price). When it comes up, it is usually about perceived value, not affordability. Frame the cost against the cost of inaction. Never discount. Never negotiate on price for the standard offer.
"Not sure if it is right for me" "That is a smart thing to be cautious about. Let me ask you: what would make it right for you? What would you need to see or hear to feel confident this is the right move?"

Let them tell you what they need. Then give it to them.

"Our members are [describe ICP]. If that sounds like you, and the gap you described is real, this is exactly what it is built for. And if you get in and it is not the right fit, you will know quickly." 		This objection is about identity. They are asking "am I the type of person who does this?" Reflect their own words back. Show them they already described themselves as the ideal member. Social proof helps here: "We have dozens of members in a very similar situation to yours."
"I need to talk to my partner/spouse" "Of course. What do you think their main concern would be?"

"Would it help if I sent you a summary of what we discussed? Something you could share with them that covers the key points?"

"And would it make sense to book a follow-up for Thursday so we can address any questions they have?" 		Legitimate objection for HNW couples. Equip them to sell internally. Send a follow-up email with the key points. Book a specific follow-up time. Do not leave it open-ended.
"I want to do more research first" "Smart. What specifically do you want to research?"

"The interesting thing is, most people who say that end up spending 3-6 months doing the same research our members get in week one. The framework, the asset selection criteria, the exit planning... that IS the research. You are not paying for information. You are paying for the 9 years of work that has already been done." 		Reframe: Platinum IS the research. They are not buying information. They are buying a framework that took 9 years to build. Time spent researching alone is time spent without the framework.
"Can I start with Pro ($3K) first?" "You can. But let me be straight with you. Pro gives you access to our research library and community. It is valuable. But it does not include Shift OS, the weekly Platinum calls with Ben, or the exit planning framework. Those are the things you told me you need."

"If budget is the concern, we do have instalment options. But if it is about testing the water, I would rather you go all in on the thing that actually solves the problem." 		Do not let Pro cannibalise Platinum. If they have the budget and the need, Platinum is the right answer. Pro is a gateway for people who genuinely cannot afford Platinum yet.

The Meta-Rule on Objections

If you have done the discovery properly, most objections will not come up. The closer who gets the most objections is the closer who did the worst discovery. When you understand their situation, their pain, and their buying motive, the offer presents itself as the obvious solution.

No-Show Recovery Sequence

Every no-show is a potential $20,000 AUD sale that needs to be recovered. Not following up on no-shows is leaving money on the table.

5 MIN AFTER SMS: "Hey [Name], I was on the line for your call. Did something come up? Happy to reschedule. Here is my calendar link: [link]"
2 HRS AFTER Email: "Missed you on our call today. No stress. Here is a link to rebook at a time that works: [link]. If you have any questions before then, just reply here."
24 HRS AFTER DM (IG/ManyChat): "Hey [Name], we missed each other yesterday. Still interested in the portfolio review? Here is a fresh link: [link]"
72 HRS AFTER Final SMS: "Last check-in. If timing is not right, no worries. But if you still want to get your digital asset strategy sorted, I have a few spots this week: [link]"

No-Show Recovery Rules

  • First contact within 5 minutes of missed call (setter responsibility)
  • Use multiple channels: SMS, email, DM
  • Always offer a direct reschedule link (do not make them go through the booking flow again)
  • After 72 hours with no response, move to nurture sequence (monthly webinar invites, email sequence)
  • Track no-show recovery rate. Target: 30%+ of no-shows rebooked
  • Log all no-shows in Pipedrive with reason (if known) and recovery status

The Jeremy Haynes Rule

"The fastest way to more revenue is fixing the leakiest bucket. Do not optimise a funnel that is not yet working. Fix the single weakest conversion point before touching anything else."

Run the diagnostic. Find the one weakest point. Fix it. Then move to the next one. Sequential, not simultaneous.
